    Ground-and-Pound Defense (WIP)

    Ground · MixedGround-and-Pound Defense
    GroundMixedDefensiveAdvancedWIP

    Escape the open-camped top before the damage stacks up.

    StartBottom supine; top past the hips but NOT closed in, off to the side, no chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly.
    →
    The GoalBottom stands clean, reverses, or closes a leg loop on head-and-arm; top stays open-camped and lands ground-and-pound.
    →
    FinishStand clean, reverse, or closed loop → bottom · Open-camped 20s, no escape → reset · Top closes the position → reset (rule break) · Out of bounds → loss.

    Top can't pin,
    so bottom has to move.

    Three observable escapes: stand, reverse, closed loop. Closing the loop is enough, the finish is post-game.

    Key Positions

    🔓 Open campTop past the hips but NOT closed in, no chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly, no body lock. Top stays off to the side, leaving the space open as the defining constraint of the game. 🪢 Closed loopBottom's legs closed around top's head and arm, the structural geometry of triangle/armbar entries. The closed loop wins regardless of submission progress, the finish is post-game.

    What to Read

    Attune to the load shifts in the open camp, the moment top's hips drop to land a heavier shot, the hand that commits to a strike and leaves an arm or head reachable. That weight shift specifies which escape is open, the lightened side stands, the committed arm closes the loop. Read weight, not eyes.

    The Starting Position

    PlayersTwo, one bottom (defender, the protagonist), one top (open-camped).
    PositionBottom supine, hips on the mat. Top kneeling or posting past the hips, off to the side, past the line of the hips but not past the line of the knees. No chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly.
    BoundaryA marked perimeter on mats, both stay inside.
    RolesBottom escapes by stand, reverse, or closed loop. Top stays open-camped and lands ground-and-pound, top cannot win.
    Start & resetBegin from open camp; reset on any bottom escape, the 20-second cap, a rule break, or out of bounds.

    The Matchup

    🤸
    Bottom (Defender)

    Trying to stand clean, reverse to top, or close a leg loop around top's head and arm.

    Three observable terminal escapes. Stand-clean requires full separation, if top connects on a leg or hip on the way up, not over yet. Reverse means ending on top. Closed loop is the structural geometry of triangle or armbar around head-and-arm, the finish is post-game.
    VS
    🥊
    Top (Open Camp)

    Trying to stay open-camped past the hips and land significant ground-and-pound.

    No chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly, no body lock. Stay off to the side, past the hips not past the knees. Damage is the only output, top cannot win the game, the constraint isolates the open-top ground-and-pound problem rather than letting it collapse into a regular pin.

    The Rules

    🔓 Top stays OPENNo chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly, no body lock. Top camps off to the side, past the hips, leaving the space open. Closing the position is a rule break, the round resets. 🥊 Strikes scaled per levelNo strikes at L1 (pure position). Light strikes from L2. Light-to-moderate ground-and-pound at L4+. Always mimic ground-and-pound, never deliver it for real. 🎚️ GnP dial-up, by permissionAt the striking levels, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial within the per-level caps above: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. Use striking as a disincentivization tool, the cost that punishes stalling and lazy structure. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake. ⏱️ 20-second cap, top has no winIf top stays open-camped for 20 seconds without bottom escaping, the round resets, same roles. Top has no positive win, the clock is bottom's threat window, not top's score. 🦶 Three observable escapesBottom wins by standing cleanly, reversing to top, or closing a leg loop around top's head and arm. The closed loop is the structural geometry, the submission finish is not required. ⬛ Stay inside the perimeterPlay happens on mats inside a marked perimeter. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.

    How to Win

    Stand Bottom stands clean → bottom wins, switch roles.Full separation to feet, top has no connection on bottom's legs or hips. If top connects on the way up, the stand is not clean, continue.
    Reverse Bottom reverses to top → bottom wins, switch roles.Bottom gets above the opponent, on top of them or standing over them in control. Reversal beats damage, the position has flipped.
    Loop Bottom closes a leg loop on head and arm → bottom wins.Legs closed around top's head and one of top's arms, the closed structural geometry of triangle or armbar. The closed loop is the win, the finish is post-game and not required for scoring.
    Reset Top open-camps the full 20s, no escape → reset, same roles.Top stayed open and bottom failed all three escapes before the cap. The round resets from open camp for another rep. Top still does not score, the clock is the threat window.
    Reset Top closes the position → reset, rule break.If top achieves chest-to-chest, knee-on-belly, or a body lock, the open-camp constraint is broken and the round resets to the start. Top does not score from a closure, the game is not for that.
    Loss Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.

    The Levels

    1Position onlyNo strikes.Top stays open-camped without strikes. Bottom drills the three escape paths against a positional-only top. Calibrates the open-camp shape and the closed-loop geometry.
    2Light strikesDamage threat on.Top adds light strikes from the open camp. Bottom must defend strikes AND find one of the three escapes. The strike threat changes which escape is available moment to moment.
    3Side switchingTop swaps the camp.Top may switch sides past the hips, increasing the scramble demand on bottom. Reading which side is loaded becomes the task.
    4Sustained ground-and-poundLight-to-moderate.Top stays open-camped and lands sustained light-to-moderate strikes, simulating real ground-and-pound. Bottom must time the escape under continuous damage threat.
    5Full expressionLive, open hunting.Top stays open-camped and hunts damage with everything they have inside the rules. Bottom finds the escape under live pressure. Most representative of the real ground-and-pound problem.

    Recall Check

    Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.

    Q Why is the top NOT allowed to close the position?
    The constraint isolates the open-camped ground-and-pound problem. Closing to chest-to-chest, knee-on-belly, or a body lock collapses it into a regular pin, a different game (mount, side, KOB). Open camp is the variable being trained here.
    Q Why does the closed loop count without finishing the submission?
    The closed structural geometry (legs around head-and-arm) is the observable terminal event. The finish is post-game, requires control speed and a tap, which is not what's being trained here. Closing the loop proves the escape via attack.
    Q What counts as a "clean stand," and what doesn't?
    A clean stand is full separation to feet with no top connection. If top connects on a leg or hip on the way up, the stand is not clean, continue. The standard is the same as a wrestling stand-up, hands and hips off, range restored.
    Q Why does the top have no win condition?
    Bottom is the protagonist. Top's job is to BE the threat, not to score against it. The 20-second clock is bottom's threat window, not top's score, this keeps top hunting damage rather than stalling for time.

    Go Deeper

    Task focus & coaching cues

    Each role's job

    🤸
    Bottom (Defender)

    Three escape paths active at all times. Read the load, time the stand, hunt the closed loop on a committed arm, look for the reverse when top's base lightens.

    🥊
    Top (Open Camp)

    Stay off to the side past the hips. Hunt damage with hands and feet inside the rules. Do not close the position, it breaks the game. Keep moving, side-switch as openings appear.

    Coaching cues

    ⚓
    Which path opened?

    Ask bottom after each rep: "Stand, reverse, or loop?" Direct attention to which one was available, not to a memorised escape.

    🔓
    Did you stay open?

    Ask top: "Were you off to the side, or did you slip into a pin?" Keeps the open-camp constraint honest.

    Constraints-Led analysis

    Constraints → Affordances

    Top must stay open→Isolates the open-camped GnP problem, not a regular pin
    Three observable escapes→Multiple solutions, develops degeneracy
    Top has no win, 20s cap→Urgency for the bottom, no stalling for the top
    Closed loop is enough→Observable terminal proxy, no tap required for scoring
    Live, light-to-moderate strikes→Keeps the strike-threat perception intact

    Implements Constrain to Afford (Renshaw et al., 2019). The open-camp constraint creates the affordance of escape-by-attack (closed loop) and escape-by-movement (stand, reverse), which the regular pin geometry would not allow. The closed-loop-as-win is a sharp affordance, the geometry itself is the proof.

    What the bottom reads

    ✋
    Haptic

    Load through top's hands and base → which side is committed, when an arm leaves range, when the camp lightens for a stand.

    👁️
    Visual

    Top's hand committing to a strike → the arm that closes the loop. Top's base on one side → the lightened side to stand toward.

    🧭
    Proprioceptive

    Own hips and feet → whether a clean stand is set up or a connection on the leg will catch the rise.

    What we measure (order parameter)

    Whether the bottom matches the escape to the moment, the stand to the lightened side, the loop to the committed arm, the reverse to the over-extended base. Track which of the three paths is taken across reps and whether each is matched to the actual opening. When the bottom consistently picks the right escape for the moment, the skill has formed.

    Representativeness

    Models: the open-camped ground-and-pound problem in MMA, where the top fighter has space to strike but cannot or will not commit to a pin, and the bottom must escape via attack or movement before damage stacks up.

    Simplified: top stays open20s threat windowthree observable escapes

    Builds on Ground Escape, Ground to Standing, and Leg Reclaim; transfers into Full MMA Expression as the ground link of the open-space → wall → ground cycle.

    Readiness to progress

    • Reads the open-camp load and picks the matching escape
    • Stands cleanly when the lightened side appears
    • Closes the loop on a committed arm, not on a stalled one
    • Continues escaping under sustained light-to-moderate strikes (L4)

    Warning signs

    Waits for a pin to escape from (game is open-camp only) Tries to finish the submission instead of closing the loop Stands without checking top's connection Top drifts into chest-to-chest (rule break, reset)

    Safety & related games

    🤝 Light-to-moderate ground-and-pound 🛑 Stop on closed-loop control, no completed submissions 🔁 Reset on rule break (top closes the position) or 20s cap
