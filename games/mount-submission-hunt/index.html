    Mount Submission Hunt (WIP)

    Ground · GrapplingMount Submission Hunt

    Provisional (WIP): invented in class 2026-06-20, pending final coach sign-off

    Built on the mat on 2026-06-20 as the finishing layer of the mount ladder (Mount Escape → Mount Maintenance → this). It trains converting a held mount into an observable submission entry, the first dedicated submission-offense game in the library. Refined from the class version (back-take and fatigue rules clarified). Awaiting a final pass before the WIP tag comes off.

    GroundGrapplingOffensiveAdvancedSubmission Entry

    Isolate the finish from mount, one arm over the head or the back sealed, before they frame, recover a leg, and get out.

    StartAttacker fully mounted; defender sets their own arm frames first, inside the mat.
    →
    The GoalAttacker isolates one arm above the head, or seals the back on top; defender denies the isolation and recovers a leg.
    →
    FinishArm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s, or back sealed on top 1-2s → attacker · One leg recovered, reversal, or stand → defender.

    Mount is not the finish.
    It is the doorway to it.

    Don't lounge in the mount, hunt the isolation. Show the strike to open the frame, then take the arm over the head. The position you hold is only worth the finish it leads to.

    What to Read

    Attune to where the defender's frames are loaded, the felt resistance of their arms against your hips and chest. A shown strike makes them cover, and a covering arm that crosses the centerline is the arm to chase over the head. Read the committed frame, not a memorized submission. When the layout shifts, a wobble, a turn, an exposed arm, the finish is suddenly afforded, take it.

    The Starting Position

    PlayersTwo, one attacker (mounted), one defender (bottom).
    PositionFull mount. The defender sets their own arm frames before "go", they are not pre-pinned.
    BoundaryThe mat, out of bounds is a reset.
    RolesAttacker isolates the finish; defender denies the isolation and recovers a leg.
    Start & resetMount set, defender frames, then "go"; reset on a finish, a leg recovered, a reversal, or a stand. Keep the rounds short.

    The Matchup

    🥋
    Attacker (Finishing)

    Trying to isolate one arm above the defender's head with your hands locked (the arm-triangle / armbar entry), or seal the back on top if they turn.

    Ride the hips high. Use a shown strike to make them cover, then trap the arm that crosses the centerline and walk it over the head, bicep to the ear. Don't lounge and hold, mount is only worth the finish. If they turn away, take the back, but you must seal it on top.
    VS
    🛡️
    Defender (Denying & recovering)

    Trying to keep both arms framed and connected, deny the over-the-head isolation, and get one leg back in to recover guard.

    Elbows tight, never let an arm cross your own centerline. Frame the hips, shrimp, and fight one leg back between you, that is your win. A reversal or a stand is a bonus, but the first job is to deny the arm, not to escape clean every time.

    The Rules

    🎯 Arm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s → attackerOne arm isolated fully above the defender's head with your hands connected, bicep visibly to the ear, the arm-triangle / armbar entry. Held one to two seconds to clear the scramble. If you have it, you have already won, you don't need to crank the finish. 🔄 Back sealed on top, 1-2s → attackerIf the defender turns away, chest-to-back with both hooks in and you on top, held one to two seconds. Your own back on the floor is not a seal yet, the defender can still escape, so it does not count. 🛡️ Defender's first win is deny + recoverKeep both arms in and get one leg back between you, that is guard recovered and the defender wins. This is the reachable goal that keeps the round honest, not a full clean escape every time. ⬆️ Reversal or stand → defender bonusReversing to top or standing fully up is also a defender win, the bigger prize, but recovering one leg is enough. 🥊 Shown strikes onlyLegitimate threat, no damage. An arc between strikes and a semi-legitimate reaction, no clean landing. No elbows or knees without pads. The strike opens the frame, it does not concuss a partner. ⏱️ Short rounds, reset oftenThe finish-hunt is high intensity for both players. Keep the rounds short and reset on any outcome, fatigue is a dial we turn down here so the skill, not the gas tank, is the limit. ⬛ Stay on the matPlay happens inside the mat perimeter. Rolling out of bounds is a reset.

    How to Win

    Attacker Arm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s, or the back sealed on top 1-2s → attacker wins.One arm fully above the head with your hands connected (bicep to ear), or, if they turned, chest-to-back with both hooks in and you on top. Either held one to two seconds. You don't crank the finish, the entry is the win.
    Defender Both arms kept in and one leg recovered → defender wins (reversal or stand is a bonus).Deny the over-the-head and fight one leg back to guard. That reachable win is the point, it keeps both players working instead of grinding to exhaustion. A reversal or a stand is the bigger bonus.
    Loss Attacker reversed or a leg let back in; defender's arm isolated over the head → that player loses.For the attacker, losing the mount to a reversal or a recovered leg. For the defender, letting an arm get walked over the head and locked.

    The Levels

    1Positional, no strikesHunt the crossing arm.Attacker rides high and hunts one arm over the head; defender frames and keeps elbows tight. No strikes yet. Finds the trap-the-crossing-arm mechanic.
    2Add the shown strikeOpen the frame.The shown strike comes on. The threat makes the defender cover, the covering arm crosses the centerline, and that is the arm to isolate. Legitimate threat, no damage.
    3Recovery liveDeny vs isolate.The defender's recovery is now a win, one leg back in. The isolation-versus-recovery race becomes the task, and the defender finally has a reachable goal.
    4Add the backThe turn is punished.If the defender turns away to escape, the attacker may take and seal the back on top. Turning is no longer free, it costs the back.
    5Full expressionShort rounds, all wins live.Shown strikes on, the arm, the back, the recovery, the reversal, all live. Short rounds, reset often, hunt the finish without gassing.

    Recall Check

    Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.

    Q Why is holding the mount not the goal?
    Mount is only worth the finish it leads to. Lounging lets the defender rest and recover. The game rewards converting the position into an observable submission entry, not sitting in it.
    Q How does a shown strike help you isolate the arm?
    It makes the defender cover. The covering arm crosses their centerline, and a crossed arm is the one you can trap and walk over the head. The threat shifts the layout and opens the finish.
    Q Why is "took the back" not a win when your own back is on the floor?
    Because that is not a stable, dominant position, the defender can still escape. A back-take only scores as an observable seal: both hooks in, you on top, held 1-2s.
    Q As the defender, why is "deny and recover one leg" the goal instead of a full escape?
    Against a secured mount, escaping clean every time is unreachable and gasses you out. Denying the arm and getting one leg back to guard is the reachable, winnable goal, it keeps you working instead of grinding to exhaustion.

    Go Deeper

    Task focus & coaching cues

    Each role's job

    🥋
    Attacker (Finishing)

    Ride the hips high, show the strike to open the frame, trap the crossing arm and walk it over the head to the bicep-to-ear lock, or seal the back on top if they turn. Hunt, don't lounge.

    🛡️
    Defender (Denying & recovering)

    Elbows tight, never cross your own centerline, frame the hips, shrimp, fight one leg back to guard. Deny first, escape second.

    Coaching cues

    🎯
    Hunt, don't lounge

    Ask the attacker: "Did you threaten a finish, or just sit there?" Mount is the doorway, the arm over the head is the room.

    ⏱️
    Short rounds, fatigue is a dial

    Both players gas in a long mount battle. Keep the rounds short and reset often so the skill is the limit, not the gas tank.

    Constraints-Led analysis

    Constraints → Affordances

    Win = arm over the head, 1-2s→An observable submission entry, not a memorized or cranked finish
    Back-take = sealed on top, 1-2s→Rewards a real dominant seal, ends the "did he get it?" disputes
    Defender wins by deny + recover one leg→A reachable goal that breaks the gas-out stalemate
    Shown strikes open the frame→The finish is afforded by the threat, the finishing sequence in miniature
    Defender sets frames before "go"→A representative, fightable start, not a gifted isolation

    Implements the Finishing Sequence: the shown strike shifts the position's attractor layout so a new affordance, the crossing arm, opens up. The fighter attunes to the shift and takes the afforded finish, rather than computing a memorized submission. The position is trained as a doorway to the finish, not an end in itself.

    What athletes read

    ✋
    Haptic

    Load through the defender's frames against your hips and chest → which arm is committed, when the frame breaks, where the isolation is open.

    🧭
    Proprioceptive

    Own hips riding high and base wide → whether you are stable enough to attack an arm without being bridged off.

    👁️
    Visual

    A covering arm crossing the centerline, a turn away → the arm to chase over the head, or the back to seal.

    What we measure (order parameter)

    Whether the attacker converts a held mount into an observable finish-entry by opening the frame and chasing the crossing arm, rather than holding the mount and waiting. Track finish-entries versus lounging, and whether the defender denies-and-recovers versus gets isolated. When mount reliably becomes a finish entry, the submission-offense skill has formed.

    Representativeness

    Models: the real mount finishing sequence, the arm-triangle / armbar / back-take fork, the layer above simply holding the mount. The first dedicated submission-offense game, and the top of the mount ladder.

    Finish layer of the mount ladderarm over head or back sealed, 1-2sshort rounds

    The finish layer above Mount Maintenance; the offensive answer to Mount Escape; operationalizes the Finishing Sequence.

    Readiness to progress

    • Hunts the arm, never just lounges in the mount
    • Uses the shown strike to open the frame
    • Seals the back on top, never claims it from underneath
    • Resets and goes again without gassing out

    Warning signs

    Lounges in mount, never threatens a finish Calls a brushed arm a finish Claims a back-take with his own back on the floor Defender gasses chasing a full escape instead of recovering one leg

    Safety & related games

    🥊 Shown strikes only, no elbows or knees without pads 🛑 The entry is the win, no need to crank the finish, tap early 🔁 Short rounds, reset on any outcome
