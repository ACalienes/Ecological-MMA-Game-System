    Kick Defense Isolation (WIP)

    Skill Isolation · StrikingKick Defense Isolation

    Provisional (WIP): invented and run in class 2026-06-20, pending final coach sign-off

    Built on the mat on 2026-06-20 to isolate the kick-defense read, the first dedicated kick-defense game in the library. It trains checking, blocking, and catching the roundhouse, one kick at a time, then layers a jab to make the kicker tricky. Awaiting a final pass before the WIP tag comes off.

    Skill IsolationStrikingDefensiveIntermediateKick Defense

    Read the kick and kill it, check, block, or catch, before it lands, one kick at a time.

    StartOne kicker, one defender, inside a marked perimeter; roundhouses only, one at a time.
    →
    The GoalDefender checks, blocks, or catches the kick; kicker lands clean and earns the next one.
    →
    FinishA clean defense → roles switch. A clean landed kick → kicker continues. Catch and keep → gold star.

    You can't block what you read late.
    Time the kick, don't chase it.

    Attune to the rising shin and the hip that throws it, the time-to-contact, not the foot. Catch only when it's really there, reach for everything and you get faked to the head.

    What to Read

    Attune to τ (time-to-contact), the closing rate of the rising shin, and the hip and shoulder pre-rotation that says which kick is coming and where. Defend on the read, not the impact. The catch is a higher-risk, higher-reward read than the block, take it only when τ gives you the room.

    The Starting Position

    PlayersTwo, one kicker (offense), one defender.
    PositionStriking range, both on their feet, roundhouses only. Switch stance freely.
    BoundaryA marked perimeter, the wall is live, use it to corner the kicker or get walked onto it yourself.
    RolesKicker lands clean roundhouses one at a time; defender checks, blocks, or catches.
    Start & reset"Go"; roles switch the moment the defender successfully checks, blocks, or catches. A clean, undefended kick earns the kicker the next one.

    The Matchup

    🦵
    Kicker (Landing)

    Trying to land a clean roundhouse to the leg or body, one at a time, and earn the right to throw the next one.

    One kick at a time, no spamming. Use feints and stance switches to disguise the kick, and gauge your own commitment, a heavy kick that gets checked hurts you. In the jab variant, use a lead jab to open the kick. Don't leave a caught leg hanging, pull it free.
    VS
    🛡️
    Defender (Reading)

    Trying to check, block, or catch the kick on the early read, and take over the offense.

    Load your weight on the back leg, ready to check. Read τ and the hip, not the foot. Check with the shin, block with two points of contact, and catch only when the time is really there. Keep your fingers curled. In the later levels, return fire the moment you defend.

    The Rules

    🦵 Roundhouses only, one at a timeLeg or body roundhouses; head only with full control. No spamming, throw one, read the result, then throw again. The kicker continues only by landing clean. 🛡️ Check, block, or catch wins the exchangeCheck = the shin, above the ankle and below the knee. Block = two points of contact absorbing the kick. Catch = secure and keep the leg, the gold star. Any of the three switches the offense to the defender. 🔁 Land clean and continueA clean, undefended kick earns the kicker the next one. A successful defense switches the roles, the defender becomes the kicker. 👊 Jab variantThe kicker may add a lead jab as a probe. It scores no points, it only opens the kick. The defender may return fire or counter for a bonus. 🚫 No head kicks, no calf kicksThe coordination-stage nerf, it makes the catch safe to learn. The mastery level puts the head-kick threat back, with gear, so the catch carries its real risk. ✋ Curl the fingersOpen, splayed fingers get jammed and broken, and they foul. Keep a soft curl on every block and catch. A real safety habit, learned here so it holds in a fight. ⬛ Stay inside the perimeterPlay happens inside a marked perimeter. Leaving it loses the exchange.

    How to Win

    Defender Check, block, or catch the kick → win the exchange and take the offense.Any clean defense wins the exchange and hands you the offense. Catch and keep the leg for the gold star, and a returned counter on top of the defense is the full expression.
    Kicker Land a clean, undefended roundhouse → continue and earn the next kick.A clean kick to the leg or body that is not checked, blocked, or caught keeps you on offense. One at a time, read each result before the next.
    Switch / loss A defended kick hands over the offense; a caught and kept leg can be punished.Getting checked, blocked, or caught switches the roles. A leg left hanging in a catch can be swept or countered, so pull it free.

    The Levels

    1Check and blockRead τ, no jab.Roundhouses only, no jab, no head kicks. The defender reads τ and the hip, and checks or blocks. Finds the early read.
    2Add the catchThe gold star, but risky.Catching and keeping the leg becomes a win. But don't reach for everything, an over-reach against a tricky kicker gets faked. Take the catch only when τ affords it.
    3Add the jabThe kicker gets tricky.The kicker adds a lead jab to probe and open the kick. The defender has to read through the misdirection.
    4Return fireDefend and punish.A defense plus an immediate counter is the full win. Check and return, or catch and sweep, the defender stops being a wall and starts punishing.
    5Mastery: the head kick returnsCalibrate the real risk.With control and gear, head kicks are live again. Now the catch carries its real risk and the read has to be honest, this calibrates the safe habit from the earlier levels to a real fight.

    Recall Check

    Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.

    Q What are you actually reading, the foot or something earlier?
    τ, the time-to-contact of the rising shin, and the hip and shoulder that throw it. Defend on the early read, not at the impact, by the time you see the foot it is too late.
    Q Why not reach to catch every kick?
    The catch is a high-risk read. Reach for everything and a good kicker feints and goes to the head. Catch only when τ gives you the room, otherwise block safely.
    Q Why does the no-head-kick rule come off at the mastery level?
    Nerfing the head kick makes the catch artificially safe. To calibrate the catch's real risk you have to put the head-kick threat back, or the safe habit won't hold in a real fight.
    Q Did your kick defense carry over from your grappling or boxing?
    No. Attunement does not transfer across action modes, reading a kick is its own skill, learned from your own stance. That is exactly why this game isolates it instead of assuming it comes free from another base.

    Go Deeper

    Task focus & coaching cues

    Each role's job

    🦵
    Kicker (Landing)

    Land clean roundhouses one at a time, feint and switch stance to disguise them, gauge your own commitment, and pull a caught leg free. In the variant, use the jab to open the kick.

    🛡️
    Defender (Reading)

    Weight on the back leg ready to check, read τ and the hip, check or block on the early read, catch only when it's there, keep the fingers curled, return fire in the later levels.

    Coaching cues

    👁️
    Read the hip, not the foot

    The kick is specified by the hip and shoulder before the shin arrives. A defender who watches the foot is always late.

    ⚖️
    Gauge your own kick

    Ask the kicker: "How sure were you?" A heavy kick that gets checked hurts the kicker. Commit hard only when the kick is really there, the same risk read the catcher makes.

    Constraints-Led analysis

    Constraints → Affordances

    Roundhouses only, one at a time→Isolates the kick-defense read without spam noise (task simplified, loop intact)
    Win = check / block / catch→Realized defensive affordances, not a cue merely noticed
    Catch is the gold star, but risky→Rewards the higher read only when τ affords it
    No head kicks, then back at mastery→A safe coordination stage, then calibration of the real risk
    Jab variant adds option-space→Repeats the problem with more variability, not a new movement

    Implements per-mode learning (the non-transfer rule): kick-defense attunement does not carry over from a grappling or boxing base, so it gets its own isolated path. The kicker stays live and adaptive (feints, stance switches), so this is a task-simplified game with the perception-action loop intact, not a decomposed feed.

    What athletes read

    👁️
    Visual

    τ of the rising shin and the hip / shoulder pre-rotation → which kick, where, and when, the early read that beats the impact.

    ✋
    Haptic

    The kick's weight on the block, the leg in the catch → how committed the kick was, whether the catch will hold.

    🧭
    Proprioceptive

    Own stance loaded on the back leg → whether you are positioned to check, or stuck flat and late.

    What we measure (order parameter)

    Whether the defender defends on the early τ read, checking and blocking in time and catching only when afforded, versus reacting at impact and reaching for catches. Track defenses on the read versus late reactions, and faked-catch losses. When the defender reliably reads the hip and the closing shin, the kick-defense skill has formed.

    Representativeness

    Models: the live kick-defense problem in striking and MMA, the check / block / catch decision under feints. A per-mode skill that does not transfer in from grappling, so it earns its own isolated game.

    Roundhouses, one at a timecheck / block / catchτ read, calibrated risk

    Follows Close-Range Defense; the return-fire levels feed Counter-Striking.

    Readiness to progress

    • Checks and blocks on the early read, not at impact
    • Catches only when τ affords it, doesn't reach
    • Keeps a curled hand on every block and catch
    • Stays composed under feints and stance switches

    Warning signs

    Reacts at impact, always late Reaches for every catch and gets faked Splays the fingers on the block Over-commits a heavy kick that gets checked (kicker)

    Safety & related games

    🦵 Shin guards on, light control, no calf kicks ✋ Curl the fingers, slow on the catch 🔁 Switch roles on a clean defense
