Design law · restated

Modules, not infinite games

"It's not about building an infinite amount of games. It's about building modules that can be broken down or expanded at an infinite level. No duplicate games: similar games get merged, with variants."

So: when a mined game is ~80% similar to an existing page, it becomes a variant block, not a new page. This is now settled law, it matches the Curation Pass exactly.
Design law · new constraint

Ground games are GnP-first

"The emphasis has to be ground-and-pound focused. It cannot be jiu-jitsu. Practicing smashing somebody on the ground, not practicing grappling."

So: every ground game adapted from BJJ sources needs a strikes-live dimension before it goes canonical. New audit criterion for the SMGC-derived escapes.

The most valuable thing on the tape: live game design

Block 2 is a constraints-led design session captured in real time. Student reception drove every change. This v1→v3 arc is the threshold rule being discovered live, and it belongs in the game doc as provenance.

v1
Base rules, 8-minute round
Students: "it felt dissonant... a lot of crashing." Defenders missing their lead-hand probes, not enough lateral motion.
v2
Defense gains the lead-leg teep
"A little bit more defensive capability in terms of keeping somebody away." Fixes the range problem v1 exposed.
v2b
Partner switch, same rules
Pure experiment. Debrief: more feints with a fresh opponent. Alex: "I genuinely don't know why. I'm curious."
v3
Connect AND destabilize
Defensive win raised: move their feet from the spot. The threshold rule, applied live. "Still trying to figure out the best way to define it."

Alex original #3: Turtle Breakout

"I literally just made it up right now." Working name pending the LANGUAGE.md convention, [WIP] until blessed.

Ground · Wrestling · WIP

Turtle Breakout

Defense in turtle. Offense standing, freestyle/Greco posture, palms on the small of the back, arms fully extended.
Start
Defense initiates: they say "go." Asymmetric trigger, the defender owns the timing.
Offense wins
Connect hands around the waist, or around any leg. Connection is the entire win, nothing else matters.
Defense wins
Stand up, turn, face, and stay away. Separation ends it.
Redemption
As the defender escapes, offense gets one final connection attempt. Miss it and it's over.

! Open design question

Players drifted from turtle to supine instead of exploding up: turning to face from the bottom rather than collecting hands and breaking free.

"Maybe we need to constrain the rules so that they can't go supine. I'm not sure."

Flaw or affordance? Your call. The draft doc carries it as an open note either way. Student counter-discovery worth keeping: stop chasing the body lock, re-orient hand connections, pin the defender's hands to the mat first.

The win-scaling ladder: maybe a FORMAT, not a game

Block 3's mechanic: every win moves offense to a worse starting position. Self-scaling difficulty with zero coach intervention. Same shape as the Positional Battle demotion: the mechanic may outgrow the game it appeared in.

Rung 1
High single
Leg up, grip under the knee, hiked high. The starting handicap favors offense most here.
Rung 2
Body lock
Double underhooks, hands locked. Defender sets up with a whizzer.
Rung 3
Regular single
Connected hands, but the leg is down. Defender has their base back.
Rung 4
Low single
Knees on the ground. Hardest start, earned by winning everything above it.
