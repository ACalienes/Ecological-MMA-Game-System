First Half · The Striking Ladder

One game, three rungs: weapons escalate on both sides

Blocks 1-3 are the same close-the-distance game, made harder each round. The no-weapons start is the scaffold for the brand-new student. Same self-scaling shape as the Win Ladder format.

Rung 1 · B1
OffenseNo weapons at all. Block, catch, time the clinch.
DefenseLead leg only (teep / front / side kick) + hands.
Rung 2 · B2
OffenseStill no strikes, but may reach to manipulate and post.
DefenseGains the lead jab on top of the teeps.
Rung 3 · B3
OffenseGains the rear cross (head or body, straight only) to threaten.
DefenseCan now win too, by catching a leg on the level change.

In your own voice, better than anything paraphrased. Candidates for concept pages and cue backports.

Concept page candidate

Winning the Circle

Control the edge, herd the opponent to the wall, take your time, do not chase. The bull, the matador, and the soldier. Recurs all through sparring.

22:27 + all class
Concept page candidate

The 4 ways to defend

Block, parry (straights only), evade (not be there, or be too close), and encroach on his offense. A clean taxonomy, taught to the beginner.

81:55 – 83:14

Don't hold and wait

Holding a grip and waiting loses, "especially against someone stronger." Off-balance immediately. Aimed straight at the concede-to-stronger pattern.

65:36 – 66:13

Feint volume

Pump-fake 6-7 times, feint top and bottom, "feints unlock your doors." Breaks a readable forward-motion pattern and opens body shots.
