Three structural options

Pick one. The choice is irreversible-ish (each requires real rework to undo). My recommendation is starred, but it's your call.

Option A

One canonical page. Others fold in.

games/ ├── the-square (8–10 levels) │ ├── L1 block-only │ ├── L2 vary-the-tool │ ├── L3 counter │ ├── L4 opp-side 2-strike │ ├── L5 kick-finished │ ├── L6 counter-only+jab │ ├── L7 clinch off-balance │ ├── L8 kick counters │ └── L9 takedown finish └── one-shot-discovery
  • Strictest read of "core foundation that works best"
  • One game = one home in the library
  • Long page (8–10 levels)
  • Awkward to choose single-shot vs combo on one page
Option B

Two pages, distinct value props.

games/ ├── the-square (full domain progression) │ └── 5 levels: striking → ground ├── combo-exchange (NEW) │ └── 5 levels: G2 → G6 └── one-shot-discovery
  • Each page has a clear unique selling point
  • Combo is more MMA-representative than single-shot
  • Manageable Levels per page
  • one-shot-discovery deprecation feels like sunk cost
Option C

Three siblings + family page.

games/ ├── the-square ├── one-shot-discovery └── combo-exchange concepts/ └── perimeter-exchange-family (connects all 3, comparison matrix)
  • Preserves all work; no deprecations
  • Each cousin truly distinct on its knobs
  • 3 pages in one family approaches BJJ-Fanatics anti-pattern
  • Family page must do real navigation work
