Every methodology signal in our spec showed up in class

These are the principles our spec encodes. Each one came out of your mouth on the recording, almost in our exact phrasing.

🎯
Asymmetric starts

Every game opened with a clear offense / defense split, never a symmetric neutral start.

👁️
Observable wins

"Lands significantly," "closed loop," "feet out," "stand cleanly." All concrete events.

🪢
Closed loop is enough

G5 win on closing the leg loop, finish post-game. Same logic as our "arm under chin 3s."

🔁
Role switch on success

Win the exchange, you swap. Both sides develop, no one specialises in one role.

🪜
Constraint = level

G4 body-kick-only is the same game as G3, restricted weapon. A Level, not a new game.

⏳
Continuous vs terminal

"Top player cannot win." Top is the threat; the clock is bottom's threat window.

🟧
Perimeter loss

Both feet out, you lose. Same convention as our existing pages, no exception.

🎭
Feints as counter openings

A feint is a threat at L3, an invitation at L4. Defender can act before contact.

One predicted defect, confirmed

The public BJJ-games corpus almost never specifies a time/count threshold. Today's only ground game inherited the same gap, and your house rule is the fix.

Your phrasing today (G5)

"as long as possible"

"Top is just staying there as long as you possibly can, doing as much damage as you possibly can from that limitation."

Open-ended, no clock. Same gap that runs through ~12 of the 35 SOLID-labeled Discord games and almost every SMGC maintenance game.

→
Our house rule applied

20-second threat window

If top stays open-camped for 20s without bottom landing a stand-clean, reverse, or closed loop, the round resets, same roles.

Top still has no positive win, the clock is bottom's urgency, not top's score. Built into ground-and-pound-defense.md Level by Level.
