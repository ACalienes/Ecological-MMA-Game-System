Why this is system-safe

The passer wins by getting past three lines. The guard player wins by reinserting across the same three lines. Same axis, opposite directions, so both win conditions become two ends of one ruler.

Defining recovery this way is not a new idea bolted on, it is the inverse of the line-of-feet → line-of-knees → hips passing language already in the harvest. That is the argument for trusting it.

Decision 1

Graded, or flat?

Do the rungs score degrees (more recovered beats less), or are they just a menu of equally-valid wins? This is the real fork, and it changes whether the page measures quality or just pass/fail.

Option A
Graded ladder

Any qualifying rung wins, but more recovery is a cleaner win. Sets a floor, then rewards getting further. Teaches the progression itself.

Bottom wins by getting a knee or leg back between (half guard or better). Cleaner the more you recover: one knee < both knees < closed guard.
+ Trains the spectrum, richer feedback
- Needs a coach to judge "cleaner"
Option B
Flat menu

Any one insertion = guard recovered = win, full stop. No degrees. Simplest to officiate, fully binary, fastest resets.

Bottom wins by getting any leg back between the players (any guard), reversing, or standing.
+ Dead simple, unambiguous
- Throws away the progression you just named
Decision 2

Where is the floor?

Whichever way Decision 1 goes, you need a minimum bar that counts as "escaped." Set it too low and survival reads as a win; too high and a real escape gets denied.

Floor option 1
One foot back

Generous. Rewards stopping the pass even before a real guard forms. Good for beginners, but close to "survived."

Lowest bar
Floor option 2 Fits mount
One knee in (half guard)

The realistic mount-escape outcome. From mount you recover to half guard first, rarely straight to closed. Matches the actual position.

Realistic for this game
Floor option 3
Both knees / full guard

Strict. Only a fully re-established guard counts. Denies half-guard recoveries that are genuinely escapes from mount.

Highest bar
Decision 3

Page, or shared concept?

Guard recovery is a win condition in many games, not just this one. Decide once where the definition lives.

Scope option 1
Mount Escape only

Write the graded definition into this page and move on. Fast now, but the next ground game redefines guard from scratch, risking drift.

- Definition can fork across games
Scope option 2 Recommended
Shared concept page

Define "guard recovery = points reinserted across the hip / knee / foot line" once as a concept, like we did for the clinch and wall-pin definitions. Every ground game links to it and inherits it.

+ One source of truth, no drift, mirrors passing
