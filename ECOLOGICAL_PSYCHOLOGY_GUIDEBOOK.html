The One Idea

The animal and its environmentare one system.

Mutuality

You can't understand the fighter without the cage and the opponent — they're part of the system from the start, not "factors" added on. The unit of analysis is the fighter–opponent–cage system, never the fighter alone. (p.57)

Direct Perception

There's no little computer in the head turning blurry input into the world. The environment lawfully structures energy (light, sound, force), and the animal directly detects that information. Nothing is computed — only picked up. (p.56, 82)

The Core Principles

14 ideas,
and what they mean in the cage.

01
Affordances

Opportunities for action that exist in the relationship between animal and world — relative to you, right now. A puddle affords jumping when running, not when standing.

In the cageStrikeable, takedown-able, escape-able. Win conditions reward the taken opportunity, not the move.

p.73–89

02
Body-Scaling (π ratios)

Affordances scale to body size as a ratio. Warren's stairs: "climb-able" isn't an inch count — it's step-height ÷ leg-length, with a critical value of 0.88.

In the cageThis is HOW we individualize: set range/height as a ratio of reach & leg length — not absolute numbers.

p.78–81 (Warren, 1984)

03
Information & Invariants

You attune to higher-order invariants, not raw data: τ (time-to-contact, rate of expansion), optic flow, and the inertial array felt through a grip.

In the cageName the invariant per game. "Reading" = attuning to τ, not vaguely "seeing openings."

p.82, 123–127, 165

04
Perception–Action Loop

Perceiving and acting aren't separate stages — they're one continuous loop (a Möbius strip). Information is revealed only as you move.

In the cageNever decompose. Static, unopposed drilling removes the very information the skill needs.

p.192, 207

05
Synergies

The body has ~10²⁶ ways to move one arm. It solves this by soft-assembling "choirs of muscles" that preserve the goal, not a fixed set of parts.

In the cage"Points for staying standing," not "you must sprawl." Let the solution self-assemble.

p.142–146 (Bernstein)

06
Forces for Free

Movement happens in a world of physics — gravity, reactive forces, body connectivity are gifts. "Walking is merely controlled falling." Coordination is sculpting, not building.

In the cageCue base, suspension, redirecting force — cooperate with physics instead of muscling it.

p.143–144

07
Repetition w/o Repetition

No two reps are ever the same. You learn by repeating the same problem; you master by repeating the goal across varied contexts.

In the cageVariability is the feature. Vary opponents/contexts once the pattern exists.

p.145, 169

08
Attractor Layouts

Skill is a landscape of attractors (deep = stable = default under pressure). Coaches reshape it by turning control parameters — speed, pressure, range.

In the cageExpect abrupt reorganization past a threshold — that's why a small rule change can revolutionize a style.

p.234–251 (HKB)

09
Self-Organization

Order emerges from local rules + constant interaction — no conductor, no blueprint. Ants find the shortest path; robots build houses.

In the cageThe coach gives rules and lets athletes adapt to circumstance — not a choreography.

p.270–285

10
Constraints

You change behavior by changing constraints, not components (flip the cans, the metronomes sync). A good constraint reduces options so behavior emerges.

In the cage"Outlets every 12 feet," not "outlet right here." Constrain to afford, never to prescribe.

p.61, 271

11
Learning = Attunement

Learning fine-tunes perceiving-and-acting: educate intention (which affordance), educate attention (which information), then calibrate (scale the response).

In the cageBuild paths as: choose the affordance → attune to the invariant → calibrate with feedback.

p.204–206

12
No Transfer Across Modes

Skills are specific. Infants who learned safe slopes crawling started from zero when walking — different limbs, different information.

In the cageReading τ for striking ≠ reading τ for takedowns. Build separate per-mode paths; verify transfer.

p.160, 206

13
Perceiving WITH Others

You perceive affordances for yourself AND the opponent — and read them by watching the opponent move. It's information detection, not mind-reading.

In the cageReward reading what the opponent's position affords them. Study movement that shares force dynamics with the threat.

p.175–191

14
Dynamic Touch

You feel an object's length, structure, and "off-balance-able-ness" through the inertial array — resistance to rotation at the grip — even blindfolded.

In the cageClinch/grappling wins = perceiving & exploiting felt structure. Cue attuning to pressure through the grips.

p.122–127

The Evidence That Sticks

Eight studies
worth remembering.

The Metronomes

Metronomes on a free-moving board self-synchronize. To change them you don't touch the metronomes — you flip the cans so the board can't move. Change the constraint, not the components.

Warren's Stairs

"Climb-able" isn't a height — it's a ratio (step ÷ leg, critical 0.88). Tall and short people perceive the same boundary by ratio. Affordances are body-scaled.

Bernstein's Blacksmiths

Expert smiths' joint paths varied every strike — but the hammer-head path stayed constant. The synergy preserves the goal, not the movement.

The Jaw Tug

Tug a speaker's jaw mid-syllable: the lips compensate for "b," the tongue for "z." The synergy self-corrects to protect the goal, recruiting whatever parts it needs.

The Robot Tournament

Robots trained on variable infant-walking paths beat robots trained on regular paths 2,888 to 75 over 4,000 games. Variability builds adaptability.

Crawling ↛ Walking

Babies who mastered safe slopes crawling went right off the edge once walking. Skill doesn't transfer across action modes — the information is different.

Kendo Distance

Fighters live at two ranges — near (affords offense), far (affords defense) — and avoid the dead middle. Experts spend more time in the offensive range.

Chicago's Lines

Painted lines that speed up optic flow into a curve made drivers slow down with zero conscious thought — 36% fewer crashes. Information beats instruction.

How It Changes Our Logic

The working rules.

The practical takeaways — what every game and constraint we build must obey.

The unit is the fighter–opponent–cage system — never the fighter alone.
Constrain to afford, never prescribe. If a rule forces one solution, it's over-constraining.
Scale by ratios, not absolutes — so every body faces the same relational problem.
Name the invariant to attune to per game (τ, optic flow, inertial array).
Reward the taken opportunity, not isolated cues.
Never decompose the perception–action loop. No static, unopposed drills.
Let synergies assemble; cue cooperation with free forces.
Repeat the problem, not the movement. Variability is the feature.
Treat skill as an attractor layout you reshape with control parameters.
Use instability on purpose — the amber zone is functional noise.
Assume no transfer across modes; build separate per-mode paths.
Coach by local rules, not a blueprint — give athletes room to adapt.
Build distance games on two ranges + a dead middle (near=offense, far=defense).
Prefer information over instruction; design affordances into the environment.
Quick Lexicon

The words.

Affordance
An opportunity for action in the animal–environment relationship. "-able."
Invariant
A higher-order pattern in energy that stays constant and specifies an affordance.
τ — Tau
Rate of optical expansion → time-to-contact. The information for timing.
Synergy
A temporary "choir" of muscles assembled to a task; goal-preserving, self-correcting.
Attractor
A stable behavior the system falls into; deepest = most stable & efficient.
Control parameter
A dial (speed, pressure, range) that reorganizes the attractor layout.
Order parameter
The macro variable you measure (e.g., relative phase / timing).
Self-organization
Pattern emerging from local rules + interaction, with no leader.
Education of attention
Learning which information matters most for an intended action.
Calibration
Learning to scale your response to the detected information (mode-specific).
Critical π ratio
The body-scaled value where an affordance appears/disappears.
Degeneracy
Many different solutions reaching the same goal (multirealizability).
