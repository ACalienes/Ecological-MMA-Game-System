# Safety & Intensity Rules

<div class="emma-meta"><span>Coaching Tool</span><span>Non-Negotiable</span><span>Applies to Every Game</span></div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Safety principles in this system are non-negotiable.</b> They cannot be overridden for competitive pressure, athlete preference, or training efficiency. <i>The game serves learning, never the other way around.</i></p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Five Non-Negotiable Principles</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🦵</div><div class="emma-card__t">No falling bodyweight onto legs</div><p>Takedowns and transitions never drop bodyweight onto an opponent's legs or knees.<span class="emma-pop">This is the most common source of serious training injury, protect the knee at all costs.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">⚖️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Control precedes gravity</div><p>All takedowns are created via <b>off-balancing</b>, not dumping. The base must be compromised before descent begins.<span class="emma-pop">If you have to force someone down, you haven't earned the takedown, and you're inviting a scramble injury.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">👆</div><div class="emma-card__t">Strikes are informational</div><p>Strikes in games are <b>light and informational</b>. They measure distance, create reactions, and test control, not cause damage.<span class="emma-pop">A strike's job in a game is to specify information, not to hurt a training partner.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔒</div><div class="emma-card__t">Submissions are emergent</div><p>Submissions (especially DNS) are <b>defensive tools</b> that emerge from constraints, not primary objectives to be hunted.<span class="emma-pop">No jumping guard, no sitting to finish, no chasing submissions as the main goal.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🛑</div><div class="emma-card__t">Coach intervention is mandatory</div><p>The coach <b>must</b> stop play the moment the game stops serving its purpose.<span class="emma-pop">Intervention isn't optional supervision, it's a structural part of every game.</span></p></div>
</div>

    The **Submissions are emergent** principle contains errors and needs revision. Content is provisional until corrected.

<p class="emma-glabel">When the Coach Must Stop Play</p>

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li>Structure degrades into scrambling chaos</li>
  <li>Intensity escalates beyond appropriate levels</li>
  <li>Any safety violation occurs</li>
  <li>Learning value has disappeared</li>
</ul>

<p class="emma-glabel">Intensity Zones</p>

<p class="emma-hl">Match effort to development. These are <b>learning environments, not competition simulations</b>, quality reading beats maximal output at every level.</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🟢</div><div class="emma-card__t">Beginner · 30–40%</div><p>Focus on pattern recognition.<span class="emma-pop">Low intensity keeps the problem visible, the athlete is learning what to look for before learning to do it under fire.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🟡</div><div class="emma-card__t">Intermediate · 40–60%</div><p>Add resistance and timing pressure.<span class="emma-pop">Enough live pressure to force adaptation, still controlled enough to keep structure intact.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🟠</div><div class="emma-card__t">Advanced · 60–75%</div><p>Realistic problem-solving.<span class="emma-pop">Close to fight realism, but still inside the cap, the skill must hold under genuine resistance.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔴</div><div class="emma-card__t">Maximum Cap · 80%</div><p>Never exceed in training games.<span class="emma-pop">Even advanced athletes stay under 80% in ecological games, beyond it, learning gives way to survival and risk.</span></p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Red Flags: Stop Immediately</p>

<div class="emma-warns">
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Brute force without technique<span class="emma-pop">Indicates the <b>constraints are mis-set</b>, the game is rewarding strength over skill.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Memorized repetitions without adaptation<span class="emma-pop">The game is <b>too predictable</b>, there's no live problem left to solve.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Ego-driven intensity escalation<span class="emma-pop">A <b>competitive mindset has taken over</b>, the learning frame is gone.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Panic responses<span class="emma-pop">The <b>athlete is overwhelmed</b>, the problem is too hard for their current level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Static grinding without progress<span class="emma-pop"><b>No learning is occurring</b>, the exchange has stalled out.</span></span>
</div>

When red flags appear, stop the game and reassess:

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li>Are the constraints appropriate?</li>
  <li>Is intensity appropriate?</li>
  <li>Does the athlete understand the problem?</li>
</ul>

<p class="emma-glabel">Safety Rules by Environment</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🟦</div><div class="emma-card__t">Open Space</div><p>No chasing collisions · no forward crashes · reset if exchanges become static.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧱</div><div class="emma-card__t">Wall</div><p>No grinding into the wall · no neck cranks · light strikes only.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔻</div><div class="emma-card__t">Transitions (Wall → Ground)</div><p><b>Control precedes gravity</b> · no dumping bodyweight · no slamming · stop if descent becomes uncontrolled.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🟫</div><div class="emma-card__t">Ground</div><p>No falling onto legs · DNS recognized and stopped before completion · no sustained suffocation.</p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">DNS in Training</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Defensive Neck Submissions</b> (guillotines, front headlocks) occur when the attacker's head drops into a vulnerable position. <b>DNS is not a safety concern</b>, it's a natural consequence of poor head position, and the defender may capitalize with a choke.</p>

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Playing to submission</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">DNS goes to finish</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">DNS leads to ground, submission maintained</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Continue to finish</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Any submission</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Standard tap rules apply</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Intervention Language</p>

<p>Coaches use clear, consistent commands so play stops the instant it needs to.</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔁</span> "Reset"<span class="emma-chip__detail">Return to the starting position.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">❄️</span> "Freeze"<span class="emma-chip__detail">Stop all movement immediately.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📉</span> "Check intensity"<span class="emma-chip__detail">Reduce effort level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> "Time"<span class="emma-chip__detail">Round complete.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚪</span> "Out"<span class="emma-chip__detail">Exit the game, a safety concern has been raised.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Athlete Responsibility</p>

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li>Respect tap signals immediately</li>
  <li>Self-regulate intensity</li>
  <li>Report any discomfort or injury</li>
  <li>Prioritize partner safety over "winning"</li>
</ul>

!!! tip "Culture of Safety"
    The safest gyms are those where athletes actively protect each other, not just themselves.

??? abstract "System evolution notice"
    Safety rules may be expanded as new games or contexts are added. They expand, they never relax.
