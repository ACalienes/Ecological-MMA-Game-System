# The Principles

## Mutuality & Ecological Scale

The foundational assumption: **the animal–environment system is the fundamental unit of analysis.** You cannot understand the animal without its environment, nor the environment without the animal that inhabits it, the environment is not a "factor" added to an equation, it is part of the system from the start. Fred's fear on a rickety bridge is nested in the *Fred-bridge* system *because he cannot fly*; his particular action capabilities constrain the meaning of the event.

The second move is **scale discipline.** Ecological psychology rejects reductionism, the whole is different from the sum of its parts, and studies *macroscopic, ecological* variables, not the lower-order variables of Newtonian physics. "If the answer is still ambiguous, you are probably still standing too close." Causality is treated as nonlocal and nonlinear; the body is a *system, not a machine*, and you change a system's behavior by **changing constraints, not components** (flip the cans so the board can't move, and the metronomes sync).

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Every game includes the opponent and the live environmental constraints (cage edge, range, surface). Describe behavior at the macroscopic scale, reads, distance, pressure, off-balancing, never at the joint-angle scale. The stylistic matchup is the unit, not the fighter.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 57–64)*

## Direct Perception

Traditional theories posit a **central executive** that receives ambiguous stimulation, "fixes" it, and infers the world. Blau &amp; Wagman banish it: it requires a *loan of intelligence* (it must already be smart to do its job), suffers the *outness* and *feedback* problems, and, fatally, **lacks explanatory power** (positing it just pushes the question back a step).

Perception is instead **direct**: the animal detects lawfully structured information; nothing needs to be processed, only detected. *Direct does not mean unmediated by tools*, perception through an interface, a hand-held rod, or a grip is still direct, because directness is about whether perception is mediated by something *mental* (a representation or inference), not something *physical*.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Cues direct attention to information, never prescribe a computation or an internal body-part movement. Tight Block's "<b>see center mass</b>, the eyes lie" is exactly this: it points the eyes at the informative variable. Drop any "read → decide → react" sequential framing.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 56, 82, 87–88, 219)*

## Information & Invariants

Animals are immersed in an **ocean of energy**, patterns of light, sound, and physical force. This energy is not uniform; surface and substance properties *lawfully structure* it. At any **point of observation** the animal encounters a unique distribution of structured energy (the **ambient optic array** for light). Because the structuring is lawful and unambiguous, the energy pattern **specifies**, carries information about, the affordances of surrounding surfaces. The relevant variables are **higher-order invariants**, not lower-order properties.

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⏱️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Tau (τ)</div><p>The relative rate of optical expansion of an object's texture; specifies <b>time-to-contact</b>, assuming current conditions continue. Used for braking, catching, defensive blinking. <i>(p. 74, 165–166)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🌊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Global optic flow</div><p>Specifies self-motion and how to control it; rate of flow = movement speed. The moving-room and Chicago-lines paradigms. <i>(p. 147–148, 211)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Inertial array (I)</div><p>For effortful touch, resistance to rotation at a grasp point specifies length, orientation, and "hit-with-able-ness." <i>(p. 123–127)</i></p></div>
</div>

Lawfulness implies the **same information is available across energy forms** (multiple modalities can specify the same affordance) and **across nervous systems** (the brain's details are "potentially irrelevant", this is a psychology for *all* animals).

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Name the invariant per game. "Reading" means attuning to <b>τ</b> on a closing strike or shot, to <b>global optic flow</b> for self-motion and cage approach, and to the <b>inertial array</b> through grips and frames, not vaguely "seeing openings."</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 82, 91–93, 123–127, 165–166, 218)*

## Affordances

**Affordances are opportunities for behavior that emerge from the relationship between animal and environment.** They are neither purely objective (a property of the environment) nor purely subjective (in the animal), like friction, they emerge from a *relationship*. "Hide-behind-able" exists for the cat but not the dog, and only when there is both an organism and a place to hide. Affordances are the activity-specific *meanings* of the environment, and because meaning is in the relationship, *meanings can be perceived directly*.

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔗</div><div class="emma-card__t">Relational</div><p>Animal-relative and contextual: a puddle affords jumping when running, not when standing still. <i>(p. 76)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔮</div><div class="emma-card__t">The (im)possible future</div><p>Catching a ball requires perceiving where it <i>will be</i>; you perceive whether a behavior could be performed before attempting it. <i>(p. 74)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧩</div><div class="emma-card__t">Multiple fit</div><p>A surface affords standing only if horizontal AND flat AND rigid AND extended, miss one and the affordance is absent. <i>(p. 77)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎒</div><div class="emma-card__t">Animal-plus-object</div><p>Affordances are for the system including gloves, pads, fatigue, a moving body. <i>(p. 86–87)</i></p></div>
</div>

A subtle point: perceiving an affordance **does not require perceiving its component properties.** You perceive the opportunity *in and of itself*, not the measurements, improving perception of leg length does not improve perception of the jump-and-reach affordance.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Keep the "-able" vocabulary, strikeable, takedown-able, escape-able. Score the <i>taken opportunity</i> (a clean counter, a completed off-balance), not an isolated cue like "hands dropped." An affordance is a simultaneous multi-property fit, not a single property.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 73–89)*

## Body-Scaling (Critical π Ratios)

Whether a step is "step-on-able" depends not on absolute step height but on the **ratio of step height to leg length.** Warren's (1984) stair-climbing studies are the paradigm: plotted against absolute height, tall and short people give different curves; plotted against the *ratio*, the curves overlap onto a single critical π value of **0.88** (above which stepping becomes impossible), and the *perceived* boundary matched the biomechanical one.

The same logic governs crossing gaps (leg length), fitting through apertures (shoulder width, critical ratio ≈ 1.16–1.3), and reaching (arm length). A related finding: people transition to a new mode, one-hand to two-hand grasp, reach to lean, at a comfortable **preferred boundary** *before* their absolute limit.

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Absolute height</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Different curves per body, ambiguous</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Ratio (step ÷ leg)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">One critical value, 0.88, the same problem for every body</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> This is HOW we individualize. When a game sets a distance, target height, or aperture, scale it to the athlete's reach / leg-length / shoulder-width ratio, so a 5'5" wrestler and a 6'2" kickboxer face the <i>same relational problem</i>. Let athletes find their own preferred transition point rather than forcing the absolute-limit solution.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, Warren, 1984, p. 78–81, 84–88)*

## Perception–Action Loop

Perception's purpose is to **guide doing**, Turvey's reframing is "getting light into the *muscles*." Perceiving and acting are continuous, not separate sequential stages, a **Möbius-strip** relationship. Perceiving is an activity that unfolds over time: information (τ, or the inertial array) "can be detected only as the animal or an object moves." Action generates the information that guides further action. **This loop cannot be decomposed without destroying the information.**

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Maintain the loop in every task. Simplify by scaling the <i>whole</i> task, slower, smaller, lighter, fewer options, never by breaking perception off from action. A static, unopposed drill removes the very information the skill needs.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 90, 139, 192, 207)*

## Synergies & Degrees of Freedom

Controlling the body via individual anatomical units is a nonstarter: one arm alone involves 7 joint degrees of freedom and ~26 muscles → ~4×10²⁶ combinations; the whole body has 600+ muscles. This is **Bernstein's degrees-of-freedom problem.**

The solution is the **synergy** (coordinative structure): "a grouping of potentially independent anatomical components that work together as a functional unit", a "choir of muscles that harmonize." Synergies are **soft-assembled** (temporary, task-specific), **goal-preserving and self-correcting**, and produce **micro-level variability with macro-level consistency.**

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🗣️</div><div class="emma-card__t">The jaw tug</div><p>Perturb the jaw mid-syllable: the <b>lips</b> compensate for "b, " the <b>tongue</b> for "z." The synergy preserves the goal, recruiting whatever parts it needs. <i>(p. 146)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔨</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bernstein's blacksmiths</div><p>Joint trajectories vary strike to strike, but the <b>hammer-head trajectory</b> stays consistent. <i>(p. 145)</i></p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Let synergies soft-assemble to the goal. "Points for staying standing" lets a sprawl / underhook / lateral solution assemble; "when they shoot you must sprawl" wrongly prescribes anatomy. Score the goal, not the movement.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 142–146)*

## Forces for Free

Movements occur *in* a world and *with* a body that both obey physics, so many forces are provided gratis, gravity, reactive forces, the body's connectivity. "Walking is merely controlled falling." Coordinated movement is about *capitalizing on and cooperating with* free forces, it is "more like sculpting than like building."

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Cue cooperation with physics. Tight Block's "<b>base under fire</b>, create suspension like an ATV over bumps" and The Square's "<b>redirect directional force</b> rather than absorb it" are textbook forces-for-free cues. Make this a standard cue category.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 143–144)*

## Repetition Without Repetition

"No two instances of a given behavior are exactly the same. They can't be. The context will always differ." Therefore *learning* a skill may require practicing the same movement in the same context, but **mastering** it requires achieving the *goal* using different behaviors across different contexts.

The strongest evidence: in a 4,000-game tournament, robots trained on highly *variable* infant-walking paths beat robots trained on regular paths **2,888 to 75.** Variability is what builds adaptability.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Repeat the <i>problem</i>, not the movement. Coordination stage, repeat the same problem to assemble the synergy. Mastery stage, repeat the goal across varied opponents and contexts. Variability is the feature, not the bug.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 145, 169–170)*

## Dynamical Systems & Attractors

Behavior is modeled as a **ball on an attractor layout.** Locations on the sheet are possible behaviors; the ball is current behavior; basins are **attractors**, stable behaviors that attract nearby behaviors "the way a magnet attracts iron." The deepest attractor is the most stable and usually the most efficient.

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">📍</div><div class="emma-card__t">Order parameter</div><p>The macro variable that captures the coordination state, e.g., <b>relative phase</b> between two oscillators. What you measure. <i>(p. 251)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎛️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Control parameter</div><p>A variable that, when changed, reorganizes the layout, e.g., <b>movement speed</b> makes the anti-phase attractor vanish. <i>(p. 250)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🌑</div><div class="emma-card__t">Death of an attractor</div><p>A stable pattern destabilizes and disappears, breaking a habit; anti-phase at speed. <i>(p. 238)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✨</div><div class="emma-card__t">Birth of an attractor</div><p>Learning a genuinely new pattern, the juggling study. <b>This is learning.</b> <i>(p. 240)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">↔️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Movement of an attractor</div><p>Adjusting an existing skill to a new context, prism-glasses throwing. <b>This is adaptation.</b> <i>(p. 241)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">〰️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Functional noise</div><p>Perturbation lets the ball escape a shallow attractor and find a deeper one, not error. <i>(p. 242–243)</i></p></div>
</div>

The distinction between **learning** (new attractor) and **adaptation** (moved attractor) matters. So do **hysteresis** (current state depends on history), **equifinality** (different starts, same end), and **pluripotentiality** (same start, different ends).

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Treat a fighter's habitual patterns as an attractor layout the coach reshapes, deepen a desired pattern, destabilize an unwanted one. Name your control parameters (speed, pressure, range, fatigue, # opponents) and your order parameter (what you measure). Expect <i>abrupt</i> reorganization past a critical value, that's why a minor rule change can revolutionize a style.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 234–251)*

## Self-Organization

**Self-organization** is the alternative to *other*-organization (a central executive, a blueprint, a conductor), and it is the rule, not the exception, in biological systems. Its definition has four load-bearing parts:

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li><b>Not other-organized</b>, no external leader, no blueprint, no rigid hierarchy.</li>
  <li><b>Components are minimally intelligent</b>, they follow simple rules; no loan of intelligence.</li>
  <li><b>Rules are local</b>, followed using only local information, requiring constant mutual interaction (remove the interaction and the pattern stops).</li>
  <li><b>Patterns emerge</b>, a nonrandom configuration comprehensible only at a larger scale than the parts, and not predictable from them.</li>
</ul>

Self-organized systems are dynamic, **recover from perturbations** (the orchestra player who loses their place re-synchronizes with neighbors), are **multistable** (convection rolls, bathtub vortex), and **multirealizable** (the same legs walk, kick, skate, arabesque). Ants find the shortest path via pheromone local rules; robots build whole houses from simple local rules. The deep lesson: a wise designer gives contractors *a list of rules and lets them adapt to local circumstances*, "the houses will be far more adapted… and you will have to do a lot less work."

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> The coach is a rule-giver, not a choreographer. Give local rules (the game's constraints) and let the fighting style emerge. Perturb a working pattern and reward re-organization, score the recovery, not just the pattern.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 270–285)*

## Learning = Attunement

Learning is **the fine-tuning of perceiving-and-acting abilities**, not the accumulation of facts or the enrichment of representations. Four named processes:

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Education of intention</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Which behaviors to attempt, and which affordances to perceive</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Education of attention</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Which components of the array carry the most useful information, shift toward them</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Calibration</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">How to scale the response to the detected information (the information–action mapping)</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Perceptual differentiation</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Sensitivity to an informative variable develops slowly, over long timescales</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Sequence games as <b>educate intention → educate attention → calibrate.</b> Choose which affordance to go for, attune to the invariant that specifies it ("see center mass, " attune to τ), then scale the response with feedback that reduces over time. Don't expect beginners to perceive what experts perceive.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, Jacobs &amp; Michaels, 2007, p. 204–206; differentiation p. 168)*

## No-Transfer-Across-Modes

The single most important learning finding for us: **animals learn *specific* affordances, not general facts.** Infants who learned which slopes afford safe *crawling* did **not** transfer that to *walking*, they "started over from zero" after the new motor skill, because the *information* about affordances for crawling is entirely different from that for walking (different limbs, different optic flow, different base of support).

Calibration is the same: subjects calibrated to a visual drift while *walking* did **not** transfer it to *throwing or kicking*, what they learned was "a specific relationship between optic flow direction and walking, " not a general fact about the world.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Assume <b>no transfer across action modes</b> and verify empirically. A fighter who reads τ for <i>striking</i> defense has <i>not</i> thereby learned to read τ for <i>takedown</i> defense, the information differs. Build separate per-mode learning paths. The Square's striking → clinch → takedown scaffold is right precisely because it re-educates attention at each new mode.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, crawling↛walking p. 160–161; walking↛throwing p. 206)*

## Perceiving / Acting With Others

The same lawful principles that govern animal–environment systems govern animal–animal–environment systems. The book frames combat directly. Three pillars:

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👀</div><div class="emma-card__t">Affordances for others</div><p>The same properties that structure the energy <i>you</i> encounter structure what <i>others</i> encounter, so you perceive affordances for self AND for them. Watching someone <i>walk</i> informs their jump-and-reach (shared leg forces); watching them <i>twist</i> does not. Basketball players read others' jump-reach better. <i>(p. 175, 180–182)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">➕</div><div class="emma-card__t">Emergent, not additive</div><p>Two people through a doorway turn shoulders at <i>less</i> than the sum of their widths, they share personal space. Pair affordances are emergent. <i>(p. 184–185)</i></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🪑</div><div class="emma-card__t">Social synergies</div><p>Rocking chairs synchronize <i>unintentionally</i> when people can see each other. Driven by detecting information about each other, <b>not</b> mirror neurons. <i>(p. 177–178, 186–191)</i></p></div>
</div>

**The combat-sport gem (kendo):** competitors exhibit **two preferred interpersonal distances**, a **near** distance that affords offense and a **far** distance that affords defense, and they **avoid the middle distance that affords neither.** More-experienced competitors spend *more* time at the near (offensive) distance because they can move into striking position faster. The decision to step toward or away is based on **perceived affordances for striking the opponent.**

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Reward reading what the opponent's position affords <i>them</i>. Build distance games on two preferred ranges + a dead middle; make "spends more time in the offensive range" a progression marker. Study opponent movement that shares force dynamics with the threat (level change ↔ shot). Frame it as information detection, never "imagine yourself in their position."</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 172–191; kendo Box 11.2 p. 188–189)*

## Dynamic Touch & Haptics

The information for touch is the **inertia tensor (I)**, the resistance to rotation in different directions at a grasp point. Wielding an object specifies its length, orientation, and "hit-with-able-ness." Perceived length tracks the **major principal moment of inertia (I_xx)**, *not* geometric length directly, Fred perceives a racket's length and hit-with-able-ness blindfolded, by feel alone.

A fighter perceives the opponent's structure, base, and "off-balance-able-ness" through **what their grips and frames feel**, the inertial array, exactly as Fred perceives the racket. Stable base is the postural synergy that *supports* offense: posture and the behavior it supports are cooperative, not competitive.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>In the cage:</b> Clinch and grappling games should make the win condition about <b>perceiving and exploiting felt information</b>, off-balancing by redirecting directional force (The Square's "redirect, don't just absorb"). Cue athletes to attune to pressure and resistance-to-rotation through the grips.</p>

*(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 122–127, 149–151)*
