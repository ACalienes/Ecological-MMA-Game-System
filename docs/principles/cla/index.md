<p class="emma-glabel">What Is the Constraints-Led Approach?</p>

<p class="emma-hl">CLA is a coaching methodology predicated on <b>Ecological Dynamics Theory</b> — it reframes what athletes, skill, learning, and the coach actually are.</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🧬</div><div class="emma-card__t">Athletes</div><p>Are <b>complex adaptive systems</b>.<span class="emma-pop">Networks of integrated physical, cognitive, emotional, and tactical subsystems — not a fixed set of techniques to be programmed.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🌱</div><div class="emma-card__t">Skill</div><p>Is <b>successful adaptation</b> to the environment.<span class="emma-pop">Not the acquisition of fixed techniques — skill lives in the relationship between performer and environment.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔀</div><div class="emma-card__t">Learning</div><p>Emerges from the <b>interaction of constraints</b>.<span class="emma-pop">Not from instruction-following — behavior reorganizes as constraints change.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🏛️</div><div class="emma-card__t">The Coach</div><p>Is an <b>environment architect</b>.<span class="emma-pop">A learning designer, not a technique prescriber — they build the conditions and let solutions emerge.</span></p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Fundamental premise:</b> "The individual–environment relationship is the appropriate scale of analysis for understanding human behavior." For MMA, the <b>fighter–environment relationship</b> (opponent, cage/ring, rules, surface) is the fundamental unit — not the fighter in isolation.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Four Environment Design Principles</p>

These principles operationalize CLA into actionable coaching methodology — see [Environment Design](environment-design/) for full details.

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">1️⃣</div><div class="emma-card__t">Session Intention</div><p>What is the overriding goal that guides all decisions?<span class="emma-pop">The intention acts as an overriding, organizational constraint — every other decision answers to it.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">2️⃣</div><div class="emma-card__t">Constrain to Afford</div><p>What constraints will invite the behaviors we want?<span class="emma-pop">Design-in constraints that offer, invite, and encourage — without forcing one specific solution.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">3️⃣</div><div class="emma-card__t">Representative Learning Design</div><p>Does practice look AND feel like competition?<span class="emma-pop">Sample the critical informational constraints of the performance environment so solutions transfer.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">4️⃣</div><div class="emma-card__t">Repetition Without Repetition</div><p>Are we repeating problem-solving, not movements?<span class="emma-pop">Design-in the right amount of variability so athletes repeat the problem, not identical reps.</span></p></div>
</div>
