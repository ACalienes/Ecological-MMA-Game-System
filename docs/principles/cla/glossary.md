# Glossary of Terms

<p class="emma-hl"><b>The shared vocabulary of the system.</b> Every term traces to ecological dynamics, affordances, synergies, constraints, attractors. Hover or tap any term for its full definition.</p>

??? abstract "Sources"
    Definitions adapted from Renshaw et al. (2019), Gibson (1979), Bernstein (1967), Newell (1986), and Kelso (1995). For complete bibliography, see <a href="../../../reference/sources/">Sources &amp; Theoretical Foundations</a>.

<p class="emma-glabel">A</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📐</span> Action Fidelity<span class="emma-chip__detail">The degree to which movement solutions developed in practice will be effective in the performance environment.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔄</span> Adaptation<span class="emma-chip__detail">The process by which behavior becomes better suited to a specific environment.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">💓</span> Affective Learning Design (ALD)<span class="emma-chip__detail">Practice design that represents the emotional demands of competition, ensuring practice not only looks like but feels like the real thing.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎁</span> Affordance<span class="emma-chip__detail">An opportunity for action that the environment offers relative to an individual's capabilities. Affordances are relational (between person and environment), action-relevant, and can be perceived directly.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🗺️</span> Affordance Landscape<span class="emma-chip__detail">The range of opportunities for action available in a practice or performance environment, ranging from narrow (few options) to rich (many options).</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🟠</span> Amber Zone<span class="emma-chip__detail">A variability zone representing productive instability at the "edge of chaos" where new solutions must be searched for, reorganization is promoted, and optimal learning occurs.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Attunement<span class="emma-chip__detail">The process of becoming increasingly sensitive to relevant information in the environment.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">C</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎛️</span> Calibration<span class="emma-chip__detail">Adjusting the relationship between perceived information and action execution to achieve desired outcomes.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤝</span> Co-Adaptation<span class="emma-chip__detail">The continuous mutual adjustment between interacting individuals, such as attacker and defender in combat sports.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🕸️</span> Complex Adaptive System<span class="emma-chip__detail">A system composed of many interacting components that can adapt and self-organize. Athletes and teams are viewed as complex adaptive systems in CLA.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚧</span> Constraints<span class="emma-chip__detail">Information sources that shape the emergence of behavior; boundaries that guide organization. Constraints are not negative limitations but neutral features that reduce possible configurations to functional ones.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧭</span> Constraints-Led Approach (CLA)<span class="emma-chip__detail">A coaching methodology based on manipulating task, individual, and environmental constraints to guide skill development through self-organization rather than prescription.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔗</span> Coordinative Structure<span class="emma-chip__detail">See Synergy.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">D</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">♻️</span> Degeneracy<span class="emma-chip__detail">The ability of structurally different elements to achieve the same functional outcome. In MMA, having multiple techniques that can accomplish the same goal (e.g., various ways to achieve a takedown).</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔢</span> Degrees of Freedom<span class="emma-chip__detail">The independent components (muscles, joints, limb segments) that must be organized for coordinated action. The "degrees of freedom problem" refers to the challenge of organizing these many components.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤹</span> Dexterity<span class="emma-chip__detail">The ability to find motor solutions that are correct, quick, rational, and resourceful. Reflects the ability to solve novel movement problems in the moment.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">👥</span> Dyadic System<span class="emma-chip__detail">A two-person interaction unit (e.g., attacker-defender pair) that forms the basic unit of analysis in combat sports.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">E</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🌐</span> Ecological Dynamics<span class="emma-chip__detail">The theoretical framework integrating ecological psychology, dynamical systems theory, and complexity science that underlies CLA.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">💪</span> Effectivity<span class="emma-chip__detail">An individual's capability to act on environmental affordances. The complement to affordance (environment offers, individual can use).</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">✨</span> Emergence<span class="emma-chip__detail">Novel behaviors arising from the interaction of system components, not prescribed or pre-planned. In CLA, technique emerges from constraints rather than being taught directly.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📐</span> Environment Design Principles (EDP)<span class="emma-chip__detail">Framework of four principles for CLA session design: Session Intention, Constrain to Afford, Representative Learning Design, and Repetition Without Repetition.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🌍</span> Environmental Constraints<span class="emma-chip__detail">Global features external to the task that shape behavior, including physical conditions (temperature, surface), social factors (culture, crowd), and historical factors (traditions, rule evolution).</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">F</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📍</span> Fixed Principles<span class="emma-chip__detail">Signature positions or anchors that functional movements tend to pass through, representing stable states in coordination.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🏛️</span> Form of Life<span class="emma-chip__detail">The behaviors, skills, attitudes, and practices that shape culture in a society, organization, or training environment.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧊</span> Freezing Degrees of Freedom<span class="emma-chip__detail">A strategy in early learning where the performer locks out movement options to reduce complexity, resulting in stiff or rigid movement patterns.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">G</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🟢</span> Green Zone<span class="emma-chip__detail">A variability zone representing optimal challenge where success likelihood is high, exploration is promoted, and adaptability can develop without overwhelming the learner.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📋</span> GROW Model<span class="emma-chip__detail">Goal-Reality-Options-Way Forward planning framework adapted for CLA session design.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">I</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">👤</span> Individual Constraints<span class="emma-chip__detail">Personal characteristics unique to each performer that shape behavior, including physical (height, weight), psychological (motivation, confidence), experiential (skill level, history), and biological (fatigue, injury) factors. Also called Organismic Constraints.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔗</span> Individual-Environment Mutuality<span class="emma-chip__detail">The inseparable relationship between performer and performance context. The fundamental premise of ecological dynamics.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">〰️</span> Instability<span class="emma-chip__detail">Temporary perturbation of existing patterns, often necessary for reorganization and learning. Distinguished from stability (consistent patterns) and chaos (excessive disruption).</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Intention<span class="emma-chip__detail">A goal-directed cognitive state that shapes perception-action coupling. Session intention is the overriding goal that guides constraint manipulation.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧬</span> Intrinsic Dynamics<span class="emma-chip__detail">Existing coordination tendencies an individual brings to a learning situation, which interact with task demands to produce behavior.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">K</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📖</span> Knowledge ABOUT<span class="emma-chip__detail">Declarative, verbalizable understanding of the environment, gained through instruction, video, and discussion.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤸</span> Knowledge OF<span class="emma-chip__detail">Procedural, action-supporting understanding developed only through direct experience in the environment. Cannot be taught verbally.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">N</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🌀</span> Nonlinear Pedagogy<span class="emma-chip__detail">The pedagogical framework derived from ecological dynamics that serves as the bridge between theory and CLA methodology.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📈</span> Nonlinearity<span class="emma-chip__detail">Disproportionate relationship between inputs and outputs; small changes can have large effects, and large changes can have small effects. Learning is nonlinear.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">O</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤲</span> Optimal Grip<span class="emma-chip__detail">Having functional control over the field of affordances in a given context. The hallmark of skilled performance.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">👤</span> Organismic Constraints<span class="emma-chip__detail">See Individual Constraints.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔒</span> Over-Constraining<span class="emma-chip__detail">Manipulating constraints to force rather than invite behavior, removing decision-making and exploration.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">P</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔁</span> Perception-Action Coupling<span class="emma-chip__detail">The tight integration of information pickup and movement execution. Information regulates action; action guides further information pickup. A continuous loop, not sequential processes.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">R</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏳</span> Rate Limiter<span class="emma-chip__detail">A constraint that currently limits the emergence of higher-level performance. Identifying the rate limiter helps target training.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔴</span> Red Zone<span class="emma-chip__detail">A variability zone representing excessive chaos where variability is unmanageable, overwhelm occurs, and learning is disrupted.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔄</span> Repetition Without Repetition<span class="emma-chip__detail">Bernstein's principle that practice should repeat the process of problem-solving rather than identical movements. Variation in execution while repeating the goal.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📡</span> Representative Learning Design (RLD)<span class="emma-chip__detail">Practice design that samples critical informational features of the performance environment, ensuring what athletes see, hear, and feel in practice is similar to competition.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">S</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🌱</span> Self-Organization<span class="emma-chip__detail">Spontaneous emergence of coordinated patterns without external micromanagement. Behavior organizes itself under constraints rather than being prescribed.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Session Intention<span class="emma-chip__detail">The overriding goal that guides constraint manipulation in a training session. One of the four EDP principles.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤝</span> Shared Affordances<span class="emma-chip__detail">Opportunities for action that exist between multiple individuals, as in partner or team training.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚡</span> Skill<span class="emma-chip__detail">In ecological dynamics, the ability to successfully adapt to a performance environment. Defined as relationship between performer and environment, not as internal acquisition.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧩</span> Soft Assembly<span class="emma-chip__detail">The temporary, context-dependent formation of coordinative structures. Synergies are soft assembled (not hardwired) and can rapidly form and dissolve.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎼</span> Synergy<span class="emma-chip__detail">A temporarily assembled grouping of body segments, muscles, and joints that function as a single unit to achieve a task goal. Also called Coordinative Structure.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">T</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">📋</span> Task Constraints<span class="emma-chip__detail">Features specific to the activity being performed that shape behavior, including goals, rules, equipment, spatial boundaries, and time limits.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">✂️</span> Task Decomposition<span class="emma-chip__detail">Breaking a task into isolated parts for separate practice. Problematic in CLA because it separates perception from action.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> Task Simplification<span class="emma-chip__detail">Scaling difficulty while keeping the task whole. Preferred over decomposition because it maintains perception-action coupling.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">➡️</span> Transfer<span class="emma-chip__detail">The application of learning from one context (training) to another (competition). The ultimate measure of training effectiveness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">V</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎲</span> Variability<span class="emma-chip__detail">Differences in movement patterns or practice conditions. In CLA, variability is deliberately designed into practice to build adaptability.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚦</span> Variability Zones<span class="emma-chip__detail">Categories (Green/Amber/Red) describing the challenge level of practice environments, from stable and predictable to chaotic and overwhelming.</span></span>
</div>
