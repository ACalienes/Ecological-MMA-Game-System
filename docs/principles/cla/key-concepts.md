<p class="emma-glabel">Constraints</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Constraints are NOT negative limitations.</b> They are "boundaries or features that shape the form of a complex adaptive system searching for functional states of organization." <i>, Newell, 1986</i></p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">📡</div><div class="emma-card__t">Information sources</div><p>They shape behavior rather than block it.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Neutral boundaries</div><p>They guide organization, not punish.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎚️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Configuration reducers</div><p>They narrow possibilities to functional ones.</p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-sub">The three categories of constraints <span style="font-weight:400">(Newell, 1986)</span></p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🧍</div><div class="emma-card__t">Individual (Organismic)</div><p>Personal characteristics unique to each performer.<span class="emma-pop">Vary between AND within each person over different timescales (Renshaw et al., 2019).</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🎯</div><div class="emma-card__t">Task</div><p>Features specific to the activity being performed.<span class="emma-pop">Goals, rules, equipment, spatial and temporal features, the layer the coach manipulates most directly.</span></p></div>

</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Affordances</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>"What the environment offers, provides, or furnishes for an organism"</b>, opportunities for action relative to an individual's capabilities. <i>, Gibson, 1979</i></p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔗</span> Relational<span class="emma-chip__detail">Between individual capabilities and environmental properties, never purely in one or the other.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚡</span> Action-relevant<span class="emma-chip__detail">They specify what can be done, and they solicit behavior, affordances invite action.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">👁️</span> Perceived directly<span class="emma-chip__detail">No complex cognitive processing required, perception detects them, it does not compute them.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚖️</span> Not all functional<span class="emma-chip__detail">Some affordances lead to poor outcomes, "either for good or ill" (Gibson, 1979). Not every available action is a good one.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-sub">Types of affordances in combat sports <span style="font-weight:400">(applied from Hristovski et al., 2006)</span></p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">👊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Strikeability</div><p>When/where to strike.<span class="emma-pop">Open chin, dropped hands, forward lean.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🤼</div><div class="emma-card__t">Takedown-ability</div><p>A takedown opportunity.<span class="emma-pop">Overextension, single-leg position, clinch access.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔒</div><div class="emma-card__t">Submission-ability</div><p>A submission becoming available.<span class="emma-pop">Extended limb, back exposure, neck access.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🚪</div><div class="emma-card__t">Escape-ability</div><p>An escape route.<span class="emma-pop">Hip space, posture breaks, frame creation.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">↩️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Counter-ability</div><p>A counter opportunity.<span class="emma-pop">Telegraphed attack, recovery position.</span></p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-sub">The affordance landscape</p>

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Narrow landscape<br><small>few options, highly structured, prescription-based, directive</small></span><span class="emma-flow__arr">↔</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rich landscape<br><small>many options, unstructured, discovery-based, exploratory</small></span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Coaching implication:</b> move learners across this landscape based on their needs and development stage (Renshaw et al., 2019).</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Shared affordances:</b> "The richest affordances are provided by the behaviors of other people." <i>, Renshaw et al., 2019.</i> Training partners provide dynamic, responsive affordances, requiring intimate knowledge of your own action possibilities, your partner's, and how the two interact moment-to-moment.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Perception-Action Coupling</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>"Information regulates action, and action guides the pickup of information for further behavioral adaptations."</b> <i>, Gibson, 1979.</i> Perception and action are not separate sequential processes, they form a continuous loop mediated by the performer's intentions.</p>

<p class="emma-sub">Information sources in combat sports</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Opponent position, distance, movement.<span class="emma-pop">Reading feints, seeing openings.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Contact pressure, grip tension.<span class="emma-pop">Feeling submission opportunities through grips and frames.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Body position, balance, orientation.<span class="emma-pop">Base awareness during grappling.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Acoustic</div><p>Corner instructions, crowd, impacts.<span class="emma-pop">Adjusting strategy mid-fight.</span></p></div>
</div>

??? abstract "The specific invariants: what actually specifies the affordance"

    Modalities are the *channels*; the information they carry is a set of **higher-order invariants**. Ecological psychology shows perception detects these directly, there is nothing to "compute, " only something to attune to (Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 82). Three named invariants matter most for combat sports:

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⏱️</div><div class="emma-card__t">τ (tau / time-to-contact)</div><p>The relative rate of optical expansion of an object's texture, specifies <i>time-to-arrival</i>, assuming current conditions continue. On a closing strike, shot, or level change the defender attunes to the <b>rate of expansion</b>, <b>not</b> absolute distance or speed. <i>(p. 74, 165–166)</i></p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🌊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Global optic flow</div><p>Self-motion and how to control it, rate of flow = movement speed. The fighter's own closing pressure and cage-edge approach. <i>(p. 147–148)</i></p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Inertial array (inertia tensor)</div><p>For dynamic/effortful touch, resistance to rotation at a grasp point specifies length, orientation, and "off-balance-able-ness" of what is held. Grips, frames, clinch: what the hands <i>feel</i> through contact. <i>(p. 123–127)</i></p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Coaching consequence:</b> "Reading" should mean <i>attuning to a named invariant</i> (τ on a closing punch, the inertial array through a grip), not vaguely "seeing openings." See the <a href="../ecological-psychology/index/">Ecological Psychology</a> section for the full treatment.</p>

<p class="emma-sub">Implications for training <span style="font-weight:400">(Renshaw et al., 2019)</span></p>

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li><b>Keep perception and action coupled</b> during practice.</li>
  <li><b>Avoid task decomposition</b> that separates perception from action.</li>
  <li><b>Use task simplification</b> (scaling) instead of decomposition.</li>
  <li><b>Include relevant information sources</b> in all practice tasks.</li>
</ul>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Example:</b> Shadow boxing separates perception from action (no opponent to read). Pad work with a reactive holder maintains the coupling.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Self-Organization & Coordination</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Self-organization</b> = spontaneous tendencies for system components to form coordinated patterns without external micromanagement (Kelso, 1995). "Spontaneous emergence" is shorthand, the full definition has four load-bearing requirements, <i>all</i> of which must hold.</p>

<ul class="emma-checklist">
  <li><b>Not other-organized</b>, no external leader, blueprint, or rigid hierarchy directing the parts.</li>
  <li><b>Minimally-intelligent components</b>, parts follow simple local rules; no "loan of intelligence" required.</li>
  <li><b>Local rules + constant mutual interaction</b>, rules use only <i>local</i> information; remove the interaction and the pattern stops (metronomes on a non-transmitting surface never synchronize, p. 271–272).</li>
  <li><b>Emergence at a larger scale</b>, a nonrandom pattern comprehensible only at a larger scale than the parts, and not predictable from them.</li>
</ul>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>For MMA:</b> the "components" are the fighter's synergies and the two-fighter dyad; the "local rules" are the game's constraints; the emergent pattern is the fighting style. The coach supplies rules and lets the pattern emerge, they do not choreograph it. <i>(Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 270–272)</i></p>

  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Murmuration of starlings</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">·</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Schooling of fish</span></div>

<p class="emma-sub">Coordination dynamics <span style="font-weight:400">(Turvey, 1990)</span></p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔄</div><div class="emma-card__t">Intra-individual</div><p>Organizing functional relations between body parts to achieve task goals.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤼</div><div class="emma-card__t">Inter-individual</div><p>Organizing body position relative to opponents, surfaces, equipment.</p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-sub">The degrees-of-freedom problem <span style="font-weight:400">(Bernstein, 1967)</span></p>

The human movement system has enormous degrees of freedom that must be organized. Solutions develop in three stages:

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Freezing</b>Lock out degrees of freedom.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Initial stage, a rigid stance, limited hip rotation. Reducing complexity to get the pattern off the ground.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Releasing</b>Gradually free more components.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Intermediate stage, adding hip torque to punches as control develops.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Exploiting</b>Use reactive/passive forces.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Advanced stage, flowing between positions with economy of motion, capitalizing on free forces.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-sub">Synergy formation <span style="font-weight:400">(Turvey, 1990)</span></p>

<p class="emma-hl">A <b>synergy</b> is a temporarily assembled grouping of body segments, muscles, and joints that function as a single unit to achieve a task goal.</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧷</span> Soft-assembled</span>
  <span class="emma-chip"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> Not permanently hardwired</span>
  <span class="emma-chip"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎛️</span> Emerge under constraints</span>
  <span class="emma-chip"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧩</span> Context-dependent</span>
  <span class="emma-chip"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚡</span> Rapidly formed &amp; dissolved</span>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>For MMA:</b> A takedown-defense synergy (sprawl) is assembled when needed and dissolves immediately after, allowing transition to other positions.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Dexterity</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>"The ability to find a motor solution for any external situation"</b>, to solve any emerging motor problem correctly, quickly, rationally, and resourcefully. <i>, Bernstein, 1967</i></p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✅</div><div class="emma-card__t">Correctly</div><p>Adequately and accurately.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⏩</div><div class="emma-card__t">Quickly</div><p>In both decision-making and execution.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">💡</div><div class="emma-card__t">Rationally</div><p>Expediently and economically.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎒</div><div class="emma-card__t">Resourcefully</div><p>Quick-wittedly and with initiative.</p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>For MMA:</b> A dexterous fighter doesn't just know techniques, they find solutions to novel problems in the moment.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Degeneracy</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>"The ability of structurally different elements to perform the same function or yield the same output."</b> <i>, Edelman &amp; Gally, 2001; applied to motor learning by Davids et al., 2008.</i> In MMA: multiple ways to achieve a takedown (single leg, double leg, body lock, trips) from the same start.</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🛡️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Builds robustness</div><p>If one solution is blocked, others exist.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔄</div><div class="emma-card__t">Enables adaptability</div><p>Copes with changing conditions.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔓</div><div class="emma-card__t">Prevents technique capture</div><p>Never stuck with a single solution.</p></div>
</div>

!!! note "Degeneracy ↔ Multirealizability"
    Degeneracy is the same phenomenon ecological psychology calls **multirealizability**, the property of self-organizing systems whereby the *same parts reorganize for many goals*, and *many different organizations achieve the same goal* (your legs can walk, kick, skate, or arabesque; many takedown entries reach the same finish) (Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 274). Building degeneracy in training *is* building a multirealizable system. See the [Ecological Psychology](../ecological-psychology/index.md) section.

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Optimal grip on affordances:</b> "Skilled performance is predicated on having an optimal grip over the field of affordances." <i>, Renshaw et al., 2019.</i> That means perceiving the relevant affordances, knowing which to use and when, executing the appropriate actions, and continuously adapting to dynamic conditions.</p>
