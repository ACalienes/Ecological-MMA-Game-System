# Coaching Implementation Guidelines

<div class="emma-meta"><span>CLA Methodology</span><span>Instruction</span><span>Feedback</span><span>Assessment</span></div>

<p style="font-size:.8rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:-.6rem 0 1.4rem;">← <a href="../mma-applications/">MMA Applications</a> · <a href="../index/">CLA Index</a> · <a href="../pitfalls/">Pitfalls</a> →</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>The coach is an environment architect, not a technique prescriber.</b> You supply local rules and direct attention to information, then let the fighting style self-organize within the constraints you set.</p>

??? abstract "Sources"
    Coaching guidelines and feedback principles from Renshaw et al. (2019). External focus research from Wulf (2013). For complete bibliography, see <a href="../../../reference/sources/">Sources &amp; Theoretical Foundations</a>.

<p class="emma-glabel">The Role Shift</p>

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Technique prescriber</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Environment designer</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Tells athletes what to do</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Creates conditions for emergence</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Corrects movements</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Manipulates constraints</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Source of solutions</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Facilitator of discovery</span></div>
</div>

??? quote "The house-building philosophy: give rules, not a blueprint"
    The single best one-line articulation of the coach's role comes from the self-organizing house-building robots (Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 284): give the contractors <b>a list of rules and let them adapt to local circumstances</b>, rather than a single rigid blueprint, <i>"the houses will be far more adapted to their circumstances… and you (the divine architect) will have to do a lot less work."</i> The coach supplies local rules (constraints); the fighting style self-organizes. (See the <a href="../../ecological-psychology/index/">Ecological Psychology</a> section.)

<p class="emma-glabel">Instruction Guidelines</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✅</div><div class="emma-card__t">Use instructions to</div><p>Direct attention to affordances (not prescribe actions) · frame intentions and goals · provide knowledge ABOUT (to complement knowledge OF) · guide exploration (not replace it).</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🚫</div><div class="emma-card__t">Avoid using them to</div><p>Prescribe exact movements · remove decision-making · direct internal focus of attention · replace experiential learning.</p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">External vs. Internal Focus</p>

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Effect on environment</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">vs</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Body part movement</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Target / outcome</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">vs</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Muscle activation</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Opponent / partner</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">vs</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Joint angles</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Equipment contact</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">vs</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Limb positions</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Example, </b> Internal (avoid): <i>"Rotate your hip when you punch."</i> External (use): <i>"Drive through the target with your fist."</i> External focus promotes automatic, fluid movement; internal focus disrupts natural coordination.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Attention-Directing Cues</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🎯</div><div class="emma-card__t">Target-focused ✅</div><p>"Hit the chin", "Control the wrist".<span class="emma-pop">Good cue, external, outcome-focused. Points attention at what to affect in the world.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">💥</div><div class="emma-card__t">Effect-focused ✅</div><p>"Make them feel your weight", "Move them".<span class="emma-pop">Good cue, names the effect on the opponent, not your body.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">↔️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Distance-focused ✅</div><p>"Close the space", "Create distance".<span class="emma-pop">Good cue, directs attention to the spatial relationship.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🏁</div><div class="emma-card__t">Outcome-focused ✅</div><p>"Score the takedown", "Land clean".<span class="emma-pop">Good cue, frames the goal, leaves the solution open.</span></p></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-warns">
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Body part, "Rotate your hips", "Turn your shoulder"<span class="emma-pop">Poor cue, internal, mechanical. Directs attention inward and disrupts coordination.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Muscle, "Squeeze your glutes", "Engage your core"<span class="emma-pop">Poor cue, internal, mechanical.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Joint, "Bend your elbow to 90 degrees"<span class="emma-pop">Poor cue, internal, mechanical.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-warn emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">Position, "Keep your hands at chin level"<span class="emma-pop">Poor cue, internal, mechanical.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Feedback: Types</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">📋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Knowledge of Results (KR)</div><p>After attempts, outcome information: "That landed", "You escaped".<span class="emma-pop">Tells the athlete <b>what happened</b> as an outcome.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🔍</div><div class="emma-card__t">Knowledge of Performance (KP)</div><p>After attempts, process information: "You read the level change early".<span class="emma-pop">Tells the athlete about the <b>process</b> that produced the outcome.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">⚡</div><div class="emma-card__t">Concurrent</div><p>During performance, real-time cues: "Now!", "There!".<span class="emma-pop">Delivered in the moment to couple attention to information.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">❓</div><div class="emma-card__t">Questions</div><p>After exploration, promote reflection: "What did you notice?".<span class="emma-pop">Builds the athlete's own perception rather than handing them the answer.</span></p></div>
</div>

??? note "Feedback serves *calibration*"
    What feedback (KR/KP, questioning, and especially <i>fading feedback over time</i>) is actually tuning is <b>calibration</b>, the learner's mapping between the detected information and the scaled response (Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 206). Calibration is <i>how to use</i> a perceived invariant correctly (how hard to commit given this τ, how much to redirect given this felt resistance). Reducing feedback over time forces the athlete to own that mapping. Crucially, <b>calibration is action-mode-specific and does not transfer</b> (see Transfer Assessment below). (See the <a href="../../ecological-psychology/index/">Ecological Psychology</a> section.)

<p class="emma-glabel">The Questioning Approach</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">💬</div><div class="emma-card__t">Instead of telling, ask</div><p>"What did you notice about their weight distribution?" · "What options did you see there?" · "What would happen if you…?" · "What made that one work?"</p></div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Observation Framework: What to Watch For</p>

<ol class="emma-checklist">
  <li><b>Search behaviors</b>, is the athlete exploring options?</li>
  <li><b>Solution variety</b>, are multiple techniques emerging?</li>
  <li><b>Reading ability</b>, is the athlete perceiving affordances?</li>
  <li><b>Response timing</b>, are reactions becoming more coupled?</li>
  <li><b>Stability under pressure</b>, do skills hold when challenged?</li>
</ol>

<p class="emma-glabel">Transfer Assessment</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Key question:</b> does training performance transfer to competition? Do not assume, test it.</p>

??? warning "Transfer is mode-specific and fragile: do not assume it"
    The deepest reason to <i>test</i> transfer rather than assume it: ecological psychology shows that calibration and affordance-perception <b>do not transfer across action modes</b> (Blau &amp; Wagman, 2022, p. 160, 206). Infants who learned which slopes afforded safe <i>crawling</i> started over "from zero" when they began <i>walking</i>, the information is entirely different (different limbs, optic flow, base of support). Subjects calibrated to an optic-flow drift while <i>walking</i> did <b>not</b> carry that calibration to <i>throwing or kicking</i>. <b>Implication for our library:</b> a fighter who has learned to read τ for <i>striking</i> defense has <b>not</b> thereby learned to read τ for <i>takedown</i> defense. Build <b>separate learning paths per action mode</b> and <b>verify transfer empirically</b>, this is exactly why phase-scaffolding (striking → clinch → takedown) works: it re-educates attention at each new mode rather than assuming carry-over. (See the <a href="../../ecological-psychology/index/">Ecological Psychology</a> section.)

  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">1 · Compare</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Training behaviors to competition behaviors</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">2 · Assess</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Decision-making quality under pressure</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">3 · Evaluate</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Adaptability to novel situations</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">4 · Track</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Competition outcomes over time</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">5 · Test across modes</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Verify a skill built in one mode appears in another; never assume carry-over</span></div>
</div>

  <span class="emma-warn">Skills appear in training but not competition</span>

<p class="emma-glabel">Managing the Learning Environment</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🛟</div><div class="emma-card__t">Psychological safety</div><p>Failure without judgment, exploration without ridicule, questions without shame, mistakes as information.<span class="emma-pop"><b>Coach behaviors:</b> celebrate exploration not just success · frame failures as data · model curiosity about problems · avoid punitive responses to errors.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🎯</div><div class="emma-card__t">Individualization</div><p>Know each athlete's learning stage, constraints, optimal challenge, feedback response, and history.<span class="emma-pop"><b>Adjust:</b> constraint selection · variability amount · feedback frequency &amp; type · session intensity.</span></p></div>
</div>
