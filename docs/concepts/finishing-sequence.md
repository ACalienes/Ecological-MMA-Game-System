<div class="emma-meta"><span>Tactical</span><span>All Domains</span><span>Foundational</span><span>The Closing Phase</span></div>

<p class="emma-hl">The <b>finishing sequence</b> is the closing phase of a fight, the moves that actually end it. The goal of every exchange before it is to arrive here: a moment where the opponent is compromised and a finish becomes available. The skill is recognizing that moment and taking the finish it affords.</p>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The fight has stages. <br><span class="accent">Know which one you're in.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Like chess: an opening, a mid-board, and a finish. The finishing sequence is its own state, with its own attunement, and it can arrive in an instant.</p>
</div>

!!! quote "On the term"

    We credit UFC referee **John McCarthy** for the framing of the "finishing sequence", the green light that opens once an opponent is hurt or compromised. We adopt the term and build games around reaching it and recognizing it.

<p class="emma-glabel">The Three Stages</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">♟️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Opening</div><p>Probe, engage cautiously, download information.<span class="emma-pop">Enter the fight and read the opponent. Low-risk exchanges that gather information about range, timing, reactions, and weaknesses, without overcommitting.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">⚔️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Mid-board</div><p>Significant exchanges where the damage ratio tilts one way.<span class="emma-pop">Press the engagements where you do more damage than you take. This is where the opponent gets compromised, a wobbled base, a hurt body, a broken posture.</span></p></div>
  <div class="emma-card emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><div class="emma-card__i">🎯</div><div class="emma-card__t">Finishing</div><p>The green light: a finish is now available, take it.<span class="emma-pop">The opponent is compromised and a finish is afforded, a strike on a hurt target, a neck left open, a posted arm to isolate. A different state, with a different attunement.</span></p></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Calm Is Not a Decision, It Is an Attunement</p>

<p class="emma-hl">The common mistake is to <i>see</i> the moment and then <b>react</b>, an adrenaline dump, the berserker, the bull charging in. Over-arousal mis-attunes you: you rush, you lunge, you miss the finish. The fighters who finish cleanly stay <b>calm</b>, not because they summon a mental state and then calculate, but because they are <b>attuned to the right information and not over-aroused</b>. Calm is what lets you keep perceiving while the finish is live.</p>

<div class="emma-flow">
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">The opponent's state crosses a threshold</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Wobbled base, exposed neck, posted hands, broken posture</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">The position's layout changes</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">New finishes open that were not there a moment ago</span></div>
  <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">You perceive the shift and act</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Take the afforded finish, you don't compute a memorized one</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-hl">In ecological terms, the finishing sequence is a <b>nonlinear phase transition</b>. When a control parameter (damage, balance, posture) crosses a critical value, the layout of available finishes reorganizes. The skill is not a calculation, it is <b>educated attention</b>: learning to perceive that the layout has shifted and a finish is now afforded.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">Don't Jump the Guillotine</p>

<p class="emma-hl">A clean example of finishing well versus badly. Many fighters "jump the guillotine", sacrificing position to their back to chase the choke, with no backup if it fails, and end up fatigued in a worse spot. The better expression keeps the finish as a <b>threat</b>: use the guillotine to climb to top mount, where you either get the finish or keep a dominant position. One commits to a single prescribed solution; the other stays attuned to the evolving layout and takes the better afforded path.</p>

<div class="emma-cards">
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">❌</div><div class="emma-card__t">Jumping the finish</div><p>Commit everything to one move, sacrifice position, no backup. If it fails, you are worse off and gassed.</p></div>
  <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✅</div><div class="emma-card__t">Threatening the finish</div><p>Use the threat to improve position. Stay attuned to what opens next, finish or dominate, never gamble the position away.</p></div>
</div>
