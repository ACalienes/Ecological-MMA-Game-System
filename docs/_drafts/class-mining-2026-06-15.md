## Class structure (6 game blocks + sparring)

### Block 1 (00:00-10:41): Close the distance vs the lead-leg barrier (offense has NO weapons)
- **Offense:** no strikes at all. Only hands to block/catch kicks + timing to close and clinch. Win = get past the elbows into a clinch, any 50/50, both hands under, OR connect on a leg (clarified mid-round at 07:12, "connect on the leg is just as valuable").
- **Defense:** maintain distance with the lead leg (front kick / side kick / teep, "no rule on direction, just in front") + hands. Win = stay out / keep them off.
- **Threshold:** 20-second shot clock on offense ("connect or you lose," 06:02). Winner stays in, switch roles.
- **Connection law:** "past the elbows" = clinch (consistent with the above-the-knee / hand-connection family). 50/50 or both-hands-under preferred but past-elbows counts.
- Maps to: **Clinch Denial** (defense side) / **Pressure to Clinch** (offense side), but a distinct constraint set. The no-weapons-offense start is novel and is the scaffold for total beginners.

### Block 2 (10:41-20:32): Same game, defense gains the lead jab; offense may reach to manipulate
- Defense adds **lead jab** (straight only, can switch stance) on top of teeps. Offense still has no weapons but may **use hands to reach / post / manipulate / destabilize** (not as a strike). Trade-off taught explicitly: reaching exposes you to getting hit (13:55-14:09).
- Light face contact, with shoulder/upper-chest as the opt-out target (14:50-15:07).
- Design signal: textbook progressive constraint layering.

### Block 3 (20:32-33:08): "Winning the Circle" + offense gains the rear-hand cross
- **Major concept teach: Winning the Circle** (22:27-24:21). Control the edge, herd the opponent into the enclosure, take your time, do not chase. Bull / matador / soldier metaphor (28:08-). This recurs all through sparring ("win the circle, Roy").
- Offense now has the **rear straight (cross) only** (head or body, straight, no hooks/uppercuts). Still wins by connection/clinch, not by the strike. Cross is a threat to herd, not the win.
- Defense keeps lead jab + teeps and now **can also win by connecting on a leg** (level-change catch while offense throws the cross).
- **Feint emphasis** (33:54-34:26): "feints unlock your doors," feint top and bottom, pump-fake 6-7x in a row.
- Services Vince's car-audio rear-hand prescription directly.

> **Blocks 1-3 are one game on a 3-rung ladder:** offense weapons escalate (none → none+reach → rear cross) while defense tools escalate (lead leg → +jab → +leg-catch). Clean self-scaling progression, same shape as the Win Ladder format. Naming/curation candidate.

### Block 4 (33:08-44:11): Mount Maintenance vs Mount Escape (shins under the arms)
- **Start:** top mounted, shins under the bottom's arms; bottom's elbows accessible.
- **Top wins:** a **"TKO pin"** = immobilize + land realistic one-handed strikes (4-5 unanswered, 42:48), OR a submission. Must stay topside, "no dropping back to the legs" (35:15-36:21). Taught the control-vs-strike tradeoff: 2 arms holding = better control but can't strike; striking drops you to one point of control and gives them four to off-balance with (35:40-36:04).
- **Bottom wins:** get both elbows back inside in front (→ half guard is fine, 36:56), or reverse.
- **GnP realism rule (37:13-37:54):** not pitter-patter, not bashing. Show a real strike with space behind it, as a negative stimulus and a realistic counter trigger. Good rule-text language for the GnP chip.
- Coaching: pinch the hips / get heavy (40:43-41:20), control one side and get the arms high so they can't bump.
- **This is game A1 Mount Maintenance, validated.** Note the win condition Alex used is **GnP-first (4-5 unanswered strikes)**, NOT the spec's "expose+extend an elbow, 3s." This should update the A1 spec.
- "Winner stays in for the triangle" (37:57): triangle floated as the next-level sub.

### Block 5 (44:11-53:44): Same, arms fully compromised (both arms pinned overhead)
- Only change: start with both of the bottom's **arms controlled overhead** (elbows above the head, 45:35-46:00). Same win conditions.
- This is the **armbar-entry level / "hold them down, arms isolated"** rung of A1 (and the E1 mount-armbar start). So Blocks 4-5 together = L1 → the arm-isolation level of the same page.
- Coaching: be proactive not reactive on top (51:20-52:31); on bottom, be opportunistic, wait for them to lift weight to strike, then spend energy (53:11-53:44).

### Block 6 (53:44-65:03): Standing grounding game from body-lock + chest-to-back ("the standard")
- **Two starts:** body lock, and chest-to-back (standing back control).
- **Offense goal:** put the partner's hips to the ground (one side or both), held **1-2s to stop the scramble** (55:55). Tools: feet-to-feet, head as a fulcrum, off-balancing. From the body lock you may switch to a single leg (no need to keep the lock).
- **Defense wins:** break grips / separate, off-balance to break the grip, OR a top neck strangle (guillotine / "ninja") that submits. Strikes on. Defender may set their overhooks/whizzer **before "go"** (gets to secure defensive posture first, 55:23-55:38).
- **Rules:** no falling body weight. **Turtle is NOT a win** (54:21-54:25, restated 654). Hips down one or both sides. Exception: belly-down grapevine = hips down. **Redemption shot:** if they break off and you re-connect (e.g. re-shoot a single), it is on them to fully separate (56:05-56:21). Mirrors the turtle-breakout redemption rule.
- **Alex: "that's a standard game, we're going to play that one a lot" (65:11).** Strong canonical signal.
- Maps to: a standing **grounding / put-them-down** game (Back Maintenance A3 territory for the chest-to-back start, plus an open-space body-lock takedown). Same mechanic family as 6/06's wall single-leg ladder, but center-mat from body-lock/back. **Decide:** new standalone page vs Back Maintenance variant vs Wall-to-Ground sibling.

## Reusable coaching gold (for concept pages + cue backports)

1. **Winning the Circle** (22:27-24:21, recurs all class). Full articulation + bull/matador/soldier metaphor. Candidate concept page; enrich pressure-to-wall / pressure-to-clinch.
2. **The 4 ways to defend** (81:55-83:14): block, parry (straights only), evade (not be there OR be too close), and **encroach** on his offense (range denial). Clean taxonomy, taught to the beginner. Maps to close-range-defense / evade-the-punch concept copy.
3. **Feint volume** (33:54-34:26, 79:59-81:06): pump-fake 6-7x, feint top and bottom, "feints unlock your doors," feinting to break a read-able forward-motion pattern and open body shots. Ties to the missing **Feint and Commit** striking-depth game.
4. **Offense must not hold and wait** (65:36-66:13): holding a grip and waiting loses, "especially against someone stronger." Off-balance immediately, move side to side. Directly answers the Ronald-vs-stronger-partner pattern.
5. **Get their hands to the mat** (66:19-67:08): if their hands are posting to hold them up, they are not fighting your hands. (Credited to Carl Michael & Kianush's jiu-jitsu classes.)
6. **Fight for position before "go"** (67:13-68:24): arm-wrestler analogy, lock into your strongest position before the start, whizzer early to kill a body lock.
7. **Scaffolding rule** (68:24-68:46): start in the slightly-disadvantaged spot UNLESS there is a skill/power discrepancy, then scaffold. "If you feel 50/50 or the underdog, take as much claim as you can." Ties to the Win Ladder / handicap format.
8. **GnP realism** (37:13-37:54): see Block 4. Refines the GnP dial-up chip text.
9. **Old-school hand-fight escapes** (69:33-72:57): cigar-cutter / cigar-splitter thumb-split down the grip line, hip-as-blade ("surfer / cowabunga" position) so you are not parallel and can't be dragged straight back, turn-and-face, keep one hand back to stop the re-shot. Rich escape-side content for Back Escape / Turtle Survival / a chest-to-back standing escape.
