## 2. Where the heaviest gaps are (priority-ranked)

Ranked by *paying-user expectation* (what an MMA curriculum buyer expects to find) and *structural completeness* (filling a category where we currently have zero).

### HIGH priority — fill these first

1. **Per-position GROUND grid** (F+G+H+I+J ≈ **22 games**). Bottom escape side is 3/6 done (good momentum). Top control side, submission offense, submission defense are zero — biggest single gap in the product. Ground-wave spec already exists for these (14 PASS, 8 CONDITIONAL in pressure-test). **The big one.**
2. **Typed center CLINCH games** (D, 7 games). 50/50 definition now locked. Over-under battle, body-lock entry, collar-tie control, underhook battle, pummel, clinch knees/elbows, clinch break. Zero coverage today. **Top-3 hole.**
3. **Open-space striking depth** (C, 5 left). The eco-psych-named games (two-range/kendo, southpaw-vs-orthodox, feint-and-commit) are explicitly missing. One-shot-discovery covers a feint/perimeter slice but doesn't replace these.
4. **Integration / full-MMA games** (M, 5 left). What differentiates an MMA product from a striking-or-grappling one. Strike-takedown-pass chain, sprawl-and-brawl, ground-and-pound defense (built), cage-cut to finish, submission-threat-to-strike, three-phase round.

### MED priority

5. **Skill-isolation defenses** (B, 6 left). Check the kick, roll under the hook, footwork-only evasion — cheap to build, fills clear stub-categories.
6. **Wall per-position depth** (E, 6 left). Generic wall games covered; back-to-wall escape, wall takedown entry, wall sub offense missing.
7. **Open-space counters** (L, 4 left). Counter-the-kick, intercept-the-shot, counter-the-clinch-entry, counter-the-pressure.
8. **Transitions per-pair** (K, 5 left). Ground→wall reverse, knockdown follow, sprawl-to-top, takedown-to-pass, wall-to-open disengage.

### LOW priority

9. **Skill-isolation weapons** (A, 5 left). Elbow, knee, clinch-strikes, lead-vs-rear-read, southpaw weapon. Completeness items.

## 5. What we have density on (not gaps; existing strengths)

- Skill-isolation defense + offense (weapons split is clean: 4 defensive + 4 offensive).
- Open-space striking foundation (close-range defense, evasive movement, counter-striking, land-the-target, sustained-offense + touch-game + pressure-to-clinch). 7 games here is solid.
- Wall game baseline (6 games covering the position, even if per-position depth is missing).
- Bottom-side ground escapes (now 6 games: ground-escape, mount-escape, side-control-escape, back-escape, leg-reclaim, ground-to-standing).

This means: a buyer picking up the product today would already see real density in striking + wall + ground-bottom. The gaps are in clinch (entirely missing), ground top-side, submissions, and full-MMA integration.

## 6. Recommended invention sequence (next 4–6 weeks of design work)

Based on (HIGH priority) + (already partially built) + (dependency order):

1. **Curation pass first** — synthesize one-shot-discovery + 2026-05-31 combo-reset iterations into the canonical version(s). Decision: 1 page with 10 levels, 2 sister pages, or a discrete-exchange family. ← **before any new striking pages.**
2. **Concept pages from class mining** — "every exchange is a result" + "non-scoring deny tools" + "pre-loaded counters." These plug into many future games as referenced concepts. Build them once, link them everywhere.
3. **Ground top-side wave** (G + I, ≈ 11 games): mount maintenance, side ride, back maintenance, KOB, NS, mount sub hunt, back finish, guard sub hunt, side sub, guillotine series, leg-ent. Pressure-tested 14 PASS / 8 CONDITIONAL in the ground-wave spec. **The biggest single push toward 100+.**
4. **Typed clinch wave** (D, 7 games): build using the locked 50/50 def. Could be paralleled with the ground wave since they're domain-independent.
5. **Skill-isolation defenses + open-space depth** (B + remaining C, 11 games): cheap, no safety load, fills eco-psych-named games.
6. **Submission defense + safety review on leglocks** (J, 3 games): needs Alex's domain pass first.
7. **Wall per-position + integration + counters + transitions** (E + M + L + K, ≈ 20 games): the long tail.
8. **Skill-isolation weapons** (A, 5 games): completeness, can ship last.

By the end of (3) + (4) we're at ~56 live games. By the end of (5) + (6) we're at ~70. (7) + (8) takes us to ~95. Plus new design-pattern items + family pages + the Atlas itself = comfortably 100+ deliverables.
