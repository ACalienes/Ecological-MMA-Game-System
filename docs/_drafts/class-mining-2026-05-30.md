## G1 — One-Shot Discovery [WIP, warm-up]

**Coach intent (Alex):**
- Start: 2-person (or 3-person rotation: top / bottom / out, switch halfway). Inside a marked rectangular perimeter — *tight* space ("close perimeter").
- Offense: land ONE shot with any single weapon (lead hand / rear hand / lead leg / rear leg). NO combinations. Feints allowed.
- Defense: full defensive shell — close blocking, parry, head-movement evasion. Hand controls allowed.
- **Win/Loss:**
  - Offense lands → keeps offense.
  - Defense defends successfully → role switch.
  - Step out of perimeter → loss.
- **Refinement on second play:** *"if you have landed one shot, whatever it is, it cannot be thrown again. You have to come up with something different. At least one cycle."* Forces tool variation.

**Student reception:** *"a little bit harder in tight space, but I really like the drill. I think everything worked."*

**Live adjustment:** Alex called out *"I didn't see anybody fake shot"* — pushed feints harder, demoed back-fist / leg-stomp feints as legitimate setups.

**Divergence flag:** None major. Win conditions stated clearly, perimeter enforced verbally, threshold = "lands significantly" (judgment call — not a count).

**Maps to:** New game. Closest existing: `counter-striking` (defense) + `land-the-target` (offense) but neither has the role-switch-on-success + perimeter loss + weapon-cycling combo. **Fills coverage-map gap C #15 ("Range Management / Long Game") loosely; closer to a hybrid of #12 (Feint and Commit) + #17 (Pressure vs Lateral) but distinct.**

## G2 — One-Shot + Clinch Permission [WIP, layer-on]

**Coach intent:** Same as G1 PLUS:
- Defender allowed to **clinch** (and hand-control) as a defensive tool.
- *"Clinch at your own peril"* — defender can be hit on the way in.
- Perimeter enforced more strictly: **both feet out = loss.**
- **Only defense can grapple** — offense throws strikes only; defender's clinch is a defensive escape, not an offensive tool.
- If offense lands BEFORE clinch closes → offense wins, reset.

**Live adjustment:** When a student tried clinching offensively, Alex corrected: *"If he lands a punch on you before you get to clinching, then he won. You would reset."*

**Maps to:** Variant / level. Could be **Level 3 or 4 of G1's page**, or a standalone variant. Aligns with coverage-map C #17 (Pressure vs Lateral) but with the clinch-as-defense twist that's distinctly Alex.

## G3 — One-Shot + Counter [WIP, refinement]

**Coach intent:** Same as G2 PLUS:
- Defender ALSO wins by landing a **single counter shot** that connects, OR by **connecting on legs** (around one or both legs **under the waist** — no body lock).
- **Feints are now "an invitation to counter"** — defender can counter off a feint, no physical contact required.
- Defender allowed to **strike to break hand controls.**
- **Counter MUST LAND.** If defender's counter misses, they lose.

**Student question (good signal):** *"Can the counter be like a two-piece? My first one misses but then my second one lands, or you just want one?"* — Alex: *"Focus on one — landing your first shot that is a successful counter… we'll build up."* Holds the skill-isolation line.

**Maps to:** This is the cleanest game of the day. Single asymmetric start (offense + defense roles, perimeter, weapon menu), 3 observable terminal wins (offense lands / defense lands counter / defense closes on legs), perimeter loss. Hits R1–R6 cleanly. **Strong candidate for a new canonical page.**

## G5 — Open-Camped Top vs Scramble-Up Bottom [WIP, ground integration]

**Coach intent:**
- Start: Bottom supine. Top past the legs but **NOT closed in** — "camping off to the side, past the hips, not really past the knees." **No chest-to-chest, no knee-on-belly, no closing the position.**
- Top role: Stay there, land **significant ground-and-pound** (academic for class, mimics real GnP). Top **CANNOT WIN** — *"top player cannot win."* Continuous top, terminal bottom.
- Bottom role: Win by ANY of three observable terminal events:
  1. **Stand up cleanly** — full separation. If top connects on a leg/hip on the way up, NOT over.
  2. **Reverse position** — bottom gets to top, bottom stands and is over.
  3. **Close a loop with the legs around top's head and arm** — *"you don't have to finish the submission, you just got to close the loop."*

**Student question:** *"Top player can't win?"* — Alex: *"Top player cannot win. It's just a matter of staying there and doing as much damage as you possibly can from that limitation."*

**Live adjustment:** Alex emphasized top must stay OPEN (no closing distance, no body lock). Maintains the "open camped" constraint as the defining feature.

**Divergence flag:** Alex used **"as long as possible"** for the top role. **This is the exact threshold-house-rule gap.** When converted to a canonical page, we'd add a 20s clock OR score it as "most cumulative-time camped" or similar. Alex would need to land on a number.

**Maps to:** **Coverage map M #67 "Ground-and-Pound Defense"** (full-MMA cross-domain integration). NOT one of the 22 ground-wave games. This game **fills a distinct, high-value gap** — the open-top GnP-vs-scramble scenario — and is **build-worthy as a new page** after Alex's review. Closest existing: `ground-to-standing` (similar bottom escape paths) but that doesn't carry the open-top GnP constraint.
