## G1 — Speed Warm-up [WIP]

**Coach intent:**
- Start: 2 (or 3-rotation) inside the rectangular perimeter, mirrored roles.
- Both players accruing points by landing first hand or first foot. All weapons on. Single shots, feints + level-change feints allowed. No combos.
- *"Game is mirrored. You're just accruing points… don't let someone land their shot."*
- Defense: close blocking, parrying, head movement, footwork, hand controls. No clinching.
- Win: be first. Speed-focused, **not counter-focused** ("we can counter, but the point is just to be first").
- Loss: both feet out of perimeter.

**Maps to:** Calibration / range-and-timing warm-up. Closest existing: `touch-game` (mirrored, range awareness) but with all-weapons + feints + perimeter, this is a sharper variant. **Could be a Level 0 / warm-up rung of `one-shot-discovery`**, or a touch-game variant in its own right.

## G2 — Four-Shot Combo, Defense Wins by Clean Block [WIP]

**Coach intent:**
- Start: one offense, one defense (3-person rotation: top / bottom / out). Tight perimeter, both feet out = loss.
- Offense: throws up to a **four-strike combination** (hands + feet). Must land at least one in the combo to win.
- Defense: full shell only. **No counter at this level.** Successful block of the entire combo = role switch.
- Critical rule: *"every exchange is a result."* The committed combination is one chamber. You don't get to throw one punch and save the other three for later. Use your bullets or lose them. Then reset cleanly.
- Loss: combo lands → offense keeps going. Combo missed → defense earns offense.
- Hand controls on. No clinching.

**The doctrine Alex named:** *"these games are micro games… we need to acknowledge quickly. It's a little mental journal. Yep, I got it. Or nope, I didn't and I need to understand why. Reset. Go back into it."*

**Live adjustment from class:** he had to repeat the "every exchange is a result" rule several times — students kept rolling combos into combos. Big enough teaching point that he gave the football-play vs continuous-scramble analogy: *"you're not playing football play after play non-stop. You stop, you run a play, everybody gets ready."*

**Maps to:** New page or new Level of `one-shot-discovery`. The combo+reset structure is **the defining feature**, not just an addition. Calling it a separate game is defensible.

## G3 — Add Counter (Any Single Strike) [WIP]

**Coach intent:**
- Same as G2 PLUS: defender can also win by landing a clean **single counter strike** (any weapon, any side).
- **Scoring split:** block alone = 1 point + switch. Block + counter = 5 points + switch.
- **Variety constraint:** if a counter works, you can't use the same counter in the next exchange. Forces a menu.
- **Feints become counter openings:** *"a feint is an invitation for violence."* Defender can counter off a feint without waiting for physical contact.
- Counter must LAND (not just be thrown). Missed counter = defender loses that exchange.

**Maps to:** Direct evolution of G2 with the counter layer. Already-paralleled in `one-shot-discovery` L4 (counter must land) but the **point-weighted block-vs-counter scoring** is a new design lever. Worth capturing.

---

## G4 — Two-Strike Counter, Opposite Sides [WIP]

**Coach intent:**
- Same as G3 BUT defender's counter must be TWO consecutive strikes from **opposite sides of the body** (left then right, or right then left). Both must land.
- Can use legs. Can mix hand + foot. Just must be cross-body.
- *"If I counter with a kick here, I need to have a strike on this side."*

**Why:** trains the cross-body chain — *"when you start with one side and finish with the next, you get an extra bit of range and power that comes with that second shot."* Pre-loaded knowledge: lead-rear or rear-lead, both directions develop.

**Maps to:** Skill-isolation layer of the counter mechanic. Could be Level 5+ of the same page, OR a sub-game ("Cross-Body Counter").

---

## G5 — Two-Strike Counter, Must End With a Kick [WIP]

**Coach intent:**
- Same as G4 BUT the **last shot of the two-strike counter must be a kick** (opposite side still).
- The kick is the one that must land. The setup strike is the bait, fine if it misses.
- *"All we're doing now is switching to an isolation of one specific two-strike counter."*

**Maps to:** Pure skill-isolation Level. Forces the kick into the counter menu, since open-space games under-represent it. This mirrors the 2026-05-30 G4 ("body-kick-only counter") but with the setup-strike requirement.

**Live adjustment:** lots of confusion in this game. Students kept blocking-only and trying to win. Alex restated the rule mid-game then re-explained at the end.

---

## G6 — Counter Is the Only Win Path + Defender Gets a Jab [WIP, biggest insight]

**Coach intent:**
- Same as G5 BUT:
  - **Block alone no longer wins.** Defense MUST counter (kick-finished, opposite-side) to win.
  - **Defender gets a jab** they can pump at any time — but the **jab does not score**. It's a "keep them away" / "manage range" tool only.
- The kick-finished counter is the only win-path on defense; the jab keeps the offense honest.

**This is the design-lever insight worth capturing:** Alex gave the defender a **non-scoring tool** to deny without rewarding. *"You cannot win on defense by jabbing. You have to win by countering and finishing with the kick just like before. But you have something to keep their attention."*

That's a constraint pattern we don't have explicit in the library yet: a **deny-without-score weapon**, distinct from win-paths and from prohibited actions. Worth a concept page on its own ("Deny vs Score Weapons" or similar).

**Closing teach-moment (the coaching cue worth saving):**
*"When something happens, I will decide to defend and then decide to counter — I should have this completely pre-meditated. Roy's gonna throw something at me and whatever he does, boom boom, that's working for me. Lead hook, chop to the leg. It worked. You're not even worried about landing the lead hook."*

The point: the defender shouldn't *read then decide* their counter. They should have **pre-loaded counter defaults** that fire automatically off any offense, then iterate when the opponent reads them.

## Cross-check vs library + spec (what this validates and what it adds)

**Method-level validation (everything we already encoded held):**
- ✓ Asymmetric starts every game (offense / defense role split).
- ✓ Observable wins ("clean combo," "two-strike counter ending kick," "1 pt block, 5 pt counter").
- ✓ Skill isolation by added constraint = a Level, not a new game.
- ✓ Perimeter as loss.
- ✓ Role switch on success.
- ✓ Counter must LAND (same standard both sides).
- ✓ Feints as counter openings (re-emphasized).

**New patterns this class added to our toolkit:**

1. **"Every exchange is a result" doctrine.** A class of games is **discrete (football-play)**, not continuous (sparring). Each exchange resolves, then a reset, then another exchange. Worth a **concept page** because it applies across many games (one-shot-discovery, the combo ladder, the GnP defense, more).
2. **Point-weighted win paths.** Block = 1, Block + Counter = 5. Lets multiple win conditions coexist with a clear ordering, while still preserving the "either side can win" structure.
3. **Variety constraint** (no repeat counter in the next exchange). Forces menu development without inventing N separate games.
4. **Non-scoring tool on defense.** The jab in G6 — deny without score. Distinct from win-paths and from prohibited actions. **Design lever we should add to our vocabulary.**
5. **Pre-loaded counter doctrine.** The defender doesn't read-then-decide; they have defaults that fire off any offense. Coaching philosophy worth a concept-page or principle entry.
