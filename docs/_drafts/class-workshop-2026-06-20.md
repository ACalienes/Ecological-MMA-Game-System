## DOCTRINES

### D1. The Finishing Sequence (credit: John McCarthy)

**Mat version:** the goal of MMA is to reach a finishing sequence (chess: opening → mid-board → finish). Recognize the moment, switch gears, finish from a place of **calm / flow state**, "calculate" the swiftest finish.

**Eco cross-check — this is the one genuine CONFLICT of the day.** The "establish calm, then *calculate* how to end it" framing reintroduces the **central executive** that ecological psychology explicitly rejects (audit §1.2, direct perception / no internal computation). Eco-faithful reframe:

- Calm is not a mental state you *summon and then compute from*. It is what **emerges** when you are attuned to the right information and not over-aroused (the bull/berserker is over-aroused → mis-attuned).
- The "finishing sequence" is a **nonlinear phase transition** (rule 12): when the opponent's state crosses a critical threshold (wobbled base, exposed neck, posted hands), the **attractor layout changes and new affordances open**. McCarthy's "green light" = a *perceived affordance*, not a decision computed in the head.
- The skill is **educating attention** to the compromise-invariants and acting on the newly-afforded finish (rule 13: intention → attention → calibrate), NOT "calculating."
- The guillotine example proves the point: "jumping the ghillie" = committing to one *prescribed* solution (anti-rule-2); "using the guillotine *threat* to climb to top mount" = staying attuned to the *evolving* affordance layout and taking the preferred/economical finish (rules 2, 6).

**Verdict:** keep the concept (great organizing idea, names the #1 gap's *why*), but **rewrite the language** from "calm → calculate" to "attune → the layout shifts → take the afforded finish." Build as a referenced concept page; credit McCarthy. This doctrine is what **Game 2 operationalizes**.

### D2. Bull · Soldier · Matador

**Mat version:** three pressure archetypes — Bull (reckless relentless pressure, gets countered/taken down), Soldier (methodical pressure, takes space with probes/feints, walks you to the perimeter), Matador (retreating counter-fighter, uses footwork + your momentum). Two non-conceding forward movers = a center slugfest.

**Eco cross-check:** these are **emergent dyad roles / attractor states**, not personalities. They map cleanly onto rule 15 (read what the opponent's posture affords *them*) and rule 16 (the soldier walking you to the dead-middle/perimeter). The Bull is a fighter stuck in a *shallow attractor* that good noise/counters punish (rule 11).

- **Tension:** risk of becoming a "pick your fighter type" identity (prescriptive) rather than a behavior that emerges from the constraint configuration (who's getting walked to the boundary). Keep them as **opponent-reading vocabulary**, not a prescription to "be a matador."

**Verdict:** keep as a glossary/concept reading-aid. Low risk. Concept page or glossary entry.

### Reusable conventions banked (formalize as named house rules)

- **"Shown strike / legitimate threat without violence."** Blunted, arc'd strikes that produce a *real positional reaction* without landing clean; **no elbows/knees without pads**; Thai-style blunted knees (inside of thigh, not the point). This already appears in 3+ ground/clinch games (body-lock-to-ground, the GnP chip, Game 1, Game 2). It is rule 18 (train with consequence) + rule 19 (information into the environment): the strike threat is the constraint that makes ground/clinch positions *urgent and representative*. **Make it a glossary entry + named rule** like the threshold rule, referenced everywhere.
- **Finger-curl safety note** (from Sebastian's finger injury): open splayed fingers are vulnerable + foul-prone; cue a soft curl. Add to the safety chip vocabulary.

## GAME 1 — 50/50 Standing Clinch (typed center clinch)

**Mat version:** start in a 50/50 clinch (one overhook + one underhook, diagonally matched, chest-to-chest), symmetric/neutral, standing. Tools: hands grapple, feet antagonize, blunted Thai knees + punches to antagonize, **no elbows**. Win by: connect double-unders (hands locked) · connect hand-to-leg · off-balance to the ground · (gold star) get to the back. **Reset on any connection.** Wall available but start neutral.

**Cross-check:**
- Rule 2 (afford, don't prescribe): **exemplary** — four self-selected win paths, the player self-organizes. Textbook CLA game.
- Rule 4 (name the invariant): need a "What to Read" block → the **inertial array / felt load through the ties** (which way the partner's weight is committed) + their **posted base**. Haptic invariant.
- Rules 5, 6, 7, 8: all satisfied (realized-affordance wins; preferred-solution gradient with the back as gold-star; fully live; off-balance = free forces / controlled falling).
- **Glossary collision:** the mat language was "mirrored game / mirrored position." The locked 50/50 definition says **never "mirror"** (mirror implies same-side; the grips are *diagonally matched*). The page MUST say "50/50, diagonally matched, symmetric/neutral."
- **Hand connection law:** "connect to a leg" must be specified as **above the knee** (2026-06-06 law; no diving low singles).
- Mechanical: needs an explicit **perimeter** (E1) — the wall was ambient on the mat; mark it live.

**Run it (playtest):**
- **Degenerate strategy — mutual stalling.** This *actually happened* in class (players "too defensive," no attack plan). With reset-on-any-connection and no passivity cost, two defensive players stall. Alex's mat fix was a *cue* ("flush your offense") — but rule 19 says fix it with a **constraint**, not instruction. The fix already exists in the toolset: **strikes are the stalling tax.** A passive player eats antagonizing blunted knees/strikes. Spec it explicitly: the "antagonize" tools are the anti-stall constraint, not decoration.
- **Edge: simultaneous connection** (both grab at once) → tiebreak/reset rule needed.
- **Edge: off-balance where both go down** → borrow body-lock's rule: an off-balance only scores if **you stay standing / end on top.**

**Fixes:** explicit perimeter · name the haptic invariant · "diagonally matched" not "mirror" · above-knee leg connection · strikes-as-anti-stall constraint · simultaneous-connection + must-stay-standing tiebreaks.

**Verdict: ~85% there. Build after minor fixes.** Our **first true typed center-clinch game** (gap #2). Library synergy: its "off-balance to ground" feeds Game 4; its 50/50 start is the reset target of Game 4's defender win.

## GAME 2 — Mount: Arm-Over-the-Head  ( = "Mount Submission Hunt", the flagged one)

**Mat version:** offense fully mounted; **defense sets their arm position first** (not pre-pinned). Offense wins by getting one arm **fully above the head, hands connected** ("bicep to ear" = arm-triangle/armbar entry), or by taking the back **while on top**. Defense wins by recovering guard (one leg in), reversing, or standing. Strikes are **shown** (legitimate threat, no damage). Alex flagged: "this game has holes... unclear back-take rule," and players reported **fatigue**.

This is not a new "mount" game in isolation — we already have `mount-escape` (bottom) and `mount-maintenance` (top hold). The **novel** element is the **observable submission-entry win** (arm isolated overhead). That makes it the **finish layer of the mount ladder** (escape → maintain → **hunt**) and exactly coverage-map item #47, **Mount Submission Hunt** — the #1 gap (submission offense, previously zero).

**The holes, worked:**

- **HOLE 1 — back-take ambiguity (you flagged it).** "Take the back wins" is ambiguous when the bottom player rolls and the *top* ends up with back control but **back-to-the-floor**. Your mat ruling was right and it's eco-correct (rule 5: reward a *realized, stable* dominant attractor). **Fix:** back-take counts only as an **observable seal** — chest-to-back, **both hooks in, on top / shoulders controlled, held 1–2s** (same observable-proof pattern as body-lock's "hips down 1–2s," and consistent with our back-maintenance definition). Removes the live refereeing disputes heard on the mat ("was it the left arm?").

- **HOLE 2 — fatigue / no reachable attractor.** Both win conditions (top: hard sub-entry; bottom: full escape/reverse/stand vs a secured mount) are *high-effort against an equal*, so the round grinds to a gassed stalemate (rule 6 is about action-scaling to effort; a game with no reachable attractor is a *design* failure, not productive noise). **Fix:** give the bottom player a **reachable primary win** — **deny + recover**: keep both arms framed/connected (deny the over-the-head isolation) and get **one leg back in** (= mount-escape's win). Full reversal/stand stays as a bonus. Plus treat **fatigue as a control parameter** (rule 10): **short rounds, frequent resets** — the finish-hunt is high-intensity by design.

- **HOLE 3 — observability of the win.** "Bicep to the ear, hands locked" is a crisp proxy (you coached it live), but add the **1–2s hold** so a momentary brush doesn't count.

- **HOLE 4 — the "shown strike" rule** (see Doctrines) is load-bearing here: the strike threat is what opens the frame so the arm becomes available. Formalize and reference it. Without it, mount has no urgency and the bottom player can lounge (kills representativeness, rule 19).

**Cross-check:** opponent ✓; invariant = **felt load / where the bottom player's frames are committed** (haptic) + **the exposed elbow/arm** (visual); afford-don't-prescribe ✓ (many ways to free the arm: shown-strike opens the frame → arm available → isolate). This game is the **operational form of the Finishing Sequence doctrine** — a perception-action finishing loop, not a memorized submission (rule 13). That's its real value.

**Verdict: highest-value game of the day for the #1 gap, but needs your workshop call** on the reframe (mount-ladder finish layer) + the deny-and-recover bottom win. Build after that decision.

## GAME 3 — Kick Defense Isolation (+ jab variant)

**Mat version:** kicker vs defender. Kicker lands sequential roundhouses (leg/body; head only with control), **one at a time, no spam**; continues until the defender successfully defends. Defender wins by **check** (shin, above ankle / below knee), **block** (two contact points), or **catch + keep** (gold star). Variant: kicker gains a **lead jab** (probe, no points, sets up kicks); defender may **counter / return fire** (extra). **No head kicks** (nerf), no calf kicks.

**Cross-check — high-credibility eco content:**
- Rule 4 (invariant): this is **τ (time-to-contact)** — the textbook application named in the audit. "What to Read" = **τ of the rising shin** + the **hip/shoulder pre-rotation** that specifies which kick is coming. Naming τ here is exactly what rule 4 asks for.
- **Rule 14 (no transfer across action modes) — beautifully illustrated on the mat.** You coached a jiu-jitsu/karate-based player who *can't check from his stance* ("you're fighting like this, the target's clear") — his grappling attunement **does not transfer**; he must re-educate attention and re-calibrate his stance. The isolation game **is** the eco-justified per-mode learning path.
- **Is "isolation" a decomposition (rule 7 violation)?** No — it's task *simplification* (scale down the option space) with the **loop intact** (live adaptive kicker using feints, live defender). Keep the kicker **adversarial**, not a cooperative feed, or it becomes a decomposed drill. The jab variant re-adds option-space (rule 9, repeat the problem with more variability) — good progression.
- Rule 6 (preferred + risk): the **catch-vs-block risk tradeoff** ("don't reach for every catch, you'll get faked to the head") is rule 6 — reward the safe block unless the catch is genuinely there.

**Tensions / fixes:**
- **House threshold violation:** the mat framing "land kicks **as long as they can**" trips the banned-phrase rule (T). **Reword:** "lands kicks until the defender successfully defends" (which is the actual rule).
- **The no-head-kick nerf — a real eco tension you spotted yourself** ("doesn't mean we should train it poorly"). Nerfing head kicks makes the catch *artificially safe* and removes the very information that calibrates the catch decision (rules 14, 18 — calibration is specific and won't transfer to a context where head kicks exist). **Eco-honest resolution:** the no-head-kick version is the **coordination stage** (assemble the check/catch synergy safely); add a **mastery-stage level that re-introduces the head-kick threat** (with control/gear) to **calibrate the catch's real risk.** That converts a hidden flaw into an explicit progression.
- Perimeter: mark the wall live (it was a factor on the mat — "the wall is your friend, keep him there").
- The **San Chai pull** ("don't acquiesce to the catch") = a kicker-side coaching cue; the **finger-curl** safety note belongs in the safety chips.

**Verdict: ~90% there, eco-strong. Build after minor fixes.** Fills gap #5.

## GAME 5 — Wall Dirty-Boxing (integration, from the sparring debrief)

**Mat version (not run as a formal game — emerged from sparring coaching):** when you pin someone to the wall, **don't instantly default to the level-change.** Stay: forehead to center-chest, dirty box inside (hooks/uppercuts). The **double threat** (strike + takedown) is what breaks them — defend the level change and the dirty boxing opens; cover up and the takedown opens.

**Cross-check:** this is an **integration game** (category M, the MMA-differentiator gap we have almost nothing in). The **double threat is the eco mechanism** — rule 2 (the affordance emerges from *which* threat they defend) + rule 15 (read what their defense affords you). A genuine constraint-led dilemma.

**Proposed spec (needs a mat test):**
- Attacker: has a **wall pin** (per the locked definition: ≥2 points of control, hips + upper body controlled). Wins by a clean **shown-strike sequence** OR a completed **level-change takedown.**
- Defender: wins by **escaping the pin to open space** (circle off the fence) or **reversing.**
- Order parameter: does the attacker exploit the **dilemma** (strike ↔ takedown) rather than committing early to a failing single threat — the exact mistake you coached in sparring.
- Asides banked as striking cues: **Stephen Thompson sideways stance** (working around compromised kicks), **free stance-switching** is fundamental.

**Verdict: highest ceiling (fills the integration gap), least formed.** The only item not actually run as a game today. **Propose + mat-test before canonization.**
