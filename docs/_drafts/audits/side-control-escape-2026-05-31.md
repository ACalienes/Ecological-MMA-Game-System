## Flare needed (for Alex's pass)

**Cross-page consistency:**
- **4 levels vs mount-escape's 5 and back-escape's 5.** Side-control-escape's progression is: L1 chest only → L2 + underhook → L3 + cross-face → L4 full expression. Only 3 control-progression levels before the strikes layer. Mount-escape has 4 control levels + 1 strike. Back-escape has 4 control levels + 1 strike. **Recommendation:** decide if side-control wants a 5th control level (e.g. a kimura-grip threat or a hip-switch level) for symmetry, or if you intentionally find the side-control progression shorter pedagogically.
- The "No striking until the top level" chip says "Levels 1 to 3" here vs "Levels 1 to 4" on the other two. That's correct given the 4-level structure, but if you bump to 5 levels, update the chip too.

**Voice / framing for Alex to confirm:**
- L1 *"Hands and head, no grips"* as the lightest pin. Is that how you teach the entry side-control hold, or do you start with the underhook already in?
- The read cue *"When they reach, the pin opens"* — does this match your coaching of the side-control escape timing? Or is your tell different (e.g. the head turn, the hip switch)?
- "Top wins by advancing to mount OR taking the back" — is the back-take from side-control common in your teaching, or is mount the dominant target?
- The graded escape language is identical to mount-escape (half-guard floor → cleaner → reverse). Same question: does this match how you grade side-control escapes specifically?
