## Flare needed (for Alex's pass)

**Cross-page consistency:**
- This page is the **gold standard for R2 in the family** (explicit 3s hold on the observable terminal). Recommend mount-escape and side-control-escape match this pattern.
- Difficulty marked as **advanced** here, vs **intermediate** for mount/side. Defensible — the choke threat is meaningfully more dangerous and the chin-tuck + hand-fight skill is later in the curriculum. ✓
- 5 levels, matches mount-escape, doesn't match side-control's 4.

**Voice / framing for Alex to confirm:**
- The directional cue *"Turn toward the choking-arm side, into the choke, never away"* — does this match how you teach the back escape direction? Or do you teach it differently (e.g. some coaches emphasize "always turn to face," others say "turn into the hands")?
- The "choke-arm high, base light low" read — same question: is that your coaching cue, or borrowed from the source?
- L1 starts with *"Hands on the choke arm only, defend the neck"* — is that how you'd start back-escape practice in class? Or do you start with the hand-fight more dynamic from the jump?
- The "closed structural geometry" language (closing the loop is enough, finish is post-game) — is that how you'd frame it, or would you require a tap?

**One specific decision worth flagging:**
- The page lists `concepts/guard-recovery` as a "Related" item, but back-escape's "graded escape" mechanic is different from guard-recovery (which is about leg reinsertion under mount/side). For back, the gradient is *how far you turn the corner* (chest-to-chest in guard → on top → standing). The page acknowledges this with *"the turn is graded by how far you escape"* but the cross-link to `guard-recovery` might suggest equivalence that isn't accurate. Consider: a separate concept (e.g. `back-escape-gradient`) or just clarify the relationship in the link description.
