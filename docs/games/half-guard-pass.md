<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Passing</span></div>

Win the underhook, flatten them, free the trapped leg.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top in half guard, one leg trapped, underhook and cross-face contested, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top wins the chest battle and frees the leg; bottom builds the shield or hook and recovers.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Leg freed to a chest-to-chest pin (side or mount), held 3s → top · Knee shield in, inside hook in, sweep, or full guard → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Half guard is one battle, <br><span class="accent">who owns the chest.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Underhook and cross-face decide it. <b>The trapped leg frees itself once the bottom is flat.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>angle of the bottom's torso against your chest</i>. On their side, they're alive: the underhook, the shield, and the sweep all live there. Flat on the back, they're done. Every inch of shoulder you press to the mat shortens their options, the leg comes free when the hips can't follow the knee.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (passer), one bottom (half guard).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top's leg trapped between the bottom's; underhook and cross-face contested, nobody owns the chest yet.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top flattens and frees the leg; bottom fights for the underhook, the shield, and the recovery.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from contested half guard; reset on a pass, a recovery, a sweep, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Passer)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to win the underhook and cross-face, flatten the bottom, and free the trapped leg into a held pin.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Chest before leg, always. Yanking the knee while the bottom is on their side feeds the sweep. Flatten the shoulders, kill the inside frames, and the leg slides out almost on its own.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Half Guard)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to stay on the side, win the underhook or build the knee shield, and recover full guard, sweep, or stand.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">The side position is everything, the moment your back touches flat, start framing back to it. Underhook beats shield when you can get it; the shield buys time when you can't.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🫁</span> The chest battle decides<span class="emma-chip__detail">Underhook and cross-face are the contested resources. Whoever owns them owns the round's direction, the leg is a consequence, not the fight.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Pass proven by the hold<span class="emma-chip__detail">The pass counts when the trapped leg is free AND the top holds a chest-to-chest pin (side control or mount) for 3 seconds. Freeing the leg into a scramble proves nothing yet.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🛡️</span> Recovery is graded<span class="emma-chip__detail">Knee shield in, inside hook in, full guard recovered, a sweep, or standing all win for the bottom, each a cleaner answer than the last. Name which one you got.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so both sides can read the chest battle without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. The cross-face hand becomes a striking hand, exactly as it does in MMA. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top frees the leg to a chest-to-chest pin (side or mount), held 3s → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">The trapped leg cleared and the pin held. Freeing the leg but losing the chest in the scramble doesn't count, the hold is the proof.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom recovers full guard, sweeps, or stands → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Graded ladder: knee shield and inside hook are progress markers; full guard, a sweep to top, or standing fully wins the round.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Round cap expires in contested half guard → reset, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Neither the pass nor the recovery landed before the clock. Switch roles so both sides get both problems.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Win the hip access</b>Flatten them, nothing else.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The round is only about torso angle: top works to press the bottom's shoulders flat, bottom works to stay on the side. Whoever owns the angle at the bell owns the round. The fight underneath every half-guard exchange, isolated.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Single underhook</b>Chest-to-chest, one wing in.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds the underhook on one side plus the cross-face, and must keep both while flattening. Bottom hand-fights for the underhook back. The chest battle becomes explicit.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Double unders</b>Both wings, total chest control.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top works to double underhooks before any leg work. Heavier control, but the bottom's hips get freer, feel the tradeoff between arm control and hip control.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Free the leg</b>The full pass, held.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The complete task, grappling only: win the chest, flatten, free the trapped leg to side or mount held 3 seconds, against a bottom playing the full recovery ladder.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on. The cross-face hand now strikes, the bottom's shield now manages punches too. Half guard the way MMA actually plays it.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What's the one battle half guard reduces to?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Who owns the chest</b>, the underhook and cross-face. The trapped leg is a consequence of that battle, not the battle itself.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is yanking the knee out early a mistake?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because while the bottom is <b>on their side</b>, their underhook and sweep are live. Pulling the leg from there trades your base for their best moment. Flatten first.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does the bottom's torso angle tell the top?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>On the side = alive, flat = finished.</b> Every inch of shoulder pressed to the mat removes options. The leg slides free when the hips can't follow the knee.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> When should the bottom take the shield over the underhook?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">When the underhook is lost or being smashed. <b>The underhook wins position; the shield buys time</b> to angle back and re-fight for it.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Passer)</div><p>Win underhook and cross-face, press the shoulders flat, kill the inside frames, then free the leg into a held pin.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Half Guard)</div><p>Fight to the side, hand-fight the underhook, build the shield when losing, climb the recovery ladder: hook, full guard, sweep, stand.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🫁</div><div class="emma-card__t">Who had the chest?</div><p>Ask both: "When the pass came, who owned the underhook?" Ties the outcome back to the battle that decided it.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">📐</div><div class="emma-card__t">Side or flat?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "How long were you flat before the leg came out?" Builds the alarm bell that flat = the clock is running.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Angle-only opening level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates the flatten-vs-side war before any grips exist</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Underhook levels (single → double)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Makes the chest battle explicit and felt</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Pass proven by leg-free + 3s pin</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards control sequence over knee-yanking</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Graded recovery ladder</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">The bottom always has a next rung, no dead rounds</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting opponent</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the torso-angle read intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the level ladder peels the half-guard problem into its perceptual layers (angle → chest → leg) while the opponent stays live, so the read that matters, torso angle under pressure, never leaves the game.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>The bottom's torso angle against the chest → flat enough to start freeing the leg, or still alive on the side.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own underhook depth and cross-face pressure → whether the chest is actually won.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The bottom's free knee → shield coming, hook hunting, or recovery starting.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>wins the chest before working the leg</b>. Track passes held vs. sweeps conceded, and what the bottom's torso angle was at each pass. When passes consistently come from a flattened bottom rather than a knee-yank race, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the most common contested ground position in MMA, half guard after an imperfect takedown or a partial recovery, where GnP and passing share the same chest battle.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: chest-battle ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the passing side of <a href="../ground-access/">Ground Access</a>; mirrors <a href="../half-guard-bottom/">Half-Guard Bottom</a>; the recovery ladder connects to <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Wins the underhook before touching the trapped leg</li>
      <li>Flattens the shoulders and keeps them flat</li>
      <li>Frees the leg into a held pin, not a scramble</li>
      <li>As bottom: fights back to the side the moment the back touches</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Yanks the knee while the bottom is on their side</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Trades the cross-face away to hunt the leg</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Bottom accepts flat and waits</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Recovery skips rungs and gives the back</span>
    </div>
