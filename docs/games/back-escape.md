<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Defensive</span><span>Advanced</span><span>Counter</span></div>

Turn to face before the choking arm finds your neck.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom with the back taken, top in a seatbelt with both hooks in, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom defends the neck, strips a hook, and turns in to face; top keeps the back and works the choking arm under the chin.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Turn in to face, reverse, or stand → bottom · Get the choking arm under the chin → top · Hold the back the full count, no finish → reset · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Don't out-muscle the choke, <br><span class="accent">outrun it to the front.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Hands defend the neck, hips turn into the choke. <b>Every time they hunt the chin, a hook comes light.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>tension in the seatbelt and the path of the choking arm</i>, the moment the top swims a hand toward your throat or shifts weight to climb. That specifies <i>which way to turn</i> and <i>when a hook is light enough to strip</i>, not the top's voice or a memorized sequence. When the choke arm climbs high, turn low into it.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (defender), one top (on the back).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top chest-to-back in a seatbelt (one arm over the shoulder, one under the armpit, hands clasped), both hooks in; bottom seated or on the loaded side.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom defends the neck and turns to face; top keeps the back and works the choke.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from seatbelt and hooks; reset on escape, the arm under the chin, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to defend the neck, strip a hook, and turn in to face (or reverse, or stand).</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Two hands on the choking arm, chin tucked to your chest. Slide your hips down, strip a hook, then turn toward the choking-arm side, never away from it. Survival first, then escape, exposing the neck to rush the turn hands the top the finish. The turn is graded: chest-to-chest is the floor, on top is better.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep the back and slide the choking arm across the throat, under the chin.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Stay chest-to-back, follow the hips, keep the seatbelt tight. Bait the hand-fight, then swim the choking arm under the chin. Control is proven by closing on the neck, not by riding the back for time.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom defends and turns, no subs<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom hand-fights, strips hooks, and turns to face continuously. No submissions from the bottom, the task is the escape, not the counter-attack.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by getting the arm under the chin<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by sliding the choking arm across the throat and under the chin (the closing position every rear-naked choke shares), not by sitting on the back. This gives the top an active, observable target instead of a stall.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or escape<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps back control for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without getting the arm under the chin, the round resets. If the bottom turns to face, reverses, or stands first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so the bottom can read the seatbelt and hand-fight without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom turns in to face, reverses, or stands → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">The turn is graded by how far you escape: turning chest-to-chest into the top's guard is the floor, coming out on top is better, standing clear is best. Stripping a hook and facing kills the back position, the choke can no longer be closed from the front.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top slides the choking arm under the chin → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">The choking hand crossed the throat and under the jaw, the shared closing of the rear-naked choke. The observable proof that back control beat the hand-fight. Stop at the closing position, before any squeeze.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds the back the full count, no arm under the chin → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the back but never got under the chin before the count expired. The round resets from seatbelt and hooks for another rep.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Hands on the choke arm</b>Defend the neck only.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom starts with two hands gripping the choking arm and must keep the chin tucked while the top tries to climb the hand toward the throat. Isolates neck defense and the chin-down habit.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Strip one hook</b>Free a leg.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom adds the goal of clearing one hook off the hip while still defending the neck. Reading which hook is light becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Turn the corner</b>Hips down, face in.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">With a hook cleared, the bottom slides the hips to the mat and turns toward the choking-arm side to face. Forces the top to choose between chasing the choke and keeping the back.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Break the seatbelt</b>Win the 2-on-1.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom peels the clasped hands or wins a 2-on-1 on the choking arm to break the seatbelt, then turns. The hand-fight-to-turn mechanic is now the focus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from seatbelt and hooks until the bottom faces or the top gets under the chin, with light strikes on. The choke and strike threat make hesitation costly, escape under real urgency.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What tells you a hook is ready to strip?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">When the top <b>swims the choking arm high for the chin or shifts to climb</b>, the base and a hook go light. <b>Choke-arm high, turn low into it.</b> Strip the light hook and turn toward the choking-arm side.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does the top win by getting the arm under the chin rather than just riding the back?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">The arm under the chin is the <b>shared closing of the rear-naked choke</b>, and it is observable. It gives the top an active target instead of a stall, and it keeps the bottom's chin honest.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What is the bottom's first job, before escaping?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Defend the neck, two hands on the choking arm, chin tucked.</b> Rushing the turn with the neck exposed hands the top the finish. Hands first, then turn.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Which way do you turn to escape the back, and why?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Toward the choking-arm side, into the choke</b>, never away. Turning away feeds your neck to the choke; turning in chases the choking arm off your throat and brings you to face.</div>
  </details>
</div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Defense restricted per level (hands → strip hook → turn → seatbelt)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates one escape variable at a time</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by getting the arm under the chin</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the bottom's chin tucked, gives the top an active target</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or escape</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the bottom, no stalling for the top</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">No strikes until the top level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Frees the bottom to read the seatbelt before strike defense</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting top</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the tension-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the defense ladder isolates one escape variable per level while the bottom keeps reading seatbelt tension and timing from a real, resisting opponent. The top's arm-under-chin win keeps the representativeness, this is the actual danger of the back.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Seatbelt tension and hook pressure → which way the choke is coming, which hook is light, when to turn.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own chin and hip position → whether the neck is safe and whether the corner can turn yet.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The choking arm swimming high → the timing window to strip a hook and turn into it.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>strips a hook and turns to face faster than the top can slide the arm under the chin</b>. Track escapes to face vs. chokes closed, and whether the chin stays tucked as the top swims. The hand-fight-and-turn versus swim-and-close race is the order parameter; when the bottom consistently turns into the lightened choke arm, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> being on the back under a seatbelt and turning to face before the choke closes, the exact problem of back defense in grappling and in MMA.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: defense ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the back-control danger behind <a href="../ground-escape/">Ground Escape</a>; the hand-fighting transfers into <a href="../ground-defensive-submission/">Ground Defensive Submission</a> and <a href="../ground-to-standing/">Ground to Standing</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Keeps the chin tucked and two hands on the choke arm under pressure</li>
      <li>Strips a hook before turning the corner</li>
      <li>Turns toward the choking-arm side, not away</li>
      <li>Breaks the seatbelt or wins the 2-on-1 when the first turn fails</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Chin lifts, neck exposed</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Turns away from the choke</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Pulls the arm without stripping a hook</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Panics when the first turn fails</span>
    </div>
