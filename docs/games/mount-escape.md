<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Defensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Counter</span></div>

Get out from under mount before the top isolates an arm.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom flat under mount, top settled, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom frames, makes hip space, and recovers; top stays mounted and hunts an isolated arm.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Knee back in (half guard) or better, reverse, or stand → bottom · Isolate an arm or hold mount the full count → top · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">You don't escape the weight, <br><span class="accent">you escape the space it leaves.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Frame to make room, bridge into the light side. <b>Every time the top reaches, the base lightens.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>felt load through the top's hips and hands</i>, the moment the base lifts as they posture, climb, or reach for your arm. That weight shift specifies <i>which way to bridge</i> and <i>when the hip space opens</i>, not the top's eyes or a memorized sequence. When they grab high, the base goes light low.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (defender), one top (mounted).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Bottom flat on the back, top in a settled mount, hips low.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom frames and escapes; top maintains mount and hunts an isolated arm.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from settled mount; reset on escape, arm isolation, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to frame, make hip space, and recover a guard (half guard or better), a reversal, or standing.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Keep elbows tight to deny the arm. Bridge into the side the top loads, then shrimp into the space. Protect the arms first, then escape, panic exposes the arm the top wants. Recovery is graded: the more leg you get back between you, the cleaner the escape.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep mount and pull one of the bottom's arms away from the body.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Ride the bridge, stay heavy on the hips, climb when threatened. Bait a frame, then attack the arm it leaves. Control is proven by isolating an arm, not just sitting on top.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom frames and escapes, no subs<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom works frames, bridges, and hip escapes continuously. No submissions from the bottom, the task is the escape, not the counter-attack.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by isolating an arm<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by pulling one of the bottom's arms clear of the body (the entry every mounted submission shares), not by sitting still. This gives the top an active, observable target instead of a stall.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or escape<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps mount for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without isolating an arm, the round resets. If the bottom recovers guard, reverses, or stands first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so the bottom can read weight and build frames without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom gets a knee back between (half guard) or better, reverses, or stands → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Recovery is graded by how much leg you reinsert: one knee in (half guard) is the floor, then both knees, then both feet, then a closed loop (closed guard), each a cleaner escape. A reversal to top or standing up is a full escape. See <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top isolates an arm clear of the body → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">One of the bottom's arms pulled away from the torso and controlled, the shared entry to armbar, arm-triangle, and back-take. The observable proof that mount control has beaten the frames.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds mount the full count, no arm → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the position but never isolated an arm before the count expired. The round resets from settled mount for another rep.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Two hands, one hip</b>Both hands frame one hip.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom starts with both hands framing one of the top's hips and must hold that frame while the top works to pull an arm free. Isolates the single strongest frame and the elbow-tight habit that denies the arm.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>One hand each hip</b>Split the frames.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">A hand on each of the top's hips. Now the bottom must keep both elbows tight at once and pick which side to bridge. Reading the loaded side becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Frames of choice, hands to mat</b>Force the base down.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom uses any frames and adds a goal: force the top to put a hand on the mat to keep balance. A posted hand is a committed base, the first crack in the mount.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Attack the posts</b>Off-balance the committed base.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Once the top posts, the bottom bridges hard into that post to break the base and start the recovery. The bridge-into-the-light-side mechanic is now the focus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from settled mount until the bottom gets a knee or hips between the players, with light strikes on. The top's strike threat makes hesitation costly, escape under real urgency.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What weight shift tells you the hip space has opened?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">When the top <b>postures, climbs, or reaches high for your arm</b>, the base lifts and goes light low. <b>Grab high, base light low.</b> Bridge into the side they loaded.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does the top win by isolating an arm rather than just holding?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">An isolated arm is the <b>shared entry to armbar, arm-triangle, and the back-take</b>, and it is observable. It gives the top an active target instead of a stall, and it keeps the bottom's elbows honest.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What is the bottom's first job, before escaping?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Protect the arms, elbows tight.</b> Panic and flailing expose the exact arm the top is hunting. Frame first, make space, then move.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does forcing the top to post a hand actually buy you?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">A posted hand is a <b>committed base</b>. Bridge hard into that post and the top has no frame to catch the fall, the mount breaks and the recovery starts.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Keep elbows tight, frame the hips, read the loaded side, bridge into it, shrimp into the space, chain the next frame when one fails.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Attacker)</div><p>Stay heavy on the hips, ride the bridge, climb when threatened, bait a frame and attack the arm it leaves.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⚓</div><div class="emma-card__t">Where is the base?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "When did the base go light?" Direct attention to the load shift, not to a named technique.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🫷</div><div class="emma-card__t">Elbows or arm?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you get the arm, or just sit?" Keeps the top hunting the isolated arm instead of stalling for the count.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Frame restricted per level (one hip → split → posts)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates one frame and one read at a time</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by isolating an arm</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the bottom's elbows tight, gives the top an active target</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or escape</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the bottom, no stalling for the top</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">No strikes until the top level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Frees the bottom to read weight before adding strike defense</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting top</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the weight-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the frame ladder isolates one escape variable per level while the bottom keeps reading load and timing from a real, resisting opponent. The top's arm-isolation win keeps the representativeness, this is the actual danger of mount.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Load through the top's hips and hands → which side is heavy, when the base lifts, which way to bridge.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own elbow and hip position → whether the arm is safe and whether hip space exists yet.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Top posturing or reaching high → the timing window to bridge into the lightened base.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>re-frames and opens hip space faster than the top can isolate an arm or re-settle weight</b>. Track escapes recovered vs. arms isolated, and whether the elbows stay tight as the top climbs. The frame-and-bridge versus settle-and-isolate race is the order parameter; when the bottom consistently times the bridge to the lightened base, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> being mounted and recovering before an arm is isolated or strikes accumulate, the exact problem under mount in competition and in MMA.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: frame ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the mount level of <a href="../ground-escape/">Ground Escape</a>; the weight-reading transfers into <a href="../leg-reclaim/">Leg Reclaim</a> and <a href="../ground-to-standing/">Ground to Standing</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Keeps both elbows tight under pressure</li>
      <li>Bridges into the loaded side, not away from it</li>
      <li>Forces the top to post, then attacks the post</li>
      <li>Chains a second frame when the first fails</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Arms drift away from the body</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Bridges straight up, not to a side</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Waits and survives instead of framing</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Panics when the first escape fails</span>
    </div>
