# The Square — Close Defense to Takedown Progression

!!! warning "Provisional (WIP) — derived from class recording 2026-05-25"
    This game was captured live from a class and is **pending review on the platform**.
    Name and structure may change. Remove the WIP status once confirmed.

!!! info "Game Identity"
    - **Problem:** Defending and surviving in a confined space, then earning the right to counter, clinch, and finish
    - **Environment:** Open Space → Confined Square (no exit)
    - **Stage:** Access → Counter → Control

This is a **progression game**, not a single fixed game. It begins as close-range blocking defense inside a confined square and is layered live — one constraint at a time — until it becomes a full striking-to-takedown game. It is the in-class bridge between [Tight Block](tight-block.md), [Close-Range Defense](close-range-defense.md), and [Pressure to Takedown](pressure-to-takedown.md).

---

## Goal

This is an **asymmetric game** with roles that switch on a win. The objective shifts by phase: **survival → defensive dominance → control.**

| Role | Objective |
|------|-----------|
| **Offense** | Land significant strikes (and, later, takedowns) within the square |
| **Defense** | Survive with a tight shell, stay in the square, and — as the game opens — counter, off-balance, and finish |

---

## Invariants

1. **Confined space — no footwork escape.** Both players stay in the square.
2. **Both feet leaving the square = automatic loss**, whoever you are. The boundary is live, like a cage edge.
3. No tying up. Hand controls are legal to nullify the opponent, but break fast — boxing rules.
4. Strikes scale to the partner; offense must allow the game to play (no teeing off).
5. **One new variable at a time.** Earlier constraints persist unless explicitly lifted.

---

## Task Focus

### Defense
- Maintain a tight shell; absorb on arms/gloves, not exposed targets
- Keep eye contact on **center mass** to read incoming strikes (see [Close-Range Defense](close-range-defense.md#reading-center-mass))
- **Base down — bend the knees, create suspension** so strike force doesn't rob your counters
- Use hand controls to interrupt the opponent's boxing; redirect directional force rather than absorb it
- Stay conscious of where your feet are relative to the boundary

### Offense
- Land significant strikes in scaled combinations (start ≤3, escalate to a 2-significant-impact requirement)
- Land with the **front two knuckles** for clean energy transfer
- In later phases, mix striking with leg attacks / double-under entries to finish

!!! question "Key Internal Questions"
    - "Can I see the shoulder and hip motion, or am I blind behind my gloves?"
    - "Am I based and balanced, or getting jostled out of my counters?"
    - "Where is the boundary right now?"

## Key Logic

### The boundary is the lesson
Confining the square removes the footwork-escape habit and forces the defender to solve the problem *in the pocket*. Making "both feet out = loss" explicit converts the boundary from scenery into a live constraint — the same awareness a fighter needs near the cage.

### Redirect, don't just absorb
When the offense abandons clean boxing to bully forward ("cheater McGeeter"), that pressure is the defender's opportunity: redirect the directional force to off-balance and win. Being pushed out is the defender's problem to solve, not a foul to call.

## Levels

These are the live phases, added **one at a time**:

=== "Phase 1 — Confined Block + Hand Controls"
    - Offense: ≤3-punch combination, land 1 significant shot to win
    - Defense: close blocking only — no parrying, no head movement, no footwork escape
    - Hand controls legal to nullify; no tie-ups (break fast)

=== "Phase 2 — Counter + Live Boundary"
    - Defense may counter with **one** strike (interrupt the rhythm; keep defending)
    - **Both feet out of the square = loss**

=== "Phase 3 — Clinch Off-Balancing"
    - Defense can win by **off-balancing the opponent in the clinch** (redirect directional force)
    - Offense now must land **two** significant strikes, and may enter on the legs / double-under body lock

=== "Phase 4 — Kick Counters"
    - Defense may counter with **kicks above the waist** (e.g., body kick after a block)
    - A caught kick is not a score and leaves the kicker liable
    - No kicks to head or legs — body only

=== "Phase 5 — Single-Leg Takedown Finish"
    - Start from a single-leg position; inside grip, alternating to outside
    - Offense: get the opponent's hips to the ground (hands down / feet up = on track); no scrambling once hips land
    - Defense: separate and get out, strike at any level, redirect-to-top, or finish with a top neck strangle / guillotine
    - Back exposure = bonus

---

## Win Conditions

By phase (cumulative):

- **Offense wins** by landing the required significant strikes (1 → 2), or — later — connecting a leg attack / double-under and finishing to the ground, or pushing the defender's feet out of the square.
- **Defense wins** by surviving, landing a clean counter (1 strike; later a body kick), off-balancing the offense in the clinch, or establishing a top neck strangle.
- **Either player loses** the instant both feet leave the square.
- A caught kick = not landed; the kicker is liable if it's used to push them out or take them down.

## Advanced Expressions (Coach Context)

- The progression is built to **fill the class's collective deficiency** — "the games are designed to fill the deficiency in total of the class." Friction is the point.
- **Add one variable at a time.** When students asked for more counter tools early (shadow knee, clinch), the answer was a deliberate "no — keep it hands only" until the base skill held; grappling came a phase later. Resist enriching the game before the current constraint has done its work.
- Watch for the **half-enjoying / half-struggling** signal — that blend is the desensitization working. Students reported the value was exactly the clinch tolerance and eye-contact reading "that we don't normally work on."
- Everything ties back to the cage: the boundary, the base, and the in-pocket defense all transfer to fighting near the fence.

---

## Safety

- **Contact limits:** Light to moderate — felt but not damaging. Basing + front-two-knuckle contact makes punches stronger; scale accordingly.
- **Stop conditions:** Loss of control, caught-kick scrambles going wrong, boundary disputes, hips hitting the ground (game over — no scrambling).
- **Coach intervention:** Reset if the offense crowds and removes the defensive opportunity, or if tie-ups stall the action.
