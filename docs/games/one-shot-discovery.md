<div class="emma-meta"><span>Open Space</span><span>Striking</span><span>Combined</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>WIP</span></div>

Discover where you can land your first shot with different tools, in a perimeter that won't let you run.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Two fighters at striking range inside a tight rectangular perimeter; one on offense, one on defense.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Offense lands one clean shot off a feint; defense reads the path, neutralizes, and at higher levels lands the counter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Offense lands → keep offense, switch tool · Defense clears → switch roles · Defense counters (L4+) → switch roles · Both feet out → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">One shot, four tools, <br><span class="accent">no exit.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Feints are the work. <b>One correct read beats ten thrown shots.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>moment a feint resolves into a shot</i>, the load through the lead foot, the shoulder turning, the elbow committing. That commitment specifies <i>when</i> the strike arrives and <i>which weapon</i> is loading, not their eyes or rhythm. A feint that shows commitment without the shot is a counter opening, not a threat to defend.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, or three on rotation (top, bottom, out, switch halfway).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Range</span>Striking range, neutral stance.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked rectangular perimeter, kept deliberately tight. Both feet out, you lose.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>One offense, one defense; roles switch on a clean defense.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Offense initiates; reset on land, clean defense, counter, or perimeter loss.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥊</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Offense</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to land one clean shot with any single weapon (lead hand, rear hand, lead leg, rear leg), set up by feints.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Feints to head, body, legs, or stance (back-fist show, foot-stomp, level-change feint). Once you land a shot, you may NOT throw the same weapon next, you have to vary the tool for at least one cycle. No combinations. The skill is the menu, not one favourite.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🛡️</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Defense</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to neutralize the shot with the full defensive shell, and at higher levels counter or close to clinch.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Close blocking, parry, head-movement evasion, hand controls always available. At L3+ you may clinch as a defensive escape (at your own peril, you can be hit on the way in). At L4+ you also win by landing a clean counter or connecting under the waist on legs (no body lock).</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> One shot, four tools<span class="emma-chip__detail">Offense throws exactly one strike per exchange, chosen from lead hand, rear hand, lead leg, or rear leg. No combinations. After a landed shot, the same weapon cannot be thrown again until at least one other has been used.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎭</span> Feints allowed<span class="emma-chip__detail">Feints to head, body, legs, or stance are the primary setup tool. A feint is an invitation to counter at L4+, the defender can act off the feint without waiting for physical contact.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🛡️</span> Defensive shell allowed<span class="emma-chip__detail">Block, parry, head movement, footwork inside the perimeter, hand controls. Defense may strike to break up hand-control attempts.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤼</span> Grapple-as-defense (L3+)<span class="emma-chip__detail">At L3 and above the defender may clinch to negate strikes, at their own peril. Offense does NOT grapple, offense throws strikes only. If offense lands before the clinch closes, offense wins the exchange.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">A marked rectangle, kept deliberately tight. If both feet leave the perimeter, that player loses the exchange regardless of who had the advantage.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Stay</span> Offense lands cleanly → keep offense, switch tool.<span class="emma-pop">A clean single shot to the target zone. Offense keeps the role for another exchange, but cannot reuse the same weapon until at least one other has been thrown.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Defense clears the shot → switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Block, parry, evasion, or grapple-clinch (L3+) that prevents the clean shot. Defender earns the offensive role.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Counter</span> Defense lands a counter (L4+) → switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">A clean counter strike that lands, or a connection on legs under the waist (no body lock). A counter that MISSES is not a defense, defender loses that exchange (you spent the moment without a return).</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Both feet leave the perimeter → loss.<span class="emma-pop">The tight perimeter is the constraint that forces you to solve in the pocket. Stepping out loses the exchange regardless of position.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Pure shell</b>Defense uses shell only.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Offense throws single shots with feints. Defense uses block, parry, head movement, and hand controls. No clinch, no counter. Pure defensive-shell calibration in tight space.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Vary the tool</b>No-repeat rule.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">After offense lands, the same weapon cannot be thrown again until at least one other weapon has been used. Forces the menu of four to actually develop, not just one favourite.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Clinch as defense</b>Grapple to escape.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Defender may clinch (and hand-control) to negate strikes. At your own peril, offense can hit you on the way in. Offense still throws strikes only, never grapples. Perimeter enforcement tightens, both feet out = loss.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Defense counters</b>Land or lose.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Defense also wins by landing a clean counter, OR by connecting under the waist on legs (no body lock). The counter must LAND, a missed counter loses the exchange. Feints become counter openings, defender can act on the feint without waiting for the shot.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Body-kick counter</b>Skill isolation.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Defender's counter must be a kick to the body (front kick, round kick, lead or rear leg, switch stance allowed) OR a leg connection. Forces the body-kick into the menu, since open-space games tend to under-represent it.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What role do feints play in this game?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Feints <b>draw responses you can exploit</b> on offense. At L4+ they become a <b>counter opening</b> for the defender too, a feint shows commitment without the shot, so defender can act on it without waiting for physical contact.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why can't you repeat the weapon you just landed?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">It forces the <b>menu of four to develop</b>, not just one favourite. Discovery of where each tool works in which moment is the game; one favourite weapon is the failure mode the rule prevents.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why must the defender's counter LAND, not just be thrown?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">A missed strike is <b>exposure without reward</b>. You spent your moment without a return. The rule punishes throwing for free and aligns defense with the same standard as offense, one clean shot or nothing.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> How does the L3 clinch work as defense and not as offense?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Clinch <b>closes range to neutralize strikes</b>, that is its job in this game. Offense throws strikes only, the defender's clinch is an escape, not a takedown attack. If offense lands before the clinch closes, offense wins, the clinch is at your own peril.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Offense</div><p>Pick the moment with a feint, vary the menu, commit on the read. One shot, four tools, set traps with feints.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🛡️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Defense</div><p>Defensive shell first, then add tools as levels open. At L4+, treat feints as counter openings, not threats to absorb.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎭</div><div class="emma-card__t">What feint set up that shot?</div><p>Ask offense: "What did you show before the shot landed?" Direct attention to the setup, not the strike itself.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🎯</div><div class="emma-card__t">Did you land or just throw?</div><p>Ask defense (L4+): "Did your counter land, or did you just throw it?" Keeps the land-or-lose standard honest, no free strikes.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">One-shot constraint</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Forces commitment + discovery, not volume</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Tight perimeter</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">No retreat, solve in the pocket</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Tool-variation rule</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Develops the four-weapon menu, not a favourite</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Role switch on success</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Both sides develop, no one specialises in one role</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Counter-must-land (L4+)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Punishes throwing for free, equal standard both sides</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019) and <b>Representative Learning Design</b>, the one-shot + perimeter constraints isolate the read-and-commit problem, while the role switch and L3+ layers add representativeness back in. The skill develops as <i>degeneracy</i>, multiple weapons matched to multiple feints.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether each landed shot is <b>preceded by a feint that drew a defensive response</b>, vs. cold commits. Track the feint-to-land ratio and the variation of weapons used over a round. When commits start to ride on read feints rather than cold timing, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the read-feint-commit cycle that decides exchanges at every level of striking, in a perimeter tight enough to enforce solving rather than escaping.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: one shot</span><span>tight perimeter</span><span>role switch</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Builds on <a href="../counter-striking/">Counter-Striking</a> and <a href="../land-the-target/">Land the Target</a>; transfers into <a href="../pressure-to-clinch/">Pressure to Clinch</a> when the L3 clinch escape is upgraded into an offensive entry.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Lands at least 2 different weapons per round</li>
      <li>Sets up shots with at least one feint</li>
      <li>At L4+, lands counters from feints, not only from shots</li>
      <li>Does not chase out of the perimeter</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">One favourite weapon repeated</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Cold commits with no feint</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Counter thrown but not landed</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Steps out of the perimeter to reset</span>
    </div>
