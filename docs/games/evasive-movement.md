Prevent engagement entirely through footwork and ring generalship — "getting on your bike."

<div class="emma-arc">

  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>

  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The best defense —<br><span class="accent">is the fight that never starts.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Avoid engagement through footwork and ring generalship, not by absorbing strikes. <b>Win the space, don't get cornered.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>rate the open space is closing</i> (global optic flow) and the <i>pursuer's preferred distance</i> — far affords your escape, near affords their engagement. Read their angle of approach and weight commitment, plus the gap to the perimeter behind you, <b>not</b> their eyes. That information specifies <i>where your space is</i> and <i>when to pivot vs. circle</i>.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two — one evader, one pursuer.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Range</span>Medium range, outside engagement.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter — prevents endless retreat.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>One evader, one pursuer.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Pursuer closes; reset when range is established or time elapses.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🦅</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Evader</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to avoid engagement and maintain distance for the set duration — pure movement, no striking at Level 1.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Keep moving — static defense doesn't count. Use lateral movement, angles, and pivots. Conserve energy, and above all don't get cornered against the perimeter.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥊</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Pursuer</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to close distance, cut off angles, and herd the evader toward the perimeter.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Actively close — not just walk forward. Cut angles, pressure without overcommitting, and herd toward the boundary. Recognize when the evader is trapped, and keep composure through the frustration.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🦅</span> Evader must keep moving<span class="emma-chip__detail">Static defense doesn't count — the evader's whole job is movement. Standing and blocking is not a solution here.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🥊</span> Pursuer actively closes<span class="emma-chip__detail">The pursuer must genuinely close and cut angles — not just walk forward passively. This creates real pressure to move efficiently.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking at Level 1<span class="emma-chip__detail">At Level 1 the evader cannot strike — pure evasion. Single counters are introduced only at Level 3.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> A marked perimeter forces decisions<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter — any shape (ring, cage, taped lines). The boundary prevents infinite retreat and creates decision points.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Win by duration or by cornering<span class="emma-chip__detail">The evader wins by keeping distance for a set time; the pursuer wins by establishing range or cornering. Clear objectives for both roles.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Keep distance for the duration → switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">The evader avoided engagement and kept the space open the whole round — ring generalship demonstrated — and earns the pursuing role.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Pursuer establishes range or corners → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The space closed: the pursuer cut the angle and trapped the evader against the perimeter, or closed to engagement. Begin again at medium range.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Diagnostic</span> Watch movement quality — angles and pivots, not gassing.<span class="emma-pop">Beyond the win, the coach reads movement quality: economy, use of angles, and composure under pursuit — not frantic circling or gassing.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Pure movement</b>Evader can only move.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Evader moves only — no strikes. Pursuer walks/jogs forward with pressure. Footwork fundamentals.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Add feints</b>Read real vs. fake.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The pursuer can feint to test reactions; the evader must read real vs. fake. Movement under pressure.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Evader can counter</b>Single counters while moving.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The evader can throw single counters while moving, punishing pursuer overcommitment. Movement + counter integration.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Full MMA expression</b>Shot / clinch threat.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The pursuer can shoot or clinch; the evader must manage all threats while moving. Evasion under MMA pressure.</span></div>
</div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🦅</div><div class="emma-card__t">Evader</div><p>Lateral movement and angles; distance management; ring awareness; time pivots; conserve energy.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Pursuer</div><p>Cut off angles; pressure without overcommitting; herd toward the boundary; recognize the trap.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🗺️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Where is your space?</div><p>Ask evaders: "Where is your space? Where is the wall?" This develops spatial awareness — the foundation of ring generalship.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✂️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Where do you want them?</div><p>Ask pursuers: "Where do you want them to go?" This develops cutting strategy over chasing.</p></div>
    </div>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Pursuer's angle and weight commitment; distance to the perimeter → escape direction, pivot vs. circle.</p></div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the <b>open space is opening or closing over time</b> — is the evader keeping distance and using angles, or is the gap collapsing toward the perimeter? Track distance kept, cornering events, and movement economy. When the evader consistently keeps the space open without gassing, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> avoiding a pressure fighter while maintaining positioning and energy efficiency.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: no strikes at L1</span><span>pure pursuit early</span><span>clear duration win</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Movement patterns transfer to any distance-management situation — leads into <a href="../pressure-to-clinch/">Pressure to Clinch</a> when evasion reaches the boundary.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Maintains distance for the duration consistently</li>
      <li>Movement is economical — not gassing prematurely</li>
      <li>Uses pivots and angles, not just circling</li>
      <li>Stays calm under pursuit pressure</li>
      <li>Can verbalize: "I saw them cutting, so I changed direction"</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Gets cornered frequently</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Gasses quickly</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Only circles one direction</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Panics near the boundary</span>
    </div>
