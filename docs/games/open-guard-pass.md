<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Passing</span></div>

Clear the feet, beat the knee line, get chest-to-chest.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top standing or kneeling at the bottom's feet; bottom on the back, knees retracted, framing inside the legs.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top clears the feet and passes the knee line; bottom off-balances and recovers.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Chest-to-chest past the knee line, held 3s → top · Top dumped to hands or seat → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The guard is two lines, <br><span class="accent">the feet and the knees.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Beat them in order. <b>A pass that skips a line gets pulled back through it.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>contact of the bottom's feet and shins on your legs and hips</i>. Every connection the bottom makes is a steering wheel; every connection broken is a lane opening. The pass lives where their feet are not, when both feet chase one side, the other side <i>is</i> the pass.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (passer), one bottom (open guard).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top standing or kneeling at the feet; bottom supine, knees retracted, feet and frames inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top breaks connections and passes; bottom steers, off-balances, and recovers.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin at the feet; reset on a pass, a dump, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Passer)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to clear the feet, beat the knee line, and land chest-to-chest past the legs.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Strip a connection, then move before the bottom replaces it. Pass on the side the feet abandoned. Heavy hips, low hands: posts get punished.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Guard)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep feet and frames connected, off-balance the top to hands or seat, and recover line by line.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Feet on hips and biceps steer the top. When their weight commits forward, lift and turn it. Retreat is graded: lose the foot line, rebuild at the knee line, never give both at once.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🦶</span> Lines fall in order<span class="emma-chip__detail">The pass works feet line first, then knee line, then chest. Jumping the lines into a scramble doesn't count, the held finish does. Order is the pedagogy.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Pass proven by the hold<span class="emma-chip__detail">A pass counts when the top is chest-to-chest past the knee line, held 3 seconds. The hold is the observable proof, not a moment of daylight.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚖️</span> Bottom wins by the dump<span class="emma-chip__detail">Off-balancing the top to hands or seat is the bottom's win, fully observable, no judgment call. Sweep mechanics without needing to come up.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so both sides read connections without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. Striking is the cost that punishes lazy frames and dead feet. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top reaches chest-to-chest past the knee line, held 3s → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">Both lines beaten and the connection held. The 3-second hold separates a pass from a fly-by.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom dumps the top to hands or seat, or recovers a closed loop → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">The top touching hands or seat to the mat is the observable off-balance win. Recovering closed guard around a passer who over-committed counts the same.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Round cap expires with the guard alive → reset, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Neither the pass nor the dump landed before the clock. Switch roles so both sides get the passing problem.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Clear the feet</b>Win the first line only.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The round is only about the feet: top works to clear them off hips and biceps, bottom works to keep them connected. Whoever owns the foot line at the bell owns the round. The steering battle, isolated.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Beat the knee line</b>Inside or outside.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Feet cleared, the top now passes the knees, inside lane or around the outside. Bottom retreats line by line and rebuilds. The decision of which lane comes from where the bottom's knees point.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Shin surf</b>Ride the legs down.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds the leg ride: shin across the bottom's thigh, weight settling, surfing the legs flat before advancing. Teaches passing as pressure over time, not a sprint. (After Craig Jones.)</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>To the chest</b>The full pass, held.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The complete task, grappling only: feet, knees, chest-to-chest held 3 seconds, against a bottom winning by the dump. Both reads at full speed.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on. The bottom's feet now also manage strike range, and the top pays for floating hands. Pass under fire.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What are the two lines, and why does order matter?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>The feet line, then the knee line.</b> A pass that skips a line gets pulled back through it, the bottom's abandoned connections re-attach behind you.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Where does the pass lane open?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Where the feet are not.</b> When both of the bottom's feet chase one side, the opposite side is the pass. Connections broken are lanes opened.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What is the bottom's observable win?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Dumping the top to hands or seat.</b> The sweep mechanic without needing to follow up, the moment the top's weight commits past their base, lift and turn it.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does the shin surf teach that a speed pass doesn't?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Passing as pressure over time.</b> The leg ride settles weight through the bottom's thighs until the recovery dies, instead of racing the recovery and losing.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Passer)</div><p>Strip connections, pass on the abandoned side, beat the lines in order, settle chest-to-chest and hold.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Guard)</div><p>Keep feet and frames attached, steer the top's weight, dump the over-commit, retreat one line at a time.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🦶</div><div class="emma-card__t">Whose feet won?</div><p>Ask both: "Where were the bottom's feet when the pass happened?" Directs attention to the connection battle that decides everything upstream.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⚖️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Felt the commit?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "When did their weight pass their base?" The dump window is felt through the frames, not seen.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">One line per early level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates the steering battle, then the lane decision</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Pass proven by a 3s hold</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards pressure over scramble-luck</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Dump-to-hands/seat win for the bottom</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Observable sweep mechanics, keeps the top's base honest</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Round cap</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency both ways, no guard-camping</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting guard</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the connection-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the line structure (feet → knees → chest) decomposes passing into perceptual stages without ever removing the live opponent, so each stage's read stays attached to real consequences.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Foot and shin contact on legs and hips → which connections are live, which side was abandoned.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own weight over the base → whether the next step is a pass or a dump waiting to happen.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The bottom's knee direction → inside or outside lane.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>breaks connections faster than the bottom rebuilds them</b>. Track passes held vs. dumps conceded, and which line each pass died at. When the top consistently passes on the abandoned side instead of fighting live feet, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> standing over an active open guard, the most common passing problem after any takedown that lands clean in MMA and grappling alike.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: line ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the passing side of <a href="../ground-access/">Ground Access</a>; mirrors <a href="../seated-guard-retention/">Seated Guard Retention</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Clears the foot line before attacking the knees</li>
      <li>Passes on the abandoned side, not into live feet</li>
      <li>Holds chest-to-chest 3s instead of racing past</li>
      <li>Keeps the base under the weight, survives the dump attempts</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Dives past both lines into a scramble</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Hands post on the mat inside the guard</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Fights the same connection over and over</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Bottom holds instead of steering, feet go dead</span>
    </div>
