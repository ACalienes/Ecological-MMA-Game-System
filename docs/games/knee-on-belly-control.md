    Fills the empty knee-on-belly bucket in the top-control grid. Sourced from the Slime Mold Grappling Club catalog (Greg Souders / Standard Jiu-Jitsu), re-expressed with our threshold rules. Passed the build rubric on paper; awaits validation against a live grappling class.

<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Control → Finish</span></div>

Keep the knee-on-belly alive through the frames, then attack an arm or step to mount.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top in knee-on-belly, posted, opposite leg out; bottom flat, framing the knee, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top stays light and balanced, switches sides on the frame, and starts an arm attack; bottom clears the knee and recovers.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Arm attack started, held 3s, or step to mount → top · Clear the knee and recover, or reverse → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Knee-on-belly is balance, <br><span class="accent">not weight.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Ride light and tall, switch with the frame, attack the arm it leaves. <b>Settle your weight and you'll get bumped off.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>bottom's framing hands and hip turn</i>, the moment they push the knee to make space or turn to recover. That shift specifies <i>when to switch sides</i> and <i>which arm extends into an attack</i>, not a memorized pin. When they push the knee hard, the other side and their pushing arm both open.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (attacker, knee-on-belly), one bottom (defender).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top with one knee on the belly, posted on the far foot, hips tall; bottom flat, framing the knee and the hip.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top keeps knee-on-belly alive and attacks; bottom clears the knee and recovers.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from settled knee-on-belly; reset on an arm attack, an advance, an escape, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep knee-on-belly live (switching sides as needed) and start an arm attack, or step to mount.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Ride light and tall, balance over the knee, don't dump your weight. When the bottom frames hard, switch to the far side or step to mount. Trap the arm they push with. Control is proven by an attack or an advance, not by perching.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to clear the knee, recover a guard, or reverse.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Frame the knee and the hip, turn to your side, and pull the knee across to recover. Don't push with a straight arm, that's the arm the top traps.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by an attack or an advance<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by starting an isolated-arm attack and holding it 3 seconds, or by stepping to mount. Keeping knee-on-belly without a threat is a stall, not a win. Switching sides to stay on top is allowed and encouraged.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom wins by clearing the knee<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom wins by clearing the knee and recovering a guard (half guard or better), or reversing. A clean, observable bottom goal.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or finish<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps knee-on-belly for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without an attack or an advance, the round resets. If the bottom clears the knee first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so both players read balance and frames before strikes are added. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound. Knee-on-belly is a classic strike-and-advance perch; strikes are the disincentivization tool that punishes a passive bottom. Ground games train smashing, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top starts an arm attack (3s) or steps to mount → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">An isolated-arm attack begun and controlled three seconds, or a clean step over to mount. Either is the observable proof that knee-on-belly is doing work, not just perching.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom clears the knee and recovers, or reverses → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Clearing the knee and getting a knee back in (half guard or better), or reversing, is the escape. See <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds knee-on-belly the full count, no threat → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top perched but never attacked or advanced before the count expired. Resets from settled knee-on-belly.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>No-hands balance</b>Ride light and tall.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top keeps knee-on-belly with no hands, balancing over the knee while the bottom bumps and frames. Builds the light, balanced ride that resists the bump.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Hands connected, maintain</b>Add the grips.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds collar-and-hip or near-arm grips and maintains through the frames. Reading the bottom's hip turn becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Switch sides, re-pin</b>Follow the escape.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">When the bottom frames the knee hard, the top switches to the far side or re-pins, staying on top through the turn. Mobility, not weight, keeps the position.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Attack the arm, step to mount</b>Convert the perch.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top traps the pushing arm for an isolated-arm attack, or steps over to mount as the bottom turns. The conversion from perch to finish becomes the focus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from settled knee-on-belly, strikes live, until the top attacks or advances, or the bottom clears the knee. The strike threat makes the perch genuinely costly to escape.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is knee-on-belly balance, not weight?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Dumping weight onto the knee makes you <b>top-heavy and easy to bump off</b>. Riding light and tall keeps your base mobile, so you can switch sides instead of getting tipped.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What do you do when the bottom frames the knee hard?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Switch to the far side or re-pin</b>, and trap the pushing arm. A hard frame both opens the other side and exposes the arm.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> How does the top prove control here?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">By <b>starting an arm attack (3s) or stepping to mount</b>, not by perching. The perch is the means; the attack or advance is the win.</div>
  </details>
</div>

      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Frame the knee and hip, turn to your side, pull the knee across, avoid pushing with a straight arm.</p></div>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🪶</div><div class="emma-card__t">Light or heavy?</div><p>Ask the top: "Were you balancing or dumping weight?" A heavy knee gets bumped; a light ride switches and survives.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔗</div><div class="emma-card__t">Attack or perch?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you start an attack or just sit up there?" Keeps the position converting to a finish.</p></div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by an attack or an advance</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Forces a finish, no perching</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">No-hands level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates the balance the position lives on</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Switching sides allowed</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards mobility over dead weight</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or finish</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the top, a real window for the bottom</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the no-hands level isolates the balance the whole position depends on, then grips, mobility, and attacks are layered on against a live, framing opponent. The attack-or-advance win keeps the representativeness, knee-on-belly is a strike-and-pass perch, not a hold.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>The bottom's frame pressure → when to switch sides and which arm is exposed.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own balance over the knee → whether you're light enough to ride or about to be bumped.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The bottom's hip turn → the step-to-mount window and the arm to attack.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>starts an attack or advances faster than the bottom clears the knee</b>. Track attacks and advances vs. knee-clears, and whether the top rides light and switches rather than getting bumped. The attack-or-advance versus clear-the-knee race is the order parameter.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the knee-on-belly perch in MMA, ride for strikes, switch with the frame, and step to mount or attack an arm before the bottom recovers.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: balance first</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the top side of <a href="../ground-control/">Ground Control</a>; the step-over feeds <a href="../mount-maintenance/">Mount Maintenance</a>; the arm trap feeds <a href="../side-control-ride/">Side-Control Ride</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Rides light and tall, balanced over the knee</li>
      <li>Switches sides on a hard frame instead of getting bumped</li>
      <li>Traps the arm the bottom pushes with</li>
      <li>Steps to mount when the bottom turns</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Dumps weight and gets tipped off</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Perches without attacking</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Never switches sides on the frame</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Reaches and loses the balance</span>
    </div>
