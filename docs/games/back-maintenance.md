    The top-side mirror of [Back Escape](../back-escape/). Sourced from the Slime Mold Grappling Club catalog (Greg Souders / Standard Jiu-Jitsu), re-expressed with our threshold rules. Passed the build rubric on paper; awaits validation against a live grappling class.

<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Advanced</span><span>Control → Finish</span></div>

Keep the back and slide the choking arm under the chin before the bottom turns to face.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top chest-to-back in a seatbelt, both hooks in; bottom seated against the chest, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top follows the hips, keeps the seatbelt, and works the choking arm under the chin; bottom defends and turns.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Choking arm under the chin 3s → top · Turn in to face, reverse, or stand → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The back is a race, <br><span class="accent">not a resting place.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Stay glued chest-to-back, follow the hips, and swim the arm to the throat. <b>If you ride for time, they turn to the front.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>bottom's hands and the side they turn toward</i>, the moment their hand-fight opens a lane to the throat or their shoulders rotate to escape. That specifies <i>when to swim the choking arm</i> and <i>which hook to weight</i>, not a memorized sequence. When they commit to turning one way, the chin opens on the other.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (attacker, on the back), one bottom (defender).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top chest-to-back in a seatbelt (one arm over the shoulder, one under the armpit, hands clasped), both hooks in; bottom seated against the chest.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top keeps the back and works the choke; bottom defends the neck and turns to face.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from seatbelt and hooks; reset on the arm under the chin, an escape, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>

    <span class="emma-pop">Stay chest-to-back, follow the hips, keep the seatbelt tight, weight the bottom hook. Bait the hand-fight, then swim the choking arm under the chin. Control is proven by closing on the neck, not by riding for time.</span>

    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to defend the neck, strip a hook, and turn in to face (or reverse, or stand).</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Two hands on the choking arm, chin tucked. Slide the hips down, strip a hook, then turn toward the choking-arm side. Survival first, then escape.</span>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by the arm under the chin<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by sliding the choking arm across the throat and under the chin (the closing position every rear-naked choke shares), held 3 seconds, not by sitting on the back. Stop at the closing position, before any squeeze.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom wins by facing<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom wins by turning in to face, reversing, or standing clear. Stripping a hook and facing kills the back, the choke can no longer be closed from the front. Mirrors <a href="../back-escape/">Back Escape</a>.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or finish<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps the back for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without getting the arm under the chin, the round resets. If the bottom turns to face first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so both players read the seatbelt and the hand-fight before strikes are added. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound from the back: strikes are the disincentivization tool that punishes a passive defense. Ground games train smashing, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top slides the choking arm under the chin (3s) → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">The choking hand crossed the throat and under the jaw, the shared closing of the rear-naked choke, held three seconds. The observable proof that back control beat the hand-fight. Stop at the closing position, before any squeeze.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom turns in to face, reverses, or stands → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">The turn is graded: chest-to-chest is the floor, on top is better, standing clear is best. Mirrors <a href="../back-escape/">Back Escape</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds the back the full count, no arm under the chin → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the back but never got under the chin before the count expired. Resets from seatbelt and hooks.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Chest-to-back, no hands linked</b>Stay glued.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top keeps chest-to-back contact and follows the hips with no seatbelt or hooks yet, just connection. Builds the glued-to-the-back base that makes everything else possible.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Connect hips and shoulders</b>Seatbelt on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds the seatbelt and weights the bottom hook, controlling the upper body and hips together. Reading the bottom's turn becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Seatbelt and both hooks</b>Maintain through the scramble.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Full back control: seatbelt and both hooks, maintained as the bottom hand-fights and slides the hips. Staying on the back through the escape is the focus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>One hook, hunt the chin</b>Trade a hook for the choke.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top drops to one hook and commits to swimming the choking arm under the chin, accepting the lower control to chase the closing. The control-to-finish trade becomes the skill.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from seatbelt and hooks, strikes live, until the top gets under the chin or the bottom turns to face. Finish slow, tap early. The bottom's turn is now urgent.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is the back a race rather than a resting place?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">If the top rides for time, the bottom <b>strips a hook and turns to face</b>, killing the position. The top has to <b>close on the neck</b> before the turn, the win is the arm under the chin, not the seconds held.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What opens the lane to the throat?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">The bottom's <b>commitment to turning one way</b>, or an over-eager hand-fight. When they turn one way, the chin opens on the other, swim the choking arm then.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does the top trade at Level 4, and why?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Drops to <b>one hook</b> to free the body to chase the choke. Less positional control, but the closing is the win, so the trade is worth it once the chin is there.</div>
  </details>
</div>

      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Two hands on the choking arm, chin tucked, slide the hips down, strip a hook, turn toward the choking-arm side.</p></div>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🏁</div><div class="emma-card__t">Chin or just riding?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you close on the chin, or ride for time?" Keeps the back a race for the finish, not a stall.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧲</div><div class="emma-card__t">Glue and follow</div><p>Ask the top: "When did your chest peel off the back?" The instant the connection breaks is the instant the bottom turns.</p></div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by the arm under the chin</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Forces the chase to the finish, no stalling</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Under the chin, 3s, no squeeze</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">A clean, observable closing short of the choke</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or finish</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the top, a real turn window for the bottom</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, hand-fighting bottom</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the hand-fight and hip-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): each level adds one control layer (connection, seatbelt, hooks, then the chin chase) while the top keeps reading the bottom's hands and hips from a live opponent. The arm-under-the-chin win keeps the representativeness, back control only matters if it closes on the neck.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Seatbelt tension and the bottom's hand-fight → the lane to the throat and the hook to weight.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own chest connection and hooks → whether the back is still controlled, when to trade a hook for the chase.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The side the bottom turns toward → the open chin and which way to swim.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>gets the arm under the chin faster than the bottom strips a hook and turns</b>. Track chin-closings vs. turns to face, and whether the top stays glued chest-to-back through the scramble. The close-the-chin versus strip-and-turn race is the order parameter.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> keeping the back and finishing the strangle before the opponent turns to face, the exact problem on the back in MMA and grappling.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: control ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Mirror of <a href="../back-escape/">Back Escape</a>; deepens the top side of <a href="../ground-control/">Ground Control</a>; the chin-closing transfers to the rear-naked-choke finish game.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Stays glued chest-to-back through the bottom's hip-slides</li>
      <li>Weights the bottom hook to deny the turn</li>
      <li>Swims the chin when the bottom commits to a side</li>
      <li>Trades a hook for the closing only when the chin is there</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Rides for time instead of chasing the chin</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Chest peels off and the bottom turns</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Both hooks lost in the scramble</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Squeezes before the closing position</span>
    </div>
