    Fills the empty north-south bucket in the top-control grid. Sourced from the Slime Mold Grappling Club catalog (Greg Souders / Standard Jiu-Jitsu), re-expressed with our threshold rules. Passed the build rubric on paper; awaits validation against a live grappling class.

<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Control → Finish</span></div>

Pin from north-south, then transition to a side or trap an arm before the bottom brings the knees in.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top chest-to-chest north-south, elbows in the bottom's armpits, the bottom's head at the beltline, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top flattens, kills the knees, then transitions to side control or traps a kimura; bottom brings the knees in and sits up.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Transition and hold 3s, or a kimura trap → top · Knees in, sit up, or reverse → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">North-south is a doorway, <br><span class="accent">not a room.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Flatten the shoulders, then move to a side or trap the arm. <b>Sit in it too long and the knees come back in.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>bottom's shoulders and the gap to their hips</i>, the moment a shoulder lifts to turn or an arm slides up to frame. That shift specifies <i>which way to transition</i> and <i>when a kimura is there to trap</i>, not a memorized pin. When they bridge to bring the knees in, a side opens.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (attacker, north-south), one bottom (defender).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top chest-to-chest north-south, elbows in the bottom's armpits, the bottom's head near the top's beltline; bottom on the back.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top maintains the pin and transitions or traps an arm; bottom brings the knees in and recovers.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from settled north-south; reset on a held transition, a kimura, an escape, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to flatten the bottom, then transition to side control (held 3s) or trap a kimura.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Weight on the chest, elbows in the armpits to kill the frames, head light. When a shoulder lifts, switch to a side; when an arm slides up, trap the kimura. Control is proven by the transition or the trap, not by sitting north-south.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to bring the knees in, sit up, or reverse.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Frame into the hips, bridge and bring a knee across, sit up as the top's weight shifts. Keep the elbows tight so an arm can't be slid up into a kimura.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by a transition or a kimura<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by transitioning to in-line north-south or side control and holding 3 seconds, or by trapping a kimura (figure-four) on one arm. Sitting in north-south without a threat is a stall, not a win.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom wins by recovering<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom wins by bringing the knees in to recover guard, sitting up to the front, or reversing. A clean, observable bottom goal.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or finish<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps north-south for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without a held transition or a kimura, the round resets. If the bottom recovers first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so both players read weight and frames before strikes are added. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound. Strikes are the disincentivization tool that punishes a passive bottom. Ground games train smashing, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top transitions to a side (3s) or traps a kimura → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">A clean transition to in-line north-south or side control held three seconds, or a figure-four trapped on one arm. Either is the observable proof that the pin led somewhere. Finish the kimura slow, tap early.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom brings the knees in, sits up, or reverses → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Recovering the knees to guard, sitting up to the front, or reversing is the escape. See <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds north-south the full count, no threat → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the pin but never transitioned or trapped an arm before the count expired. Resets from settled north-south.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Flatten the pin</b>Kill the bridge.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top settles chest-to-chest north-south, weight on the sternum, and simply flattens the bottom so they can't bridge or turn. Builds the heavy pin everything rests on.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Arms-out pin</b>Bury the elbows.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top drives the elbows into the armpits and pins the arms out, killing the bottom's frames. Reading the bottom's shoulder lift becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Transition to a side</b>Walk the pin around.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top moves from north-south to side control or in-line and holds 3 seconds. Maintaining contact through the walk-around is the focus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Kimura trap</b>Trap the sliding arm.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">When the bottom slides an arm up to frame, the top traps it in a figure-four. The kimura entry that north-south sets up becomes the goal.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from settled north-south, strikes live, until the top transitions or traps an arm, or the bottom recovers. The strike threat makes the pin costly to sit under.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is north-south a doorway, not a room?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Sit in it too long and the bottom <b>brings the knees back in</b>. It is a transition point, the win is moving to a side or trapping an arm, not holding the pin.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What opens the transition to a side?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">A <b>lifted shoulder</b> or the bottom bridging to bring a knee in. When they commit to one direction, walk the pin to the open side.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What sets up the kimura from here?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">An <b>arm sliding up to frame</b>. Bury the elbows so the only arm that moves is one you can trap in the figure-four.</div>
  </details>
</div>

      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Frame into the hips, bridge and bring a knee across, sit up on the weight shift, keep the elbows tight.</p></div>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🚪</div><div class="emma-card__t">Move or sit?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you transition or trap, or just lie there?" Keeps north-south a doorway to the next thing.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⬇️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Elbows buried?</div><p>Ask the top: "Are the elbows in the armpits?" Buried elbows kill the frames and feed the kimura.</p></div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by a transition or a kimura</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Forces north-south to lead somewhere</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Transition held 3s</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">A clean, observable proof the move stuck</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Arms-out pin level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates frame-killing and the kimura entry</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or finish</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the top, a real window for the bottom</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): each level adds one layer (flatten, bury the arms, transition, trap) while the top reads the bottom's shoulders and frames from a live opponent. The transition-or-kimura win keeps the representativeness, north-south is a passage, not a destination.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>The bottom's bridge and frame pressure → which side opens and which arm is sliding up.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own chest weight and head position → whether the pin is heavy enough to walk around.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>A lifted shoulder, a sliding arm → the transition lane and the kimura trap.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>transitions or traps an arm faster than the bottom brings the knees in</b>. Track transitions and kimuras vs. recoveries, and whether the top keeps the elbows buried through the bottom's frames. The move-or-trap versus knees-in race is the order parameter.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> using north-south as a transition hub, moving to a side, attacking a kimura, or holding for strikes before the bottom recovers, the way the position is actually used in MMA and grappling.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: pin then move</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the top side of <a href="../ground-control/">Ground Control</a>; the transition feeds <a href="../side-control-ride/">Side-Control Ride</a> and <a href="../mount-maintenance/">Mount Maintenance</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Flattens the bottom and kills the bridge</li>
      <li>Buries the elbows to deny the frames</li>
      <li>Walks to a side on a lifted shoulder</li>
      <li>Traps the kimura when an arm slides up</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Sits in north-south without moving</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Head heavy, gets bridged off</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Elbows out, the bottom frames in</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Lets the knees come back in</span>
    </div>
