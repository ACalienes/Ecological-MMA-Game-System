<div class="emma-meta"><span>Format</span><span>Any Game</span><span>Self-Scaling</span><span>Win = Harder Start</span></div>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>The Win Ladder is a format, not a standalone game.</b> Take any asymmetric game and define a sequence of starting positions, from most favorable to least. Every time the attacker wins, their next round starts one rung lower. The game scales itself to the player, with zero coach intervention.</p>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Winning costs you your head start, <br><span class="accent">so every round finds your edge.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Success demotes you to a harder start; failure keeps you where you are. The player always plays at the rung they've earned. <b>Self-scaling, no coach required.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules of the Format</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🪜</span> Define the rungs first<span class="emma-chip__detail">Before playing, state the ladder: an ordered list of starting positions from most to least favorable for the attacker. Three to five rungs works; each must be a real, definable position.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬇️</span> Win = demote yourself<span class="emma-chip__detail">When the attacker wins, their next round starts one rung lower. The win condition and the defender's rules never change, only the attacker's starting advantage shrinks.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏸️</span> Lose = stay put<span class="emma-chip__detail">No demotion on a loss. The player sits at the hardest rung they can still win from, which is exactly the amber-zone difficulty the session wants.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Same win, every rung<span class="emma-chip__detail">The objective never changes between rungs, only the start does. If the win condition needs to change, that's a new game, not a ladder rung.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Worked Example: Wall Single-Leg (as played 2026-06-06)</p>

<p class="emma-hl">Start shoulder to shoulder on the wall. <b>Attacker</b> wins by putting one or both of the defender's hips on the ground for a meaningful period (knees and shoulders don't count). <b>Defender</b> wins by separating, reversing to top, or threatening a top neck strangle (guillotines, ninjas; finish the threat, win the round; let it go, lose it). <b>Strikes on, GnP dial-up by permission.</b> Each attacker win moves the next start down a rung:</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>High single</b>Leg up, grip under the knee.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The leg is hiked as high as the attacker wants, gripped under the knee, not past it (no high-crotch). The most favorable start on the ladder.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Body lock</b>Double unders, hands locked.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The attacker gets connected double underhooks; the defender sets up however they want, typically a whizzer plus head control.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Regular single</b>Hands connected, leg down.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Connected hands on the leg, but the defender's foot stays on the floor. Their base is back; the finish must be earned.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Low single</b>Knees on the ground.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The attacker starts kneeling on the mat with a low single. The hardest rung, earned by winning everything above it.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>The format trains calibration on top of the base game's information.</b> Each rung removes some of the attacker's pre-made connection, so the attacker must perceive <i>what the lost grip used to give them</i> and rebuild it mid-action, while the defender attunes to the <b>felt resistance through the remaining contact</b> to find the escape the new rung affords.</p>

??? note "Why this is a format, not a game"
    The ladder doesn't define a problem of its own: the base game supplies the start type, the win condition, and the information to read. What the ladder supplies is <b>difficulty regulation</b>: success demotes the winner's start, so each player self-selects into productive instability (the amber zone) without the coach adjusting anything. It is a control-parameter schedule you can lay over any asymmetric game, the same way <a href="../positional-battle/">Positional Sparring</a> lays a live competitive layer over a defined position. See <a href="../../principles/cla/session-design/">Session Design</a> for the two dials this format turns automatically.
