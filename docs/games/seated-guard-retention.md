<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Defensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Retention</span></div>

Keep your structure, make them post, punish the post.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom seated, feet inside the top's knees; top working to flatten or pass, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom keeps structure, causes posts, and punishes them; top flattens or passes.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Top dumped to hands or seat, or shoulders connected for the back entry, held 3s → bottom · Bottom flattened to the back or passed → top · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">A seated guard isn't lying down, <br><span class="accent">it's a fighting stance on the floor.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Structure first, then offense. <b>Every post they make is a handle you can punish.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>moment the top's weight leaves their feet</i>, the hand that touches the mat, the knee that drops, the lean past the toes. A post is committed weight, and committed weight can't defend itself. Your feet and frames exist to <i>cause</i> those posts; your offense lives in the second after one lands.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (seated guard), one top (passer).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Bottom seated, feet between the top's knees, hands ready; top kneeling or crouched, working forward.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom retains, causes posts, and punishes them; top flattens the bottom or passes.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin seated and connected; reset on a dump, a back entry, a flatten, a pass, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Seated Guard)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep the seated structure, off-balance the top onto hands or seat, or connect to the shoulders for the back entry.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Sit tall, feet live, hands fighting. Don't fall to the back, the seated posture IS the guard. Push and pull until a hand hits the mat, then attack the arm that posted before it recovers.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Passer)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to flatten the bottom to the back or pass around the feet to a held pin.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Drive through the center or beat the feet around the side, but keep the weight over your own base. Every reach is a post the bottom is waiting for, advance with your legs, not your hands.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🪑</span> Stay seated, that's the guard<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom plays from the seated posture. Falling flat to the back concedes the round's central resource. Getting put flat is how the top wins, don't volunteer it.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⚖️</span> Bottom wins by the dump or the shoulders<span class="emma-chip__detail">Two observable wins: off-balance the top to hands or seat, or connect both hands at the top's shoulder line (the back-take entry) held 3 seconds. Both are concrete events, no judgment call.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by flatten or pass<span class="emma-chip__detail">Press the bottom's shoulders flat to the mat, or pass the legs to a chest-to-chest pin held 3 seconds. Hovering over the feet wins nothing.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so the bottom can learn the post-punish rhythm without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. The top's strikes punish a dead seated guard, the bottom's structure must handle punches, not just grips. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom dumps the top to hands or seat → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">The top's hands or seat touching the mat is the observable off-balance. The sweep mechanic, proven without needing to follow up.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom connects both hands at the shoulder line, held 3s → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Hands connected at the top's shoulders with the hips outside the arms, the doorway to the back. Held 3 seconds, it proves the entry was real.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Top flattens the bottom or passes to a held pin → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">Shoulders pressed flat, or chest-to-chest past the legs held 3 seconds. Either ends the seated guard's round.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Hold the structure</b>Feet in, posture tall.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The round is only retention: bottom keeps the seated posture with feet inside, top works to flatten or clear the feet. Whoever owns the structure at the bell owns the round. No offense yet, learn what a strong seat feels like.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Cause posts</b>Make a hand touch the mat.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom adds the goal: push-pull until the top posts a hand or drops a knee wide. The round ends on the post. Teaches that posts are created, not waited for.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Punish posts</b>Own the arm that landed.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Now the post is the beginning: bottom must capture and hold the posted arm (2-on-1 or wrist-and-elbow) before it recovers. The one-second window after a post becomes the whole game.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>To the shoulders</b>Climb the captured arm.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">From the captured post, the bottom climbs the connection to the shoulder line (the back entry) or converts to the dump. The full retention-to-offense chain, grappling only.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on. The top's punches test the structure, the bottom's feet now manage range and base at once. The seated guard as MMA actually demands it.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is falling to your back a concession?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because <b>the seated posture is the guard</b>. Flat on the back, your feet lose their steering, your hands lose their fight, and the top's flatten-win is half done.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What is a post, and why is it your window?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">A post is <b>committed weight</b>, a hand on the mat, a knee dropped wide. Committed weight can't defend itself. The arm that posted is yours for about a second, take it before it recovers.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What are the bottom's two observable wins?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>The dump</b> (top's hands or seat touch the mat) and <b>the shoulder connection</b> (both hands at the shoulder line, 3 seconds, the back entry).</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> How should the top advance without feeding posts?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>With the legs, not the hands.</b> Weight stays over the base; every reach is a handle. Advance in angles the feet can't follow rather than leaning through them.</div>
  </details>
</div>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Seated Guard)</div><p>Sit tall, keep the feet live and inside, hand-fight, cause the post, capture it, climb it to the shoulders or convert to the dump.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Passer)</div><p>Advance with the base, deny handles, flatten through the center or beat the feet around the side into a held pin.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🪑</div><div class="emma-card__t">Still seated?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "When did you end up flat, and who put you there?" Distinguishes being flattened from volunteering.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Who made that post?</div><p>Ask both: "Did the post happen, or did the bottom cause it?" Directs attention to off-balancing as an action, not luck.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Retention-only opening level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Builds the structure before any offense exists</span></div>

      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Two observable bottom wins</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">The bottom is the protagonist, never a survivor</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Round cap</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">The top must engage, no hovering at the feet</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting passer</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the weight-commit read intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the cause → capture → climb ladder decomposes guard offense into perceptual stages against a live passer. The post-punish window trains the timing attunement that makes seated guards dangerous rather than merely durable.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Pressure through the feet and grips → which way the top's weight is leaning, when it passes their toes.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own seat and spine → whether the structure is tall or collapsing toward the back.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The top's hands → the post landing, the one-second window opening.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>creates and converts posts faster than the top can recover them</b>. Track dumps and shoulder connections vs. flattens and passes conceded, and whether posts get punished or just observed. When the bottom's offense reliably starts within a second of a caused post, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> playing guard against a passer who can also strike, the seated structure is the only guard posture that manages both the pass and the punch, which is why MMA guards sit up.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: post ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the retention side of <a href="../leg-reclaim/">Leg Reclaim</a>; mirrors <a href="../open-guard-pass/">Open Guard Pass</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Holds the seated structure under real forward pressure</li>
      <li>Causes posts on purpose, not by accident</li>
      <li>Captures the posted arm inside the window</li>
      <li>Converts captures into the dump or the shoulder connection</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Falls to the back at the first pressure</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Watches posts land without attacking them</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Feet go passive, stop steering</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Top hovers out of range to run the clock</span>
    </div>
