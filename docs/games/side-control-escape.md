<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Defensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Counter</span></div>

Recover a guard or get to your knees before side control advances.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom flat under side control, top chest-to-chest, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom frames, gets a knee in or turns to the knees; top holds and works toward mount or the back.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Knee back in (half guard) or better, turn up, or reverse → bottom · Advance to mount or back, or hold the full count → top · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The pin loosens when they reach, <br><span class="accent">that's your frame and your knee.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Make a frame, find the space, drive the knee through it. <b>The moment they climb to advance, the chest goes light.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>chest connection and the cross-face pressure</i>, where the top's weight loads and the instant it lightens as they reach for an underhook, the cross-face, or a climb to mount. That shift specifies <i>when the knee can come through</i> and <i>which way to turn</i>, not the top's hands in isolation. When they reach to advance, the pin opens.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (defender), one top (side control).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Bottom flat on the back, top chest-to-chest and perpendicular, hips low.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom frames and recovers; top maintains side control and works toward mount or the back.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from settled side control; reset on escape, advance, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to frame the cross-face, get a knee back in (half guard or better), turn to the knees, or reverse.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Build the near elbow and forearm frames against the cross-face and hip. Use them to make space, then shrimp the knee through or turn in to the knees. Frames first, then move, never bridge with the arms flat.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to hold side control and advance to mount or the back.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Stay chest-to-chest and heavy, kill the near frames, switch hips to follow the escape. Control is proven by advancing to mount or taking the back, not by resting on top.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom frames and escapes, no subs<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom works frames, hip escapes, and turns continuously. No submissions from the bottom, the task is recovery, not the counter-attack.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by advancing<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by improving to mount or taking the back, not by sitting still. This gives the top an active, observable target instead of a stall, and keeps the pressure realistic.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or escape<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps side control for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without advancing, the round resets. If the bottom recovers a guard, turns up, or reverses first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 3 are control only, so the bottom can read pressure and build frames without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. Use striking as a disincentivization tool, the cost that punishes stalling and lazy structure. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom gets a knee back between (half guard) or better, turns up to the knees, or reverses → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Recovery is graded: one knee in (half guard) is the floor, then both knees, both feet, then a closed loop. Turning up to the knees or a reversal is a full escape. See <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top advances to mount or takes the back → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">Improving the position past side control is the observable proof that the pin has beaten the frames, and it is the real danger the bottom is racing.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds side control the full count, no advance → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the pin but never improved before the count expired. The round resets from settled side control for another rep.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Chest control only</b>Hands and head, no grips.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top holds with hands and head on the chest, no underhook, no cross-face. The lightest pin, so the bottom can find the near elbow frame and the knee-in without fighting heavy grips first.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Add the far underhook</b>One arm threatens.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds a far-side underhook, threatening to climb and turn the corner. The bottom must keep the near frame while denying the underhook's lift. Reading the climb becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Add the cross-face</b>Heaviest pin.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds the cross-face, turning the bottom's head away and killing the near frame. Now the bottom must rebuild the frame against the cross-face before any escape, the full-weight problem.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from settled side control with light strikes on. The strike threat makes a slow frame costly, recover or turn up under real urgency.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> When does the chest connection go light enough to bring the knee through?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">When the top <b>reaches for the underhook, the cross-face, or climbs to advance</b>. Reaching to improve unloads the chest. <b>When they reach, the pin opens.</b></div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does the top win by advancing rather than just holding?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Improving to <b>mount or the back</b> is observable and is the real danger of side control. It gives the top an active target instead of a stall and keeps the bottom's frames honest.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What has to happen before any escape, once the cross-face is in?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Rebuild the near frame against the cross-face.</b> The cross-face turns your head and kills the frame, so the frame comes back first, then the space, then the knee.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What is the floor that counts as a recovered guard here?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>One knee back between (half guard).</b> More is cleaner (both knees, feet in, closed loop), and turning up to the knees or reversing is a full escape.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Build the near elbow and forearm frames, deny the cross-face and underhook, make space, shrimp the knee in or turn to the knees, chain the next frame when one fails.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Attacker)</div><p>Stay chest-to-chest and heavy, kill the near frames, switch hips to follow the escape, climb toward mount or the back.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧱</div><div class="emma-card__t">Frame before move</div><p>Ask the bottom: "Did the frame come first?" Stops the flat-armed bridge that just feeds the cross-face.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧗</div><div class="emma-card__t">Advance or rest?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you improve, or just sit?" Keeps the top climbing to mount or the back instead of stalling for the count.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top control stepped per level (chest → underhook → cross-face)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates one piece of the pin at a time</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by advancing</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the pressure realistic, gives the top an active target</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or escape</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the bottom, no stalling for the top</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">No strikes until the top level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Frees the bottom to read pressure before adding strike defense</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting top</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the pressure-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): stepping the top's control isolates one frame problem per level while the bottom keeps reading pressure and timing from a real, resisting opponent. The top's advance condition keeps the representativeness, this is the actual danger of side control.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Chest load and cross-face pressure → when the pin lightens and which way to turn.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own frame strength and hip position → whether space exists yet and which escape is open.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Top reaching or climbing to advance → the timing window to insert the knee or turn up.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>rebuilds a frame and recovers a knee faster than the top can advance or re-settle the cross-face</b>. Track recoveries and turn-ups vs. advances to mount or the back, and whether the near frame stays live as the top switches hips. The frame-and-recover versus settle-and-advance race is the order parameter; when the bottom consistently times the knee to the lightened pin, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> being pinned in side control and recovering guard or turning up before the top improves to mount, the back, or starts striking, the exact problem under side control in competition and in MMA.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: stepped control</span><span>no strikes L1-3</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the side-control level of <a href="../ground-escape/">Ground Escape</a>; pairs with <a href="../mount-escape/">Mount Escape</a> and feeds <a href="../leg-reclaim/">Leg Reclaim</a> and <a href="../ground-to-standing/">Ground to Standing</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Rebuilds the near frame even against the cross-face</li>
      <li>Times the knee to the top's reach, not randomly</li>
      <li>Picks knee-in vs. turn-up by where the space is</li>
      <li>Chains a second frame when the first fails</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Bridges with flat arms, no frame</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Gives up the back turning away</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Waits and survives instead of framing</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Lets the cross-face flatten the head</span>
    </div>
