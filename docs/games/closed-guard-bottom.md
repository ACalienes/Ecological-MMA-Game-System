<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Combined</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Break &amp; Attack</span></div>

Break their posture, then take the back, the sweep, or the top.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom in closed guard controlling posture; top working to posture up, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom keeps the posture broken and converts it; top rebuilds and opens the guard.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Sweep, climb to the back (chest-to-back, 3s), or a controlled finish threat → bottom · Posture rebuilt and guard opened → top · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">A closed guard that only holds on <br><span class="accent">is just a slow way to lose.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Broken posture is currency, spend it. <b>Every second their head is down, something is available.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>top's weight through your legs and grips</i>, where their hands are based and when their head crosses their hands. A head pulled past the hands has no base under it: that's the climb. Hands on your chest are posts to trap; hands on the mat are doors to the back. The guard tells you which attack is open, listen with the legs.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (closed guard), one top (posturing).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Bottom's ankles locked behind the top, grips on; top kneeling, posture contested.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom breaks posture and converts; top rebuilds posture and opens the guard.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin locked with grips contested; reset on a conversion, an open, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Closed Guard)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep the top's posture broken and convert it into a sweep, a back-climb, or a controlled finish threat.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Arms and legs pull together, the lock is an engine, not a belt. Trap a hand when it posts on your chest, climb the hips when the head comes down. Pick the attack the posture break offers, don't force a favorite.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Posturing)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to rebuild posture, win the grips, and open the guard.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Head up, spine stacked, hands fighting grips, never both hands on the mat. Posture is rebuilt in pieces: win the head first, then the grips, then the hips. Opening the guard from a broken posture isn't possible, stop trying.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔒</span> Posture is the resource<span class="emma-chip__detail">The round revolves around the top's posture: broken, the bottom attacks; rebuilt, the top opens. Both sides should always know who owns it right now.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧗</span> The back is climbed, not jumped<span class="emma-chip__detail">The back-take counts when the bottom's hips climb outside the top's arms and reach chest-to-back, held 3 seconds. The climb route (hips to shoulder line) is the skill being trained.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🛑</span> Finish threats are positions<span class="emma-chip__detail">A finish threat wins when the controlling position is locked (arm trapped and extended, or a closed loop on the neck), then everyone stops. We prove the position, never the crank. Tap early, finish nothing fast.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A locked guard that attacks nothing is a stall, the cap punishes it. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so the bottom can read posture and base without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. A postured top now strikes down, breaking posture becomes strike defense too. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom sweeps to top → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">A reversal that lands on top. The broken posture converted into position the most direct way.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom climbs to the back, chest-to-back held 3s → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Hips outside the arms, chest to the top's back, held. The highest-value conversion and the one the climb levels build toward.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom locks a controlled finish threat → bottom wins, stop there.<span class="emma-pop">An arm trapped and extended or a closed loop on the neck, position proven, nothing cranked. Play stops on the lock.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Top rebuilds posture and opens the guard → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">Head up, grips won, ankles unlocked. From here the position is the closed-guard-pass game, that's the next page.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Own the posture</b>Keep them broken, nothing else.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The round is only the posture war: bottom keeps the top's head down with arms and legs together, top rebuilds. Whoever owns the posture at the bell owns the round. The engine of every closed-guard attack, isolated.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Trap the posts</b>Capture the basing hand.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">When the top bases a hand on your chest or the mat, trap it (wrist plus elbow, or 2-on-1) before it pulls back. A trapped post means the next posture break has nothing to catch it.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Hips to the shoulder</b>Climb outside the arms.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">From the broken posture and a trapped arm, the bottom climbs the hips outside the top's arms toward the shoulder line. The doorway position to the back, learned as its own destination first.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Convert</b>Back, sweep, or finish threat.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The full task, grappling only: break, trap, climb, and convert to whichever win the top's reaction offers. Forcing one favorite attack into every posture break is the tell this level exists to remove.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on. The top strikes from posture, so breaking posture now disarms as well as attacks. Closed guard the way MMA prices it.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is a holding-only closed guard a slow loss?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because the top gets unlimited posture-rebuild attempts against a passive lock, and in MMA, a postured top strikes. <b>Broken posture is currency: spend it on an attack or lose it.</b></div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What do the top's hands tell you?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Hands based on your chest are posts to trap; hands on the mat are doors to the back.</b> And a head pulled past the hands has no base under it, that's the climb.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What makes the back-take count?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Hips climbed outside the arms, chest-to-back, held 3 seconds.</b> The climb route is the skill; jumping past it into a scramble proves nothing.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> In what order does the top rebuild posture?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Head first, then grips, then hips.</b> Trying to open the guard from a broken posture feeds every attack the bottom has.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Closed Guard)</div><p>Break posture with arms and legs together, trap the posts, climb the hips outside the arms, convert to whichever win the reaction offers.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Posturing)</div><p>Rebuild head-grips-hips in order, never base both hands on the mat, open the guard only from a won posture.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">💱</div><div class="emma-card__t">What did you buy?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "You broke the posture three times, what did it buy you?" Posture breaks that purchase nothing are the stall in disguise.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧗</div><div class="emma-card__t">Which door opened?</div><p>Ask after each conversion: "What did the top do that made that the right attack?" Trains attack selection from the reaction, not the repertoire.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Posture-war opening level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Makes the round's central resource explicit before attacks exist</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Trap-the-post level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Same post-punishing grammar as the other guard games, compounding transfer</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Climb proven by chest-to-back 3s</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards the route, not the scramble</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Three win doors for the bottom</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Attack selection becomes the trained skill</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Round cap</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Punishes the hold-and-wait guard directly</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the break → trap → climb → convert ladder sequences closed-guard offense as perceptual stages, and the three-door win structure keeps the attack choice coupled to the top's live reaction instead of a rehearsed sequence.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Top's weight through the legs and grips → posture owned or rebuilding, hips light or heavy.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own hip height relative to the top's arms → whether the climb doorway is open.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Head past the hands, hands on chest or mat → which of the three doors just opened.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>converts posture breaks before the top rebuilds</b>. Track conversions (sweep / back / threat) per posture break, and which door each conversion used. When the attack consistently matches the reaction, three different doors across a session, the selection skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the closed guard as MMA actually prices it, a position you must attack from immediately, because a postured top turns it into a striking platform.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: door ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Builds on <a href="../seated-guard-retention/">Seated Guard Retention</a>'s post-punishing; mirrors <a href="../closed-guard-pass/">Closed Guard Pass</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Keeps posture broken with arms and legs together</li>
      <li>Traps posts before they recover</li>
      <li>Climbs the hips outside the arms on the route</li>
      <li>Picks the door the reaction offers, all three across a session</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Holds the lock and waits</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Forces the same attack into every break</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Climbs inside the arms, gives the head back</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Top bases both hands on the mat</span>
    </div>
