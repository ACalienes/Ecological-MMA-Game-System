<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Passing</span></div>

Win your posture, open the legs, pass to chest-to-chest.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top kneeling inside a closed guard, posture contested, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top breaks the grips, opens the feet, and passes; bottom breaks posture and attacks.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Chest-to-chest past the legs, held 3s → top · Sweep, climb to the back, or break posture to a finish threat → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The guard doesn't open from the legs, <br><span class="accent">it opens from your posture.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Win the head and the grips first. <b>A postured spine makes the locked feet cost more than they hold.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>pull on your posture</i>, where the bottom's grips and legs are dragging your head and hips. Every pull commits the bottom's hips to one side and tells you <i>which side to pressure</i> and <i>when the ankles unlock</i>. Posture regained is space created; pass into that space, not against the lock.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (passer), one bottom (closed guard).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top kneeling inside the bottom's closed guard, posture contested by grips.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top breaks grips, opens the guard, and passes; bottom controls posture and attacks.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from locked guard; reset on a pass, a sweep, a back-take, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Passer)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to win posture, open the locked feet, and pass to a chest-to-chest pin past the legs.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Strip the grips before you stand. Keep the spine stacked over the hips, a pulled-down head is a sweep waiting to happen. Open the feet with posture and a stand, not by digging at the ankles from a broken position.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Guard)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to break the top's posture and convert it: sweep, climb to the back, or threaten a finish.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Grips plus legs work together, pull the head down as the hips climb. When the top stands, follow the hips, don't hug the ankles. A broken posture is your window: attack it before it rebuilds.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧍</span> Posture before passing<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top's first job is winning the grips and the spine. Trying to force a pass from a broken posture feeds sweeps, that's the lesson, not a rule violation.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Pass proven by the hold<span class="emma-chip__detail">A pass counts when the top reaches chest-to-chest past the legs and holds it for 3 seconds. Flying past into a scramble proves nothing yet.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so both sides can read posture and grips without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. Use striking as a disincentivization tool, the cost that punishes a lazy posture. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top passes to chest-to-chest past the legs, held 3s → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">Both legs cleared and a chest-to-chest connection held for 3 seconds, the observable proof the guard is beaten, not just briefly evaded.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom sweeps to top, climbs to the back (chest-to-back, 3s), or breaks posture into a finish threat → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Any of the three converts the broken posture into something real: a reversal, the back, or a controlled finish position. The threat must be held, not just touched.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Round cap expires with the guard intact → reset, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Neither the pass nor the attack landed before the clock. Switch roles so both sides get the passing problem.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Win the grips</b>Strip and deny, posture only.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">No passing yet. Top works only to break the bottom's grips and keep a stacked spine; bottom works to keep posture broken. Whoever owns the posture at the bell owns the round. Isolates the grip-and-spine battle every pass starts from.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Open the feet</b>Make the ankles unlock.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top adds the next goal: force the locked feet open, by posture, knee position, or standing pressure. The round ends when the feet open or the cap expires. The bottom learns what an opening guard feels like before it costs them.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Stand to open</b>The standing break.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top must open the guard by standing. Standing changes every read: the bottom hunts the hips and ankles on the way up, the top learns to stand inside the guard without giving the sweep.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Open to pass</b>Past the knee line to chest.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The full task, grappling only: break, open, and pass to chest-to-chest held 3 seconds. Bottom fights every stage and wins by sweep or back-climb.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on for both sides. Posture now protects your jaw as well as your base, and the bottom's broken-posture attacks carry real urgency. Pass under fire.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What has to be won before the feet will open?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>The grips and the posture.</b> A stacked spine over the hips makes the locked feet cost more than they hold. Digging at the ankles from a broken posture feeds the sweep.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does the pass require a 3-second chest-to-chest hold?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because flying past the legs into a scramble proves nothing. The <b>held connection</b> is the observable proof the guard is actually beaten.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does every pull on your posture tell you?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">It tells you <b>where the bottom's hips committed</b>. A pull is information: pressure the side it came from, and pass into the space posture creates.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> When the top stands, what should the bottom chase?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>The hips, not the ankles.</b> Hugging the ankles surrenders the connection that powers sweeps and back-climbs. Follow the hips up and attack the base.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Passer)</div><p>Strip grips, stack the spine, open the feet with posture or a stand, pass past the knee line to chest-to-chest and hold it.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Guard)</div><p>Break posture with grips and legs together, follow the hips when the top stands, convert every broken posture into a sweep, a back-climb, or a finish threat.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧍</div><div class="emma-card__t">Where is your head?</div><p>Ask the top: "Was your head over your hips when you tried to open?" Directs attention to posture as the opener, not hand strength.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⏳</div><div class="emma-card__t">What did the break buy?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "You broke the posture, what did you do with it?" A broken posture that isn't attacked rebuilds for free.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Posture-only and open-only levels</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Isolates the grip battle and the opening before the pass exists</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Pass proven by a 3s chest-to-chest hold</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards control over scramble-luck, observable for the coach</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Bottom wins by sweep, back, or finish threat</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the bottom dangerous, the top's posture honest</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Round cap</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the top, no guard-sitting for the bottom</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting guard</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the posture-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): each level holds one slice of the passing problem (grips → opening → standing → passing) against a fully resisting opponent, so the perception-action loop never gets replaced by a drilled sequence.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Pull on the collar ties and sleeves, the drag on the spine → which side the bottom's hips committed to.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own head-over-hips alignment → whether the posture is actually won or about to fold.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The bottom's hip angle and knee line → which pass lane is opening as the feet unlock.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>rebuilds posture faster than the bottom can convert a break</b>. Track passes held vs. sweeps and back-climbs conceded, and whether the guard opens from posture or from ankle-digging. When passes start arriving through the stand-and-open route under a live guard, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> being locked inside a competent closed guard, the canonical top-side problem in grappling exchanges and in MMA once strikes stall.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: staged opening</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the passing side of <a href="../ground-access/">Ground Access</a>; mirrors <a href="../closed-guard-bottom/">Closed Guard Bottom</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Strips grips before attempting to open</li>
      <li>Opens the guard from a stacked posture, standing when needed</li>
      <li>Passes to chest-to-chest and holds, not through, the position</li>
      <li>Survives broken posture without conceding the sweep</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Digs at the ankles with a bent spine</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Stands without controlling the grips first</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Sprints past the legs without securing the chest</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Stalls inside the guard waiting for the cap</span>
    </div>
