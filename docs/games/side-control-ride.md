<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Control → Finish</span></div>

Hold side control and turn it into an arm or a better position before the bottom frames a knee back in.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Top chest-to-chest in side control, near arm under the head, far under the armpit, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Top kills the frames and isolates a bent arm, or advances to mount; bottom frames and recovers.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Bent-arm control 3s or advance to mount → top · Knee in, turn up, or reverse → bottom · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">A pin that goes nowhere <br><span class="accent">is just a rest.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Stay chest-to-chest, kill the near frames, then climb to the arm or to mount. <b>Prove control by advancing, not by sitting.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>bottom's near elbow and the gap at the hip</i>, where their frames load and the instant one arm drifts off the body. That shift specifies <i>when a bent arm is there to trap</i> and <i>when the hips can switch to mount</i>, not a memorized pin. When they push to frame, the arm they push with is the arm to take.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one top (attacker, side control), one bottom (defender).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Top chest-to-chest and perpendicular, near arm under the head, far arm under the armpit, hands connected; bottom flat.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Top maintains side control and works a bent-arm or an advance; bottom frames and recovers.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from settled side control; reset on a bent-arm hold, an advance, an escape, or the count.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Attacker)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to hold side control and isolate a bent arm (kimura or americana), or advance to mount.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Stay chest-to-chest and heavy, kill the near elbow and hip frames, switch your hips to follow the escape. When a frame extends an arm, trap it in a figure-four. Control is proven by the bent arm or the advance, not by resting.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Defender)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to frame, get a knee back in, turn to the knees, or escape to seated or full guard.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Keep the near elbow tight so it can't be isolated, frame the cross-face and hip, shrimp the knee through. Don't extend an arm into space, that's the arm they trap.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Top wins by a bent arm or an advance<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top proves control by isolating one arm in a bent-arm figure-four (kimura / americana) held 3 seconds, or by improving to mount. Holding side control without a threat is a stall, not a win.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤜</span> Bottom wins by recovering<span class="emma-chip__detail">The bottom wins by getting a knee back in (half guard or better), turning up to the knees, escaping to seated or full guard, or reversing. Mirrors <a href="../side-control-escape/">Side-Control Escape</a>.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Hold the count or finish<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the top keeps side control for the set count (start at 20 seconds) without a bent arm or an advance, the round resets. If the bottom recovers first, the bottom wins. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are control only, so both players read frames and weight before strikes are added. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. Strikes are the disincentivization tool that punishes a lazy bottom structure. Ground games train smashing, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Top isolates a bent arm (3s) or advances to mount → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">A figure-four on one arm (kimura or americana) held three seconds is the shared entry to the side-control submission game; advancing to mount is the positional win. Either is the observable proof that the pin beat the frames. Finish slow, tap early.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Bottom recovers a knee, turns up, or reverses → bottom wins, switch roles.<span class="emma-pop">Recovery is graded: a knee in (half guard) is the floor, then seated or full guard, then a reversal. See <a href="../../concepts/guard-recovery/">Guard Recovery</a>.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Reset</span> Top holds side control the full count, no threat → reset, same roles.<span class="emma-pop">The top kept the pin but never isolated an arm or advanced before the count expired. Resets from settled side control.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Chest-to-chest pin</b>Deny the rotation.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top settles chest-to-chest and simply denies the bottom turning in or framing a knee through. Control only. Builds the heavy connection everything else rests on.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Isolate one arm</b>Trap the bent arm.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top reads an extended frame and traps that arm in a figure-four (kimura / americana), held 3 seconds. Reading which arm the bottom commits becomes the task.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Double-arm threat</b>Attack both sides.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top hunts the bent arm on either side, switching the attack as the bottom defends one. Keeps the bottom's elbows honest on both sides at once.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Open side to mount</b>Switch the hips and climb.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Top loosens to a knee-on-belly or open side control and advances to mount as the base lightens. Adds the positional advance as a second win path.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Continuous from settled side control, strikes live, until the top isolates an arm, advances, or the bottom recovers. The bottom's escapes are now urgent.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does the top win by a bent arm or an advance, not by holding?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Holding is a <b>stall</b>. A bent arm or a climb to mount is the <b>observable proof</b> the pin is doing work, and it keeps the top hunting instead of resting.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What hands the top the arm to trap?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">An <b>extended frame</b>. When the bottom pushes with a straightening arm, that arm is exposed, trap it in the figure-four.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does staying chest-to-chest buy the top?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">It <b>kills the bottom's space to turn in</b> and keeps the weight connected, so every frame the bottom makes has to fight your chest first.</div>
  </details>
</div>

      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Defender)</div><p>Keep the near elbow tight, frame the cross-face and hip, shrimp the knee through, never extend an arm into space.</p></div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🔗</div><div class="emma-card__t">Arm or advance?</div><p>Ask the top: "Did you take the arm or improve, or just sit?" Keeps the ride hunting a finish instead of stalling for the count.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧲</div><div class="emma-card__t">Stay connected</div><p>Ask the top: "Where did your chest go light?" Direct attention to the connection, the moment it lifts is the moment the bottom turns in.</p></div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Top wins by a bent arm or an advance</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Forces an active finish, no stalling</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Bent-arm hold, 3s</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">A clean, observable control proof short of a crank</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Hold the count or finish</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Urgency for the top, a real window for the bottom</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, framing bottom</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the frame-reading perception intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): each level isolates one control variable (deny rotation, isolate one arm, both arms, advance) while the top keeps reading frames from a live, resisting opponent. The arm-or-advance win keeps the representativeness, side control only matters if it leads somewhere.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the top reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>The bottom's frames and the gap at the hip → which arm is exposed, when the hips can switch.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own chest connection and base → whether the pin is still heavy, when a climb is safe.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>An extended arm, a turning shoulder → the figure-four entry and the path to mount.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the top <b>isolates an arm or advances faster than the bottom recovers a knee or turns in</b>. Track arms and advances vs. escapes, and whether the top stays chest-to-chest through the bottom's frames. The trap-or-advance versus frame-and-recover race is the order parameter.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> holding side control and converting it to a submission or a better position before the bottom recovers, the exact problem on top in MMA and grappling.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: control ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>reset on the count</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Mirror of <a href="../side-control-escape/">Side-Control Escape</a>; deepens the top side of <a href="../ground-control/">Ground Control</a>; the bent-arm transfers toward the side-control submission game.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Stays chest-to-chest through the bottom's frames</li>
      <li>Traps the arm a frame extends, not a forced crank</li>
      <li>Switches hips to follow the escape</li>
      <li>Climbs to mount when the base lightens</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Sits and holds instead of threatening</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Chest lifts and the bottom turns in</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Cranks the arm instead of isolating it</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Never switches hips to follow</span>
    </div>
