<div class="emma-meta"><span>Open Space</span><span>Wrestling</span><span>Combined</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Center Clinch</span></div>

Win a dominant connection from a neutral 50/50 clinch, double-unders, a leg, or the back, or put them down, before they get theirs.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Both fighters in a neutral 50/50 clinch (one overhook, one underhook, diagonally matched), chest to chest, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Beat your partner to a dominant connection: double-unders locked, a hand to a leg above the knee, or the back, or off-balance them down while you stay standing.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Any clean connection or grounding → reset to 50/50. Strikes are live to tax the player who only defends.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Neutral doesn't mean wait. <br><span class="accent">Whoever attacks first owns the tie.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">The 50/50 is even by design. Flush your offense so they have to defend, and the connection is yours. <b>Especially against someone stronger, move first.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>felt load through the ties</i> (the inertial array), which way your partner's weight is committed and which post is holding them up. Drag to the unloaded side and the leg, the back, or the ground opens. Read the <i>shifting base</i> through your hands, not a memorized entry. When their hand drops to a leg or their head dips, the level change is coming.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, symmetric and neutral, neither one dominant to start.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>A 50/50 clinch: one overhook and one underhook each, diagonally matched (one per side), chest to chest. Lock in even before "go".</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter. Start one body-length off the wall, the wall is live but not automatic, it will help you or hurt you.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Both fighters have the same goals, it is a neutral race to the first dominant connection.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Lock the 50/50, then "go"; reset to 50/50 on any clean connection or grounding. A simultaneous connection is a reset, no score.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤼</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Fighter A (Connecting)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to beat your partner to a dominant connection: double-unders locked, a hand to a leg above the knee, the back, or an off-balance to the ground while you stay standing.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Don't hold the neutral tie and wait. Feel which post is loaded, drag to the light side, and pummel for the under. Blunted knees off the inside of the thigh and shown punches tax a partner who only defends. From a deep under you can lock the body, dip to a leg above the knee, or spin to the back.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤼</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Fighter B (Connecting)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Same goals, the mirror task: get to the same dominant connection first, and don't let your partner settle into theirs.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Defending alone has no path to a win here. Flush your own offense so your partner has to react, then take the under, the leg, the back, or the off-balance. Keep your hips back off the body lock, and use feet-to-feet contact to off-balance rather than out-muscle.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Double-unders + hands locked is a win<span class="emma-chip__detail">Both arms under, hands connected behind the back around the waist (a body lock). A momentary under without the lock does not count, the hands have to connect.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🦵</span> A hand to a leg above the knee is a win<span class="emma-chip__detail">Hands connected around a leg above the knee. A grab below the knee is an exposed low single, not a connection, it does not count. This is the hand-connection law.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔄</span> The back is the gold-star win<span class="emma-chip__detail">Get chest-to-back behind your partner. It is the best connection and the hardest to reach, score it whenever it is there.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬇️</span> Off-balance only scores if you stay standing<span class="emma-chip__detail">Put your partner's hips to the ground while you stay on your feet or end on top. If you both fall, it does not count, the game rewards staying over the grounded opponent.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🥊</span> Strikes are the stalling tax<span class="emma-chip__detail">Blunted Thai knees off the inside of the thigh and shown punches to antagonize. No elbows. The player who only defends gets antagonized, the strikes punish passivity so neutral never means waiting.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤝</span> Reset on connection<span class="emma-chip__detail">Any clean connection or grounding resets both fighters to the neutral 50/50. A simultaneous connection is a reset with no score.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player leaves it, that player loses the round.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Either fighter</span> Double-unders locked, a leg above the knee, the back, or an off-balance to the ground while you stay standing → win and reset.<span class="emma-pop">Four paths to the same neutral win. The connection has to be real (hands locked, leg above the knee) or the grounding has to leave you on top.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Gold star</span> The back (chest-to-back) is the highest win, and the hardest.<span class="emma-pop">Behind your partner is always the best place to be. It is the hardest connection to earn, so it is the gold star whenever you can spin to it.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Leave the perimeter, or get put down while your partner stays on top → that round to them.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round. Getting off-balanced to your back while your partner stays standing is their grounding win.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Grips only</b>No strikes, find the weak post.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Start in the 50/50, no strikes. Pummel for the under and drag to the unloaded side. Connections only, no grounding yet. Finds the felt-load read.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Add the ground</b>Off-balance is now live.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The off-balance to the ground becomes a win, but only if you stay standing or end on top. Feet-to-feet contact and side-to-side drags, not slams.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Strikes on</b>The anti-stall tax.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Blunted knees off the thigh and shown punches come on. The player who only defends now pays for it. Neutral becomes urgent.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Wall live</b>Start a step off the wall.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Begin one body-length off the wall. Drag your partner onto it to pin and connect, or get dragged onto it yourself. The wall helps or hurts.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>All wins live, short rounds.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Neutral 50/50, every win path live, strikes on, the wall available. Lock in even, then move first, connect, reset, go again.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is "neutral" not the same as "wait"?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">The 50/50 is <b>even by design</b>. The fighter who attacks first forces the other to defend, and a pure defender has <b>no path to the connection</b>. Flush your offense first so the opening is yours.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does the felt load through the ties tell you?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Which post is <b>loaded</b> and which is <b>light</b>. Drag to the light side and the base falls, opening the under, the leg, or the back. You read the shifting base through your hands, not a memorized throw.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does a leg grab below the knee not count?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">It is the <b>hand-connection law</b>: a connection is around the waist or a leg <b>above the knee</b>. Diving below the knee is an exposed low single, not control, so it does not score.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What stops two defensive players from stalling forever?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">The <b>strikes</b>. A player who only defends eats blunted knees and shown punches, the constraint that taxes passivity. It is a built-in cost, not a "stop stalling" instruction from the coach.</div>
  </details>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Go Deeper</p>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each fighter's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤼</div><div class="emma-card__t">Connecting (both sides)</div><p>Move first from neutral, feel the loaded post, drag to the light side, pummel to the under, then lock the body, dip to a leg above the knee, spin to the back, or off-balance to the ground while staying on top.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⚖️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Don't out-muscle, off-balance</div><p>Use feet-to-feet contact and side-to-side motion to break the base. Against a stronger partner, motion beats strength, the grip that waits loses.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⚡</div><div class="emma-card__t">Flush your offense</div><p>Ask: "Are you attacking, or just stopping their attack?" A defender has no win here. Make your partner react first.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥊</div><div class="emma-card__t">Strikes are the tax, not decoration</div><p>The blunted knees and shown punches exist to punish stalling. If nobody is attacking, the antagonizing strikes should make that uncomfortable, fast.</p></div>
    </div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Neutral 50/50 start, no edge</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">The skill is creating the first connection, not exploiting a gift</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Reset on any connection</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Repeated reps of the highest-value clinch moment, the first exchange</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Leg connection only above the knee</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Trains real control, not a desperate low dive</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Off-balance scores only if you stay standing</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards top control, not mutual falling</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Strikes live as the stalling tax</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Makes the neutral tie urgent, a constraint that punishes passivity instead of a cue</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Representative Learning Design</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the neutral over-under start models the most common clinch moment in MMA, the 50/50 before anyone has an edge, and the multiple win paths let the fighter self-organize the entry that the felt load affords rather than rehearsing one prescribed throw.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What athletes read</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Load through the ties (the inertial array) → which post is committed, which way to drag, when the under is winning.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own hips square or back, base loaded or light → whether you can be body-locked, and when the spin to the back is open.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>A dropped hand or a dipped head → the leg or the level change coming, and the post to attack.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the fighter <b>creates the first dominant connection by moving first and dragging to the unloaded side</b>, rather than holding the neutral tie and waiting. Track first-connection wins versus stalls, and whether the fighter stays in motion. When the connection reliably comes from moving first, the skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the neutral over-under (50/50) clinch exchange that opens almost every MMA clinch, the moment before anyone owns an underhook, the body, the back, or the takedown.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Neutral 50/50 start</span><span>four win paths</span><span>strikes as the tax</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Follows <a href="../pressure-to-clinch/">Pressure to Clinch</a>; the off-balance and body-lock wins feed <a href="../body-lock-to-ground/">Body Lock to Ground</a>; the back win feeds <a href="../back-maintenance/">Back Maintenance</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Attacks first from neutral, never just defends</li>
      <li>Drags to the weak-side post to win the under</li>
      <li>Connects only above the knee, no low dives</li>
      <li>Stays standing on the off-balance, ends on top</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Holds the neutral tie and waits</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Dives below the knee for a leg</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Both fall and claims the grounding</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Only defends, never attacks</span>
    </div>
