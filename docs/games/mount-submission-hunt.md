!!! warning "Provisional (WIP): invented in class 2026-06-20, pending final coach sign-off"

    Built on the mat on 2026-06-20 as the finishing layer of the mount ladder ([Mount Escape](../mount-escape/) → [Mount Maintenance](../mount-maintenance/) → this). It trains converting a held mount into an observable submission entry, the first dedicated submission-offense game in the library. Refined from the class version (back-take and fatigue rules clarified). Awaiting a final pass before the WIP tag comes off.

<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Offensive</span><span>Advanced</span><span>Submission Entry</span></div>

Isolate the finish from mount, one arm over the head or the back sealed, before they frame, recover a leg, and get out.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Attacker fully mounted; defender sets their own arm frames first, inside the mat.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Attacker isolates one arm above the head, or seals the back on top; defender denies the isolation and recovers a leg.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Arm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s, or back sealed on top 1-2s → attacker · One leg recovered, reversal, or stand → defender.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Mount is not the finish. <br><span class="accent">It is the doorway to it.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Don't lounge in the mount, hunt the isolation. Show the strike to open the frame, then take the arm over the head. <b>The position you hold is only worth the finish it leads to.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> where the <i>defender's frames are loaded</i>, the felt resistance of their arms against your hips and chest. A <i>shown strike</i> makes them cover, and a covering arm that crosses the centerline is the arm to chase over the head. Read the <i>committed frame</i>, not a memorized submission. When the layout shifts, a wobble, a turn, an exposed arm, the finish is suddenly afforded, take it.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one attacker (mounted), one defender (bottom).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Full mount. The defender sets their own arm frames before "go", they are not pre-pinned.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>The mat, out of bounds is a reset.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Attacker isolates the finish; defender denies the isolation and recovers a leg.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Mount set, defender frames, then "go"; reset on a finish, a leg recovered, a reversal, or a stand. <b>Keep the rounds short.</b></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Attacker (Finishing)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to isolate one arm above the defender's head with your hands locked (the arm-triangle / armbar entry), or seal the back on top if they turn.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Ride the hips high. Use a shown strike to make them cover, then trap the arm that crosses the centerline and walk it over the head, bicep to the ear. Don't lounge and hold, mount is only worth the finish. If they turn away, take the back, but you must seal it on top.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🛡️</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Defender (Denying &amp; recovering)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep both arms framed and connected, deny the over-the-head isolation, and get one leg back in to recover guard.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Elbows tight, never let an arm cross your own centerline. Frame the hips, shrimp, and fight one leg back between you, that is your win. A reversal or a stand is a bonus, but the first job is to deny the arm, not to escape clean every time.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Arm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s → attacker<span class="emma-chip__detail">One arm isolated fully above the defender's head with your hands connected, bicep visibly to the ear, the arm-triangle / armbar entry. Held one to two seconds to clear the scramble. If you have it, you have already won, you don't need to crank the finish.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🔄</span> Back sealed on top, 1-2s → attacker<span class="emma-chip__detail">If the defender turns away, chest-to-back with both hooks in and you on top, held one to two seconds. Your own back on the floor is not a seal yet, the defender can still escape, so it does not count.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🛡️</span> Defender's first win is deny + recover<span class="emma-chip__detail">Keep both arms in and get one leg back between you, that is guard recovered and the defender wins. This is the reachable goal that keeps the round honest, not a full clean escape every time.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬆️</span> Reversal or stand → defender bonus<span class="emma-chip__detail">Reversing to top or standing fully up is also a defender win, the bigger prize, but recovering one leg is enough.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🥊</span> Shown strikes only<span class="emma-chip__detail">Legitimate threat, no damage. An arc between strikes and a semi-legitimate reaction, no clean landing. No elbows or knees without pads. The strike opens the frame, it does not concuss a partner.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Short rounds, reset often<span class="emma-chip__detail">The finish-hunt is high intensity for both players. Keep the rounds short and reset on any outcome, fatigue is a dial we turn down here so the skill, not the gas tank, is the limit.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay on the mat<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside the mat perimeter. Rolling out of bounds is a reset.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Attacker</span> Arm over the head, hands locked, 1-2s, or the back sealed on top 1-2s → attacker wins.<span class="emma-pop">One arm fully above the head with your hands connected (bicep to ear), or, if they turned, chest-to-back with both hooks in and you on top. Either held one to two seconds. You don't crank the finish, the entry is the win.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Defender</span> Both arms kept in and one leg recovered → defender wins (reversal or stand is a bonus).<span class="emma-pop">Deny the over-the-head and fight one leg back to guard. That reachable win is the point, it keeps both players working instead of grinding to exhaustion. A reversal or a stand is the bigger bonus.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Attacker reversed or a leg let back in; defender's arm isolated over the head → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">For the attacker, losing the mount to a reversal or a recovered leg. For the defender, letting an arm get walked over the head and locked.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Positional, no strikes</b>Hunt the crossing arm.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Attacker rides high and hunts one arm over the head; defender frames and keeps elbows tight. No strikes yet. Finds the trap-the-crossing-arm mechanic.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Add the shown strike</b>Open the frame.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The shown strike comes on. The threat makes the defender cover, the covering arm crosses the centerline, and that is the arm to isolate. Legitimate threat, no damage.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Recovery live</b>Deny vs isolate.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The defender's recovery is now a win, one leg back in. The isolation-versus-recovery race becomes the task, and the defender finally has a reachable goal.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Add the back</b>The turn is punished.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">If the defender turns away to escape, the attacker may take and seal the back on top. Turning is no longer free, it costs the back.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Short rounds, all wins live.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Shown strikes on, the arm, the back, the recovery, the reversal, all live. Short rounds, reset often, hunt the finish without gassing.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is holding the mount not the goal?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Mount is <b>only worth the finish it leads to</b>. Lounging lets the defender rest and recover. The game rewards converting the position into an <b>observable submission entry</b>, not sitting in it.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> How does a shown strike help you isolate the arm?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">It makes the defender <b>cover</b>. The covering arm crosses their centerline, and a <b>crossed arm</b> is the one you can trap and walk over the head. The threat shifts the layout and opens the finish.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why is "took the back" not a win when your own back is on the floor?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because that is <b>not a stable, dominant position</b>, the defender can still escape. A back-take only scores as an <b>observable seal</b>: both hooks in, you on top, held 1-2s.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> As the defender, why is "deny and recover one leg" the goal instead of a full escape?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Against a secured mount, escaping clean every time is <b>unreachable and gasses you out</b>. Denying the arm and getting <b>one leg back to guard</b> is the reachable, winnable goal, it keeps you working instead of grinding to exhaustion.</div>
  </details>
</div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements the <a href="../../concepts/finishing-sequence/">Finishing Sequence</a>: the shown strike shifts the position's attractor layout so a new affordance, the crossing arm, opens up. The fighter attunes to the shift and takes the afforded finish, rather than computing a memorized submission. The position is trained as a doorway to the finish, not an end in itself.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What athletes read</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Load through the defender's frames against your hips and chest → which arm is committed, when the frame breaks, where the isolation is open.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own hips riding high and base wide → whether you are stable enough to attack an arm without being bridged off.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>A covering arm crossing the centerline, a turn away → the arm to chase over the head, or the back to seal.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the attacker <b>converts a held mount into an observable finish-entry by opening the frame and chasing the crossing arm</b>, rather than holding the mount and waiting. Track finish-entries versus lounging, and whether the defender denies-and-recovers versus gets isolated. When mount reliably becomes a finish entry, the submission-offense skill has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the real mount finishing sequence, the arm-triangle / armbar / back-take fork, the layer above simply holding the mount. The first dedicated submission-offense game, and the top of the mount ladder.</p>
