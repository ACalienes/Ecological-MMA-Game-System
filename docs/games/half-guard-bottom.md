<div class="emma-meta"><span>Ground</span><span>Grappling</span><span>Combined</span><span>Intermediate</span><span>Shield &amp; Sweep</span></div>

Hold the shield, break their base, take something better.

<div class="emma-arc">
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Bottom on the side with a knee shield framed in; top working to clear it, inside a marked perimeter.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">The Goal</span><b>Bottom causes posts, punishes them, and elevates; top clears the shield to chest-to-chest.</b></div>
  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Sweep, full guard, the back, or top elevated fully off the mat → bottom · Shield cleared to chest-to-chest, held 3s → top · Out of bounds → loss.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">The shield isn't a wall, <br><span class="accent">it's a lever.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">It holds them off only long enough to tilt them. <b>A shield that only blocks eventually gets crushed.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>pressure coming through the knee shield</i>. Light pressure means the top is basing back, safe but passive, attack their posture. Crushing pressure means their weight has committed past their knees, that's the elevation window. The shield is a pressure gauge: read it, don't just brace it.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one bottom (half guard, knee shield), one top (passer).</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Bottom on the side, knee shield across the top's chest or hip, one leg hooked; top driving to clear it.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A marked perimeter, both stay inside.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Bottom maintains the shield and converts pressure into offense; top removes the shield and flattens.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin with the shield set; reset on a sweep, a recovery, a clear, or the round cap.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🤸</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Bottom (Knee Shield)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to keep the shield framed, force and punish posts, and convert: sweep, full guard, the back, or elevate the top clean off the mat.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Stay on your side, shield angled, not flat. Use it as a lever: when their pressure commits, lift and turn. When they base back, sit up into them. Four wins, the shield is the start of all of them.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🥋</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Top (Passer)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to clear the knee shield and reach chest-to-chest, held 3 seconds.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Don't wrestle the shield head-on, change the angle until it points at nothing. Control the bottom knee, drop your hip below the shield line, and keep your weight behind your own knees while you do it.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🛡️</span> The shield is the resource<span class="emma-chip__detail">The round revolves around the knee shield: the bottom converts it into offense, the top works to remove it. Losing the shield isn't losing the round, but it's losing the lever.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬆️</span> Elevation counts<span class="emma-chip__detail">Lifting the top fully off the mat (both feet and hands clear) is a bottom win, the proof that the shield-lever and underhooks beat the base, even without finishing the sweep.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Clear proven by the hold<span class="emma-chip__detail">The top wins by removing the shield and reaching chest-to-chest, held 3 seconds. Brushing the knee aside into a scramble proves nothing yet.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⏱️</span> Round cap, no stalling<span class="emma-chip__detail">Run a set round cap (start at 60 seconds). If neither side wins by the cap, reset and switch roles. A clock, never "as long as possible".</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🚫</span> No striking until the top level<span class="emma-chip__detail">Levels 1 to 4 are grappling only, so the bottom can read pressure through the shield without defending strikes. Strikes enter at the full-expression level.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎚️</span> GnP dial-up, by permission<span class="emma-chip__detail">Once strikes are on, the coach explicitly grants a meaner dial on ground-and-pound: mid-grapple, strength is already compromised, so firmer strikes stay safe. The shield must now manage punches as well as pressure, exactly the MMA half guard. Ground games train smashing on the ground, not grappling for its own sake.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens inside a marked perimeter, any shape. If a player rolls fully out of it, that player loses the round, training mat-edge awareness.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom sweeps, recovers full guard, or takes the back → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Three conversions, each observable: a reversal to top, both legs back around (full guard), or chest-to-back. The shield did its job as a lever.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Win</span> Bottom elevates the top fully off the mat → bottom wins.<span class="emma-pop">Feet and hands clear of the floor at once. The base is beaten even if the sweep isn't finished, an honest, observable proxy that scales safely.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Switch</span> Top clears the shield to chest-to-chest, held 3s → top wins.<span class="emma-pop">The shield removed and the chest connection held. From here the position is the half-guard-pass game's flatten-and-free problem.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Loss</span> Roll fully out of the perimeter → that player loses.<span class="emma-pop">Crossing the marked perimeter loses the round instantly, regardless of position, training the mat-edge awareness a fighter needs.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Hold the shield</b>Maintain the frame only.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The round is only maintenance: bottom keeps the shield framed and the side position, top works to clear it. Whoever owns the shield at the bell owns the round. Learn the angles that survive pressure.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Cause posts</b>Tilt them onto a hand.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom adds push-pull through the shield and grips: the round ends when the top posts a hand or knee wide. The shield becomes a steering tool, not a wall.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Punish posts</b>Hips to the captured side.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">The post is the start: bottom captures the posted arm (2-on-1) and moves the hips toward it, the doorway to the back and the sweep. The window after the post is the whole game.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Elevate</b>Lift the base away.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Bottom adds the elevation win: underhook plus shield-lever lifts the top fully off the mat, or converts to the sweep, the recovery, or the back. The complete bottom game, grappling only.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">5</span><b>Full expression</b>Continuous, strikes on.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Light strikes on. The shield manages punches and pressure together, and a lazy shield gets hit through, not just passed. The MMA half guard, complete.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Recall Check</p>

<div class="emma-recall">
  <p class="emma-recall__lead">Test yourself before moving on. Answer out loud, then reveal what good looks like.</p>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does pressure through the shield tell you?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Light = they're based back, attack the posture. Crushing = weight committed past their knees, elevate.</b> The shield is a pressure gauge, not just a barrier.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> Why does a blocking-only shield eventually fail?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Because the top gets unlimited attempts against a static frame. <b>The shield earns its keep as a lever</b>, every clear attempt is weight you can redirect.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> What does full elevation prove, and why is it a win?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a">Feet and hands clear of the mat means <b>the base is completely beaten</b>. It's the sweep's proof without the slam risk, observable and safe at every size difference.</div>
  </details>
  <details class="emma-recall__item">
    <summary><span class="emma-recall__q">Q</span> How does the top beat the shield without wrestling it?</summary>
    <div class="emma-recall__a"><b>Change the angle until the shield points at nothing.</b> Control the bottom knee, drop the hip below the shield line, keep weight behind the knees while doing it.</div>
  </details>
</div>

??? note "Task focus &amp; coaching cues"

    <p class="emma-sub">Each role's job</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🤸</div><div class="emma-card__t">Bottom (Knee Shield)</div><p>Stay on the side, angle the shield, read the pressure, cause and punish posts, elevate or convert when the weight commits.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🥋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Top (Passer)</div><p>Change angles on the shield, control the bottom knee, keep weight behind the knees, clear to chest-to-chest and hold it.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🛡️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Gauge or wall?</div><p>Ask the bottom: "What was the pressure telling you before the clear?" Builds the read that turns the shield from a brace into a sensor.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">⬆️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Where did the weight go?</div><p>Ask both after an elevation: "When did the top's weight pass the knees?" Marks the commit moment both sides need to feel.</p></div>

??? abstract "Constraints-Led analysis"

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Maintenance-only opening level</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Builds shield angles before offense exists</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Cause-then-punish post ladder</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Same off-balancing grammar as Seated Guard Retention, new position</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Elevation as a win</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Rewards beating the base without slam risk</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Clear proven by a 3s hold</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">The top must control, not just brush past</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Live, resisting passer</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Keeps the pressure-gauge read intact</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Task Simplification</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019): the maintain → cause → punish → elevate ladder grows the bottom's role from survival to offense one perceptual layer at a time, against a top whose passing pressure is the very signal being learned.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the bottom reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Pressure through the shield → based-back passive, or committed past the knees: the elevation window.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own torso angle and shield angle → on the side and levered, or flattening toward the back.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>The top's hands and knee width → posts landing, angles changing on the shield.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the bottom <b>converts pressure into offense faster than the top changes angles</b>. Track sweeps, recoveries, back-takes, and elevations vs. shield-clears conceded, and whether the bottom's attacks launch at the commit moment or after it has passed. When the elevation arrives on the commit, the read has formed.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> the half-guard bottom every MMA fighter actually ends up in after a half-stuffed takedown, where staying flat means eating punches and the shield is the only structure that buys offense back.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: shield ladder</span><span>no strikes L1-4</span><span>round cap</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Deepens the recovery side of <a href="../leg-reclaim/">Leg Reclaim</a>; mirrors <a href="../half-guard-pass/">Half-Guard Pass</a>.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Keeps the side position and shield angle under pressure</li>
      <li>Causes posts through the shield, not despite it</li>
      <li>Launches the elevation at the commit moment</li>
      <li>Converts elevations into sweeps, guard, or the back</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Goes flat and braces the shield straight on</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Blocks every clear but never attacks</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Elevates without an underhook, gives the pass</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Top wrestles the shield head-on, feeds the lever</span>
    </div>
