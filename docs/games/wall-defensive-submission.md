Turn a defensive wall position into a threat, submission as weapon and escape tool.

  <div class="emma-arc__node"><span class="emma-arc__k">Start</span><b>Defender pinned against the wall; controller has established wall control.</b></div>

  <div class="emma-arc__sep">→</div>
  <div class="emma-arc__node emma-arc__node--finish"><span class="emma-arc__k">Finish</span><b>Submission or threat-to-escape → defender wins, switch · Control held &amp; subs defended → controller wins, reset.</b></div>
</div>

<div class="emma-band">
  <p class="emma-statement">Pinned isn't beaten, <br><span class="accent">make their control dangerous.</span></p>
  <p class="emma-statement__sub">Guillotine, Kimura, front headlock from the bottom of the pin. <b>The threat creates the escape even when it doesn't finish.</b></p>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">Key Positions</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧱</span> Wall pin<span class="emma-chip__detail">At least two points of pinning control, the hips and upper body held immobile and flat to the wall. If the shoulders can still rotate, it's pressure, not a pin.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">What to Read</p>

<p class="emma-hl"><b>Attune to</b> the <i>felt resistance to rotation through your grips and the controller's posture</i>, where the controller's head and arms sit against your frames, and the moment their control loosens. That changing load specifies <i>which</i> submission is available (head low → guillotine, arm exposed → Kimura) and <i>when</i> the threat opens an escape, not a memorized sequence.</p>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Starting Position</p>

<div class="emma-setup">
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Players</span>Two, one pinned defender, one controller.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Position</span>Defender pinned against the wall; controller has wall control.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Boundary</span>A wall or cage within a marked perimeter, on mats.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Roles</span>Defender threatens submissions; controller holds control while staying safe.</div>
  <div class="emma-setup__item"><span class="emma-setup__k">Start &amp; reset</span>Begin from the pin; reset when a submission lands, is defended, or the controller disengages.</div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Matchup</p>

<div class="emma-matchup">
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--def emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🛡️</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Defender (pinned)</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to threaten or complete a submission from defensive position, not just escape.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Identify what's available from position, guillotine, front headlock/DNS, Kimura, standing arm triangle. Use the threat to create escape even without finishing, and finish if the controller gives the opening.</span>
  </div>
  <div class="emma-matchup__vs">VS</div>
  <div class="emma-fighter emma-fighter--atk emma-has-pop" tabindex="0">
    <div class="emma-fighter__icon">🧱</div>
    <div class="emma-fighter__role">Controller</div>
    <p class="emma-fighter__obj">Trying to maintain wall control while avoiding submission danger.</p>
    <span class="emma-pop">Keep the intent to control, not just defend. Protect neck and arms, recognize submission setups, keep head position safe, and choose to disengage or defend in place.</span>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Rules</p>

<div class="emma-chips">
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🎯</span> Threats required<span class="emma-chip__detail">The defender must threaten submissions, not just escape. This constraint develops the submission as a defensive weapon, counter-offense from defense.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🧱</span> Controller keeps control intent<span class="emma-chip__detail">The controller maintains the intent to control, not just survive the submissions, this keeps the control situation realistic.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">✅</span> Position-available submissions only<span class="emma-chip__detail"><strong>Prescriptive.</strong> Submissions must be genuinely available from the position, no fantasy techniques. This keeps technique selection reality-based.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">🤝</span> Controlled submissions<span class="emma-chip__detail">Apply submissions with control and tap early, control before squeeze. This keeps the game safe during learning.</span></span>
  <span class="emma-chip emma-chip--info" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-chip__i">⬛</span> Stay inside the perimeter<span class="emma-chip__detail">Play happens against a wall or cage within a marked perimeter, any shape. The wall position remains the environment throughout.</span></span>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">How to Win</p>

<div class="emma-outcomes">
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--win emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Defender</span> Complete a submission or use the threat to escape → defender wins, switch.<span class="emma-pop">A finish or a threat-created escape both count, the submission threat opens space even when it doesn't finish.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-outcome emma-outcome--lose emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-outcome__tag">Controller</span> Hold control &amp; defend subs for the duration → controller wins, reset.<span class="emma-pop">Hold position and stay out of danger for the full set duration, control intact, neck and arms safe; same roles.</span></div>
</div>

<p class="emma-glabel">The Levels</p>

<div class="emma-stepper">
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">1</span><b>Guillotine only</b>One threat to read.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Defender can only threaten the guillotine. Focus: recognizing head-position vulnerability.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">2</span><b>Add arm attacks</b>Guillotine + Kimura.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Multiple submission threats available. Focus: reading which submission the position offers.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">3</span><b>Submission to escape</b>Threat as escape tool.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Defender uses threats to create escape; score for escape OR submission. Focus: submission as an escape tool.</span></div>
  <div class="emma-step emma-has-pop" tabindex="0"><span class="emma-step__n">4</span><b>Full MMA</b>Controller strikes.<span class="emma-pop emma-pop--l">Controller can strike during wall control; defender must time submissions around the strikes. Focus: defensive submissions under MMA pressure.</span></div>
</div>

    <div class="emma-cards">

      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧱</div><div class="emma-card__t">Controller</div><p>Maintain position while protecting neck and arms, recognize setups, keep head position safe, choose to disengage or defend in place.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Coaching cues</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">What was available?</div><p>Ask defenders: "What submission was available there?" This develops recognition of head and arm position.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🚪</div><div class="emma-card__t">Threat to escape</div><p>Ask: "Did you use the threat to escape?" Submission threats create escape opportunities even when they don't finish.</p></div>

    <p class="emma-sub">Constraints → Affordances</p>

    <div class="emma-flow">
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Must threaten subs (not just escape)</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Develops submission as a defensive weapon</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Controller keeps control intent</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Creates a realistic control situation</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Position-available subs only</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Reality-based technique selection</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Progressive submission options</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Scaffolds from one sub to many to escape-use</span></div>
      <div class="emma-flow__row"><span class="emma-flow__from">Wall position maintained</span><span class="emma-flow__arr">→</span><span class="emma-flow__to">Defensive submissions specific to the environment</span></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-hl">Implements <b>Constrain to Afford</b> (Renshaw et al., 2019), develops defensive-submission perception: recognizing when submissions exist from disadvantaged positions, turning defense into counter-offense.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">What the defender reads</p>

    <div class="emma-cards">
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">👁️</div><div class="emma-card__t">Visual</div><p>Controller's head position → guillotine / DNS; controller's arm position → Kimura.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">✋</div><div class="emma-card__t">Haptic</div><p>Controller's posture → what submission is available; control loosening → escape timing from the threat.</p></div>
      <div class="emma-card"><div class="emma-card__i">🧭</div><div class="emma-card__t">Proprioceptive</div><p>Own grip and hook position → submission setup.</p></div>
    </div>

    <p class="emma-sub">What we measure (order parameter)</p>

    <p class="emma-hl">Whether the controller keeps the defender's <b>shoulders and hips pinned flat vs. free to rotate</b> while staying clear of submission danger, track control time held against submission threats landed. The press→pin-or-escape phase transition is the order parameter; the skill has formed when threats reliably loosen control and open escapes.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Representativeness</p>

    <p class="emma-hl"><b>Models:</b> using submission threats from a defensive wall position, "their head is down, guillotine is available", to make control dangerous, identical to competition.</p>

    <div class="emma-meta"><span>Simplified: progressive subs</span><span>clear threat requirement</span><span>controlled submissions</span></div>

    <p style="font-size:.82rem;color:var(--fog-dim);margin:.7rem 0 0;">Builds on <a href="../wall-escape/">Wall Escape</a>; an end state, completing a submission resolves the game.</p>

    <p class="emma-sub">Readiness to progress</p>

    <ul class="emma-checklist">
      <li>Recognizes submission opportunities from position</li>
      <li>Threatens effectively, controller feels danger</li>
      <li>Finishes when the controller gives an opening</li>
      <li>Uses threats to create escape</li>
      <li>Can describe: "Their head was down so I…"</li>
    </ul>

    <p class="emma-sub">Warning signs</p>

    <div class="emma-warns">
      <span class="emma-warn">Only tries escape, no submissions</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Controller doesn't feel danger</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Cranks without control</span>
      <span class="emma-warn">Only one submission works</span>
    </div>
